**CLASSIFIED: EMPYREAN CLEARANCE ONLY**  
**ASCENDANT WATCH REF. 01-0001.11**  
**Origin:** Ascendant Watch: Celestial Deployments Directorate  
**Subject:** Celestial Intervention Protocols, Force Deployment on the Material Plane, and Operational Constraints

---

**I. AUTHORIZATION AND SCOPE**  
This dossier documents the current disposition and operational guidelines for **celestial forces deployed to or operating within the material plane** in response to the Velvet Iron Army invasion. All deployments are authorized under the **Radiant Mandate**, a divine directive issued by the Concordance of Thrones permitting limited celestial intervention in mortal affairs under conditions of existential abyssal threat.

The Radiant Mandate imposes strict constraints on the nature and scale of celestial involvement. These constraints are not arbitrary; they arise from the fundamental metaphysical reality that large-scale celestial activity on the material plane generates **divine energy signatures** detectable by both abyssal and neutral planar entities. Excessive celestial presence risks:

1. Provoking a proportional abyssal escalation, including the potential personal manifestation of Graz'zt on the material plane.
2. Attracting the attention of Thraxxia's intelligence apparatus ([[Dossier 03-0380.11]]), which actively monitors for celestial signatures through a network of arcane sensors ([[Dossier 01-0001.8211]]: "Thraxxia's Celestial Detection Grid: Coverage Analysis and Known Blind Spots").
3. Disrupting the planar balance in ways that could destabilize the material plane's fundamental structure, a concern raised by the **Mechanus Observatorate** in formal diplomatic communications ([[Dossier 01-0001.1468]]: "Mechanus Advisory, Planar Stability Thresholds and Celestial Activity Limits").

**II. DEPLOYED CELESTIAL ASSETS**

### A. Lantern Corps: Scout and Medical Detachments
**Designation:** "Lanterns"  
**Personnel Type:** Adolescent Celestials (equivalent to mortal ages 12-16 in celestial development)  
**Estimated Deployment:** 200-250 across the material plane  
**CR Rating:** Varies (CR 1-3 individually)

Lanterns are the most numerous celestial presence on the material plane. They serve in two primary capacities:

- **Scout Operations:** Lanterns are deployed individually or in pairs to monitor frontline conditions, track infernal troop movements, and maintain intelligence relays between mortal agents and the Ascendant Watch command structure. Their small size, flight capability, and natural invisibility (up to 3 rounds per minute) make them effective reconnaissance assets ([[Dossier 01-0001.3224]]: "Lantern Corps: Deployment Map and Zone Assignments").
- **Medical Support:** Lanterns possess an innate healing luminance capable of stabilizing mortally wounded individuals. They are deployed near field hospitals, refugee columns, and contested villages to reduce civilian casualties. Their presence is typically disguised as "will-o'-wisp phenomena" or ambient light anomalies.

Lanterns are explicitly **non-combatant**. They are instructed to flee rather than fight and are equipped with emergency recall sigils that will teleport them to the nearest Forward Operating Base if their physical integrity falls below 40%. Loss of a Lantern is considered a significant intelligence failure; to date, **seven Lanterns have been confirmed captured or destroyed** ([[Dossier 01-0001.6141]]: "Lantern Casualty Report: Circumstances and Lessons Learned").

### B. Forward Operating Bases (FOBs): Regional Command Nodes
**Designation:** Celestial FOBs (codenames vary by location)  
**Current Active FOBs:** 4  
**Personnel per FOB:** 3-8 celestials (mix of Devas, Archons, and support staff)

FOBs serve as concealed staging areas for celestial operations. Each is established in a location of pre-existing sanctified ground (temples, holy groves, ancient shrines) to mask its divine energy signature. Known active FOBs include:

1. **FOB Dawnspire**, Located in the Pale Marches (Preanese Theocracy border region). Commanded by **Deva Sentinel Koravael**. Primary mission: monitoring Preanese bureaucratic subversion ([[Dossier 01-0001.6424]]: "FOB Dawnspire, Operational Status and Intelligence Product").
2. **FOB Ashwatch**, Located near the Stuik-Suirx border. Commanded by **Celestial Commander Galadren**. Primary mission: frontline intelligence support and strike operation staging (most recently, Operation Valkyrhino, [[Dossier 01-0007.11]]).
3. **FOB Underhearth**: Location **[REDACTED]**. Primary mission: monitoring the jade amulet harvesting operations in the ruins of Ulbgar ([[Dossier 19-3999.11]]). The most dangerous FOB; operating within partially contested subterranean territory.
4. **FOB Windcrest**: Located in the Tsastashan Range ([[Dossier 19-3781.1267]]). Primary mission: protection of the Aarakocra enclave and monitoring of the rogue Gelugon. The only FOB with direct proximity to a confirmed **[REDACTED]** site.

### C. Celestial Commander Galadren: Senior Officer, Material Plane
**Designation:** Commander, Ascendant Watch Material Operations  
**Classification:** Solar (diminished manifestation); Estimated CR 18 (operating at reduced capacity to minimize detection)  
**Assignment:** FOB Ashwatch

Galadren is the senior celestial officer on the material plane and holds operational authority over all FOBs and Lantern deployments. He manifests in a deliberately diminished form, a tall, weathered human male with faded golden eyes, to reduce his divine signature. Even in this reduced state, he is the most powerful celestial asset in-theater.

The Command Council's operational philosophy prioritizes **mortal agency**, insisting that the war must be won primarily by mortal hands, with celestial forces providing only intelligence, logistics, and targeted support rather than direct combat. This philosophy has created significant tension with Commander Galadren and more aggressive frontline officers who advocate for overt divine intervention ([[Dossier 01-0001.2677]]: "Strategic Debate: The Council's Restraint Doctrine vs. Galadren's Interventionist Position").

**III. OPERATIONAL CONSTRAINTS AND THE DETECTION PROBLEM**  
The single greatest limitation on celestial operations is the **detection problem**. Every use of divine energy on the material plane, from a Lantern's healing luminance to an Archon's combat manifestation, produces a signature that propagates through the Astral Plane and can be detected by sufficiently attuned observers.

Thraxxia's intelligence network includes **at least twelve arcane sensor arrays** positioned at strategic locations across the continent ([[Dossier 01-0001.8211]]). These arrays can detect and triangulate celestial signatures above a certain threshold. Current estimates place the detection floor at approximately **CR 10 equivalent energy expenditure**; meaning any celestial action more powerful than a Deva's combat abilities risks detection.

Countermeasures employed by the Ascendant Watch include:

- **Signature dampening rituals** performed at all FOBs, reducing ambient divine radiation by approximately 60%.
- **Operational timing**, high-energy celestial actions are coordinated to coincide with natural celestial events (solstices, eclipses, meteor showers) that produce background noise masking artificial signatures ([[Dossier 01-0001.7718]]: "Celestial Operations Calendar, Signature-Masking Windows, Current Year").
- **Mortal proxies**: the majority of direct-action operations (such as Operation Valkyrhino) are executed by mortal operatives with celestial support limited to intelligence, planning, and post-operation assessment.
- **Sacrificial decoys**, in at least two confirmed instances, Lanterns have been deliberately deployed to generate false celestial signatures in one location while critical operations were conducted elsewhere ([[Dossier 01-0001.6843]]: "Decoy Operations, After-Action Reports and Ethical Review").

---

**IV. THE PORTAL CLOSURE DILEMMA**  
The Ascendant Watch possesses the theoretical capability to close abyssal portals through the application of concentrated divine energy. However, this capability is effectively **unusable at scale** due to the detection problem:

- Closing a **Class-III stabilized portal** (such as the Stios node) requires a sustained divine energy expenditure equivalent to CR 15 for approximately 30 seconds: well above the detection threshold. The celestial-forged destabilization charges used in Operation Valkyrhino were developed specifically to close portals through an explosive physical mechanism rather than sustained divine channeling.
- Closing a **Class-V primary invasion portal** (such as the breaches beneath Ulbgar) would require energy expenditure equivalent to CR 20+ for several minutes: a beacon visible from across the planes that would almost certainly provoke immediate and massive abyssal counter-escalation.

Research into lower-signature portal closure methods is ongoing ([[Dossier 01-0001.3214]]: "Project Quiet Gate: Low-Signature Portal Interdiction Research"). Current promising avenues include adaptation of Mechanus-derived nullification fields and mortal ritual magic amplified by celestial anchors.

---

**V. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **Current Posture:** The Ascendant Watch maintains a minimal-footprint presence focused on intelligence collection, mortal agent support, and targeted interdiction. This posture is sustainable but insufficient to turn the tide of the war independently.
2. **Escalation Risk:** Any significant increase in celestial activity risks detection by Thraxxia's sensor grid, potentially triggering a catastrophic escalation cascade. The balance between effective intervention and operational security remains the defining strategic tension.
3. **Mortal Dependency:** The war's outcome depends primarily on mortal resistance. The Ascendant Watch's role is to ensure mortal forces have the intelligence, resources, and targeted support necessary to fight effectively.
4. **Recommendation:** **[REDACTED]**, Maintain current operational posture. Prioritize Project Quiet Gate research for scalable portal interdiction. Expand Lantern deployments in the Preanese Theocracy to compensate for compromised mortal intelligence networks. Prepare contingency plans for overt celestial intervention in the event of a Phase Two escalation against Preas, the loss of the Theocracy would constitute a strategic tipping point justifying the detection risk. **[REDACTED]**
