**REF. 01-0001.2677**
**Origin:** Ascendant Watch: Command Council Records
**Subject:** The Council's Restraint Doctrine Debate: Hawks vs. Doves

---

**I. CONTEXT**\
**Commander Galadren**, leading the Ascendant Watch's material plane operations
since the campaign's inception, has increasingly advocated for direct and
forceful action against infernal forces. His operational philosophy; the
**Interventionist Doctrine**; has been constantly blocked and challenged by the
Ascendant Watch Command Council, which mandates a strict **Restraint Doctrine**.
The Council holds that celestial forces must operate below the detection
threshold at all times, accepting tactical losses and moral compromises to avoid
triggering infernal escalation.

This dossier summarizes the debate, the positions of both sides, and the
implications for operational planning.

---

**II. THE RESTRAINT DOCTRINE (THE COUNCIL'S POSITION)**

The Council's core argument:

> _"Every time we act openly, the enemy detects us. Every detection justifies
> escalation: more Balors committed, more portals opened, potentially Graz'zt
> himself entering the field. We win by being invisible. We lose by being seen.
> The mortal allies need intelligence, not intervention. Give them the
> information to win their own war."_

**Key tenets:**

- Lantern operatives operate in passive surveillance mode by default
- Combat intervention only when no other option exists (and even then, minimize
  signature)
- Mortal allies fight the war; the Ascendant Watch supports from the shadows
- Any operation that risks detection must be weighed against the escalation
  consequence
- The detection threshold is the single most important strategic constraint

**Supporting evidence:**

- LN-011's protocol breach ([[Dossier 01-0001.6141]]) demonstrates the lethal cost of
  visible intervention
- The CDG network ([[Dossier 01-0001.8211]]) proves that Thraxxia is specifically
  designed to detect and punish celestial activity
- The Mechanus advisory ([[Dossier 01-0001.1468]]) confirms that escalation (more
  portals) accelerates the existential threat

([[Dossier 01-0001.2677.1147]]: "Restraint Doctrine: Full Position Paper and
Supporting Arguments")

---

**III. THE INTERVENTIONIST POSITION (GALADREN AND THE "HAWKS")**

The opposing position is advanced by **Commander Galadren** and supported
heavily by field officers like Subcommanders Erithiel, Tariel, and Ashiel. Their
argument:

> _"Restraint is costing us the war. We sit in the shadows while mortals die in
> thousands. We have the power to destroy Balors, shatter portals, and turn the
> tide: but we refuse to use it because we fear the consequence. Meanwhile, the
> membrane degrades, the souls are harvested, and the clock runs. At some point,
> restraint becomes complicity."_

**Key tenets:**

- The detection threshold is a **self-imposed constraint**, not a physical law.
  Breaking it would trigger infernal escalation; but the infernal escalation is
  already happening without celestial intervention.
- The Mechanus advisory timeline means the war must be won within 20 months. The
  Restraint Doctrine's "long game" strategy is incompatible with this deadline.
- Selective, decisive celestial strikes: destroying the Greyloch portal,
  eliminating Ruin-Tongue, raiding the Dûrn Kordrek forges; would achieve more
  in weeks than the surveillance approach has achieved in 18 months.
- The moral cost of non-intervention (civilian deaths, soul harvesting, jade
  amulet addiction) is no longer acceptable.

**Supporting evidence:**

- LN-011's intervention at the refugee caravan: while fatal to the operative -
  saved 200 civilian lives. Was the trade worth it? Galadren says yes.
- The Balor kills at Korras Bluffs and Blackwall were achieved with celestial
  assistance; proving that intervention works when applied.
- Thraxxia's CDG network is designed to detect celestial activity, but detection
  does not mean defeat; it means the enemy knows we're coming. And they should
  fear that.

**IV. THE COUNCIL VOTE**\
The most recent Command Council meeting produced a **split decision:**

| Position                     | Vote                                                               |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Restraint (Council Majority) | High Councilor Maelis, Oraniel, Lyriel, Vaelith, Opheniel, Zephyra |
| Interventionist (Galadren)   | Galadren                                                           |

**Result: 6-1 in favor of continued Restraint.** Galadren stands entirely alone
on the Council, having formally recorded his solitary dissent for the celestial
archive.
