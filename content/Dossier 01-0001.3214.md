**Origin:** Ascendant Watch: Research and Development Division  
**Subject:** Project Quiet Gate: Low-Signature Portal Interdiction R&D

---

**I. PROJECT OVERVIEW**  
**Project Name:** Quiet Gate  
**Classification:** Research and Development; Weapons/Tools  
**Lead Researcher:** Subcommander Lyriel (Suirx Territories, R&D detachment)  
**Location:** FOB Ashwatch, Laboratory 3  
**Status:** Phase 2 (Prototype Testing)  

**Objective:** Develop a portable device that can **destroy or disable an infernal portal without generating a celestial divine signature**; enabling portal interdiction operations that do not trigger CDG detection or escalation.

---

**II. PROBLEM STATEMENT**  
Current portal destruction methods require either:
1. **Mortal spellcasters** performing a dispelling cascade ([[Dossier 01-0007.3983]]): effective for Class-III but requires 15 minutes of uninterrupted casting and a wizard of significant capability. Mortal assets of this caliber are scarce.
2. **Celestial divine strikes**: effective and rapid but generate massive divine signatures that trigger CDG sensors and invite escalation.

Project Quiet Gate aims to create a **third option**: a device that achieves portal destruction through non-divine, non-mortal-dependent means; specifically, through weaponized **arcane destabilization** delivered by a portable charge.

---

**III. RESEARCH PHASES**

### Phase 1: Theory (COMPLETE)
Analysis of portal anchor physics, including the Stios portal's recovered pylon data ([[Dossier 01-0007.3983]]), identified that all portals share a common vulnerability: **resonance destabilization**. The portal anchor's stabilization sigils maintain a specific arcane frequency; if a counter-frequency is introduced, the sigils enter **destructive resonance**, causing the anchor structure to tear itself apart.

The challenge: generating the correct counter-frequency for a specific portal requires knowing the portal's stabilization frequency, which varies by construction.

**Solution developed:** A tunable resonance crystal, a multi-faceted gem enchanted to scan a portal's frequency and automatically generate the counter-frequency within 60 seconds of exposure ([[Dossier 01-0001.3214.1147]]: "Phase 1, Resonance Destabilization Theory and Counter-Frequency Generation").

### Phase 2: Prototype (IN PROGRESS)
The prototype device, designated **"Hushstone"**, is a crystal approximately 10cm in diameter, set in an iron cradle with deployment spikes. To use it:

1. **Deploy:** Place the Hushstone within 5 meters of a portal anchor pylon
2. **Scan:** The crystal automatically scans and identifies the portal's frequency (60 seconds)
3. **Activate:** A timer trigger (physical twist of the cradle) initiates the counter-frequency emission, with set times being between one and thirty minutes
4. **Destruction:** The counter-frequency causes destructive resonance in the anchor sigils. Portal collapse follows within 30-120 seconds depending on portal class.

**Testing Results:**
- Test 1 (simulated Class-I portal): **Successful**: portal collapsed in 35 seconds, no divine signature generated
- Test 2 (simulated Class-II portal): **Successful**: portal collapsed in 90 seconds, no divine signature
- Test 3 (simulated Class-III portal): **Partial success**: portal destabilized but did not fully collapse. The counter-frequency was insufficient to overcome the Class-III self-regulating sigil system. Crystal shattered after 110 seconds.

**Assessment:** The Hushstone is effective against Class-I and Class-II portals. Class-III portals require a more powerful crystal or a revised approach to the self-regulating sigil problem ([[Dossier 01-0001.3214.2301]]: "Phase 2: Prototype Testing Results and Class-III Limitation").

### Phase 3: Enhancement (PLANNED)
Two approaches are under consideration for overcoming the Class-III limitation:

**Approach A, Brute Force:** Develop a larger, more powerful Hushstone capable of overwhelming the self-regulating sigils through sheer energy output. Risk: the increased energy output may generate a detectable arcane signature, defeating the "quiet" purpose.

**Approach B, Two-Stage Attack:** Deploy the Hushstone to destabilize the portal first, then follow with a targeted mortal *Dispel Magic* to finish the collapse. This combines the Hushstone's non-signature advantage with the mortal wizard's proven capability, while reducing the wizard's required casting time from 15 minutes to approximately 2 minutes ([[Dossier 01-0001.3214.3478]]: "Phase 3, Enhancement Approaches and Trade-Off Analysis").

---

**IV. POTENTIAL CLASS-IV APPLICATION**  
The Dûrn Kordrek Class-IV portal and the potential future Greyloch Class-IV portal represent the highest-priority targets. Class-IV portals have **dramatically more powerful self-regulation** than Class-III; a Hushstone capable of affecting a Class-IV would need to be an order of magnitude more powerful than current prototypes.

A theoretical "Class-IV Hushstone" would require:
- A crystal of at least 30cm diameter (rare and expensive to produce)
- Dedicated ley line power source (portable ley line tap device: not yet developed)
- Extended scanning time (estimated 5-10 minutes instead of 60 seconds)
- Deployment team of at least 3 to carry and position the device

**Feasibility:** ASSESSED AS POSSIBLE but requiring significant additional R&D investment ([[Dossier 01-0001.3214.4612]]: "Class-IV Hushstone: Theoretical Specifications and Feasibility Assessment").

**VI. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **Game-Changer Potential:** A working Hushstone eliminates the trade-off between portal destruction and detection risk. It enables portal interdiction that is both effective and invisible: the ideal tool for operations under the Restraint Doctrine.
2. **Immediate Value:** Even the current Class-II capability has value: the remaining eastern Suirx portals ([[Dossier 01-0007.4737]]), if they are Class-III, could be targeted with the Two-Stage approach.
3. **Recommendation:** **[REDACTED]**: Approve maximum R&D funding for Project Quiet Gate. Prioritize Approach B (Two-Stage) for Class-III development. Begin theoretical work on Class-IV immediately. Produce and deploy 3 Class-II Hushstones for the Suirx portal hunt. **[REDACTED]**
