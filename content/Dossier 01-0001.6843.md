**CLASSIFIED: EMPYREAN CLEARANCE ONLY**  
**REF. 01-0001.6843**  
**Origin:** Ascendant Watch: Operations Division  
**Subject:** Decoy Operations: After-Action Reports

---

**I. DOCTRINE**  
Decoy operations are deliberate, sacrificial deployments of celestial assets designed to generate **false divine signatures** that attract enemy attention away from actual operations. They are the Ascendant Watch's most morally complex tactical tool: each decoy deployment places a Lantern operative at elevated risk to protect the operational security of another mission.

---

**II. DECOY OPERATIONS CONDUCTED**

### Operation BRIGHTLURE (8 months ago)
**Objective:** Draw CDG attention away from the Arcanaloth "Lens" kill operation ([[Dossier 03-0378.4338]])  
**Decoy Asset:** LN-014 (Stuik Western Front)  
**Method:** LN-014 generated a sustained, conspicuous divine signature by performing multiple *Sacred Flame* castings at a position 60km from the Lens engagement zone. The signature was deliberately exaggerated; brighter and longer-lasting than any operational Lantern activity.

**Enemy Response:** A Vrock response flight (6 units) was dispatched to LN-014's position within 40 minutes. LN-014 evaded, having pre-planned an escape route with *Dimension Door*, but was pursued for 3 hours and narrowly avoided detection.

**Result:** The Lens kill operation proceeded undetected. The enemy's response forces were committed to the wrong location during the critical engagement window.

**Cost:** LN-014 was exposed, experienced residual Vrock-pursuit psychological stress, and required 2 weeks of rest rotation. No physical injury.

([[Dossier 01-0001.6843.1147]]: "BRIGHTLURE: Full After-Action Report")

### Operation STARCHURN (5 months ago)
**Objective:** Cover the insertion of mortal operatives into the Greyloch Forest approach zone for CDG sensor mapping  
**Decoy Asset:** LN-017 (Silesbia Perimeter)  
**Method:** LN-017 activated a sustained divine channel at the northwestern Greyloch perimeter; opposite the actual insertion point on the southeastern approach. The channel mimicked the signature of a Lantern conducting aggressive forest surveillance.

**Enemy Response:** The Greyloch Arcanaloth dispatched a 4-Mezzoloth patrol to the northwestern perimeter. The mortal insertion team entered the southeastern approach undetected and successfully mapped CDG sensors CDG-03 and CDG-06.

**Result:** Successful CDG mapping without Lantern exposure at the actual operation site. CDG-03's identification subsequently contributed to the analysis of LN-019's death ([[Dossier 01-0001.6141]]).

**Cost:** LN-017 was detected but not engaged; the Mezzoloth patrol was dispatched to the decoy location rather than an intercept force against LN-017 directly. No injury.

([[Dossier 01-0001.6843.2301]]: "STARCHURN: Full After-Action Report")

### Operation ASHWHISPER (3 months ago)
**Objective:** Draw enemy patrol activity away from FOB Dawnspire during a scheduled ley line maintenance operation that required temporarily lowering the FOB's anti-divination wards  
**Decoy Asset:** LN-025 (Preas Theater)  
**Method:** LN-025 performed a series of conspicuous scrying operations from 3 different positions around the eastern Preanese border, creating the appearance of a major celestial intelligence sweep.

**Enemy Response:** CDG sensors CDG-06 and CDG-07 triggered. A response was noted, increased Cambion security patrols in the eastern parishes, but no Vrock intercept was launched. The enemy appeared to assess the activity as reconnaissance-level rather than strike-level.

**Result:** FOB Dawnspire's ley line maintenance was completed undetected. However, the muted enemy response suggests either **doctrinal adaptation** (the enemy may be learning to distinguish between decoy signatures and genuine threats) or **resource constraints** (insufficient Vrock availability for intercept).

**Cost:** LN-025 was exposed in 3 separate positions, requiring rotation of all Preas Theater observation posts. No injury.

([[Dossier 01-0001.6843.3478]]: "ASHWHISPER: Full After-Action Report and Adaptation Concerns")

**Trend:** Decoy operations are effective but show **diminishing returns**. The enemy is learning; each successive decoy is less effective at diverting maximum enemy attention. Future decoy operations will need to be more sophisticated to maintain effectiveness.

**IV. ETHICAL CONSIDERATIONS**  
Decoy operations deliberately place Lantern operatives at elevated risk for the benefit of other missions. This creates a moral tension within the Restraint Doctrine:

- Subcommander Erithiel (interventionist) has argued that decoy operations represent a "perverse inversion": the Watch will risk Lantern lives to protect operational security but will not risk them to save mortal civilians.
- Commander Galadren has responded that decoy operations serve the broader mission, protecting intelligence operations that ultimately benefit all mortal populations, while individual civilian interventions serve immediate but localized needs.

The debate remains unresolved ([[Dossier 01-0001.2677]]).

([[Dossier 01-0001.6843.5738]]: "Ethical Review: Decoy Operations and the Restraint Paradox")

---

**V. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **Proven Value:** Decoy operations have been instrumental in protecting high-value operations. Their continued use is essential.
2. **Diminishing Returns:** The enemy is adapting. Future decoys must incorporate new techniques: varied signature patterns, multi-point simultaneous deployments, or combined natural/artificial masking ([[Dossier 01-0001.7718]]).
3. **Recommendation:** **[REDACTED]**: Develop a "second generation" decoy doctrine incorporating randomized signature profiles and coordination with natural masking windows. Assign dedicated decoy planning to a specialist within the Operations Directorate. **[REDACTED]**
