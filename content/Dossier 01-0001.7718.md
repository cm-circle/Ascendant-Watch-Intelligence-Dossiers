**CLASSIFIED: EMPYREAN CLEARANCE ONLY**  
**REF. 01-0001.7718**  
**Origin:** Ascendant Watch: Operations Planning  
**Subject:** Celestial Operations Calendar: Signature-Masking Windows

---

**I. PURPOSE**  
This calendar identifies **natural and artificial conditions** under which celestial divine signatures are partially or fully masked; periods when Lantern operatives can act with reduced risk of CDG detection or enemy observation. These windows are critical for planning active operations under the Calculated Risk Protocol ([[Dossier 01-0001.2677]]).

---

**II. NATURAL MASKING WINDOWS**

### A. Celestial Alignments
Three times per year, specific celestial body alignments create **planar harmonic interference**; a natural phenomenon where the divine frequency spectrum is disrupted by gravitational-arcane interactions. During these periods (lasting 48-72 hours each), CDG sensor sensitivity is reduced by approximately 60%.

| Alignment      | Date            | Duration | CDG Reduction | Notes                                 |
| -------------- | --------------- | -------- | ------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Solstice Prime | Summer solstice | 72 hours | 65%           | Strongest masking; best for major ops |
| Equinox Mirror | Autumn equinox  | 48 hours | 55%           | Moderate masking                      |
| Deep Winter    | Winter solstice | 60 hours | 60%           | Strong masking                        |

([[Dossier 01-0001.7718.1147]]: "Celestial Alignment Schedule: 3-Year Forecast and Masking Calculations")

### B. Arcane Storms
Natural arcane storms, disturbances in ambient magical energy caused by ley line fluctuations, occur irregularly but produce intense interference across all magical detection bands. During arcane storms, CDG sensors are effectively **inoperative** and all scrying operations (both friendly and enemy) are disrupted.

Average frequency: 1-2 per month, duration 6-18 hours each. These cannot be predicted more than 3-5 days in advance.

### C. Moonless Nights
While not affecting magical detection directly, moonless nights reduce visual observation ranges and degrade enemy patrol effectiveness. Combined with other masking factors, moonless nights optimize conditions for physical infiltration operations.

---

**III. ARTIFICIAL MASKING TECHNIQUES**

### A. Decoy Deployments 
Sacrificial Lantern deployments that generate deliberate divine signatures in non-critical locations, drawing enemy attention and response forces away from the actual operation area. This technique is proven but **costly**; it risks Lantern lives and consumes operational resources. ([[Dossier 01-0001.6843]])

### B. Ambient Divine Saturation
Flooding an area with low-level divine energy (e.g., through multiple simultaneous *Bless* castings by mortal clerics) raises the background divine-frequency noise level, making individual Lantern signatures harder to distinguish. This technique requires coordination with mortal allied clerical assets.

### C. Null-Field Amulets
Prototype devices developed at FOB Ashwatch ([[Dossier 01-0001.3214]]) that suppress the wearer's divine signature for approximately 30 minutes. Currently in testing; functional but with side effects including temporary loss of innate celestial abilities.

([[Dossier 01-0001.7718.2301]]: "Artificial Masking: Techniques and Effectiveness Ratings")
