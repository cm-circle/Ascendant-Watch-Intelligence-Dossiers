**CLASSIFIED: FOR CELESTIAL EYES ONLY**  
**REF. 01-0007.1627**  
**Origin:** Ascendant Watch: Portal Traffic Analysis Unit  
**Subject:** Transit Signatures: Estimated Infernal Personnel Throughput (Stios Node)

---

**I. ANALYSIS PURPOSE**  
This assessment estimates the volume and composition of infernal personnel that transited through the Stios Class-I portal during its operational period (approximately 8 months). The analysis is based on arcane residue sampling from the destroyed portal chamber, recovered log fragments, and captured Mezzoloth communication stones.

---

**II. METHODOLOGY**  
Portal transit leaves characteristic arcane residue on the physical anchor structure. Different creature types deposit distinct signatures based on their native planar energy, a Babau's passage leaves a different trace than a Cambion's. By sampling the anchor pylons (recovered after the portal's destruction), the Ascendant Watch's arcane forensics team has catalogued the **total transit history** of the Stios portal with moderate confidence ([[Dossier 01-0007.1627.1108]]: "Portal Residue Sampling, Methodology, Confidence Levels, and Error Margins").

---

**III. INCOMING TRANSIT; INFERNAL TO MATERIAL**

| Entity Type | Estimated Count | Confidence | Purpose Assessment |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cambion | 8-12 | High | Administrative/coordination |
| Mezzoloth | 25-35 | Moderate | Security rotation |
| Arcanaloth | 2-3 | High | Portal maintenance/engineering |
| Succubus/Incubus | 4-7 | Moderate | Infiltration agents; deployed into civilian populace |
| Dretch | 40-60 | Low | Manual labor; portal construction and maintenance |
| Unidentified (small fiend, CR 1-3) | 15-25 | Low | Courier/messenger function |

**Total estimated incoming transits: 94-142 individual infernal entities** over 8 months.

### Key Observations:
- The **Succubus/Incubus transit** is particularly alarming. These entities were deployed outward from Stios into the civilian population, their exit from the portal chamber was one-way. They are now operating under cover identities somewhere in the Suirx territories, unaccounted for and undetected ([[Dossier 01-0007.1627.2247]]: "Deployed Shapechangers, Distribution Estimates and Active Search Parameters").
- The **Arcanaloth rotation** (2-3 transits) suggests periodic maintenance visits rather than permanent assignment. The Arcanaloth that escaped during Operation Valkyrhino may not have been the same individual as the original installer.
- **Dretch transit** numbers are lower than expected for an 8-month operation, suggesting the Dretches were either locally summoned or arrived via a separate non-portal mechanism.

---

**IV. OUTGOING TRANSIT; MATERIAL TO INFERNAL**

| Category | Estimated Count | Confidence |
|---|---|---|
| Mortal trafficking victims | 90-100 | High |
| Jade amulets (full-charge, bulk) | 500-800 units | Moderate |
| Intelligence packages (document/item) | 20-30 | Low |
| Returning infernal personnel | 30-45 | Moderate |

### Key Observations:
- The **90-100 mortal victims** figure aligns with the 96 unrecovered individuals from the missing persons dossier ([[Dossier 01-0007.4892]]), confirming that nearly all missing persons were transited through the portal.
- The **jade amulet volume** (500-800 units incoming) exceeds the estimated distribution volume in the Stios area, suggesting the portal also served as a **collection hub** for amulets distributed across a wider geographic area, possibly receiving amulets from the two remaining portals for consolidated transit ([[Dossier 01-0007.1627.3389]]: "Amulet Consolidation Hypothesis, Hub-and-Spoke Collection Model").
- **Intelligence packages** represent physical documents, enchanted items, or intelligence products flowing from material plane operatives back to infernal handlers. The volume suggests an established intelligence reporting pipeline.

---

**V. PEAK ACTIVITY PERIODS**  
Residue intensity analysis reveals non-uniform usage patterns:

- **Initial 2 months:** Low activity: portal construction and testing, minimal transit
- **Months 3-5:** Gradual increase: operational ramp-up, personnel deployment
- **Months 6-8:** Peak activity: the portal was operating at near-maximum capacity, with an estimated 3-5 transits per day. The accelerating usage pattern suggests either increasing operational pressure or a planned timeline that was not yet complete when Operation Valkyrhino intervened

The disruption of the portal at peak activity likely maximized the operational impact, the infernal network lost its highest-throughput node at the moment of greatest dependency ([[Dossier 01-0007.1627.4534]]: "Activity Timeline, Residue Intensity Overlay and Operational Phase Assessment").

---

**VI. DESTINATION ANALYSIS**  
The Stios portal's destination terminus could not be definitively identified from residue analysis alone. However:

- The fiendish signatures present are predominantly **Abyssal** rather than Infernal (Baatezu), consistent with Graz'zt's domain
- The Arcanaloth transit signatures carry trace elements consistent with **Gehenna** (Yugoloth home plane), suggesting the portal may have had a multi-stop routing capability
- Cross-referencing with Operative Thornshade's reports ([[Dossier 19-3999.8638]]), the most likely primary destination is either Ziragz ([[Dossier 03-0380.3127]]) or the occupied Dûrn Kordrek forge complex

([[Dossier 01-0007.1627.5671]]: "Destination Analysis: Planar Signature Cross-Reference")

---

**VII. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **Deployed Assets:** The 4-7 Succubi/Incubi deployed through Stios into the Suirx civilian population represent an **active and unresolved threat**. Their identification and neutralization is a priority.
2. **Hub Function:** If the Stios portal served as a collection hub, its destruction may have temporarily disrupted amulet logistics for the remaining two portals. This window of disruption should be exploited.
3. **Recommendation:** **[REDACTED]**: Initiate a shapechanger detection sweep of Suirx settlements within a 200km radius of Stios. Cross-reference the deployed Succubus/Incubus estimates with any recent unexplained personnel changes in local governance. The shapechanger detection unit's thermal scanning protocols ([[Dossier 19-3611.3723]]) should be adapted for Suirx deployment. **[REDACTED]**
