**CLASSIFIED: FOR CELESTIAL EYES ONLY**  
**REF. 01-0007.3983**  
**Origin:** Ascendant Watch: Portal Engineering Analysis  
**Subject:** Portal Class-III Technical Assessment: Stios Node

---

**I. PORTAL CLASSIFICATION**  
The Ascendant Watch classifies portals by throughput capacity and stability:

| Class | Throughput | Stability | Duration | Construction Time |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| I | Single entity, one-way | Unstable; collapses after use | Minutes | Hours |
| II | Small group (5-10), bidirectional | Semi-stable; requires active maintenance | Hours | Days |
| **III** | **Medium volume (20-30/hour), bidirectional** | **Stable; self-sustaining** | **Indefinite** | **Months** |
| IV | High volume (100+/hour), bidirectional | Permanently anchored | Indefinite | Years |

The Stios portal was a **Class-III installation**; a bidirectional, self-sustaining planar gate capable of transiting approximately 20-30 entities per hour in each direction. Class-III portals represent the standard operational grade for infernal logistics operations.

---

**II. ANCHOR SYSTEM**  
The Stios portal anchor consisted of **six obsidian pylons** arranged in a regular hexagonal pattern, each pylon approximately 2 meters in height and 30 centimeters in diameter. The pylons were embedded 1.5 meters into the bedrock floor. Component analysis of pylon fragments recovered post-destruction reveals:

- **Material:** Obsidian sourced from a volcanic region: isotopic analysis is consistent with Abyssal plane geology, specifically the basalt flows of Graz'zt's domain
- **Internal Structure:** Each pylon contains a hollow core filled with a silver-infused liquid that serves as a conductive medium for planar energy. The liquid is maintained at a constant temperature of 47°C through an internal heating enchantment.
- **Sigil Network:** The exterior surface of each pylon is engraved with approximately 400 individual arcane sigils covering the conjuration, transmutation, and abjuration schools. These sigils function as a **self-regulating control system**: the portal adjusts its aperture, directional orientation, and energy draw automatically based on feedback from the sigil network.

([[Dossier 01-0007.3983.1147]]: "Pylon Construction: Material Composition and Internal Architecture")

---

**III. POWER SOURCE**  
Class-III portals require significant and continuous energy. The Stios portal drew power from two sources:

1. **Ley Line Tap:** A minor ley line runs beneath the Stios promontory at approximately 8 meters depth. A tap conduit, a copper wire wound around a crystal resonator embedded in the bedrock, drew approximately 40% of the portal's energy requirement from this natural source.
2. **Arcanaloth Maintenance:** The remaining 60% of energy was supplied through periodic maintenance castings by the assigned Arcanaloth engineer, approximately 2 hours of sustained spellwork per week ([[Dossier 01-0007.3983.2301]]: "Power Source Analysis, Three-Component Energy Budget").

---

**IV. SAFETY AND STABILITY ASSESSMENT**  
Pre-destruction analysis identified the following stability characteristics:

- **Failsafe Mechanism:** The sigil network includes a **shutdown cascade**: if any two pylons are destroyed or significantly damaged, the remaining four pylons automatically deactivate the portal to prevent an uncontrolled planar breach. This failsafe is designed to protect the infernal side from backlash, not to protect the material plane.
- **Catastrophic Failure Mode:** If all six pylons are destroyed simultaneously (e.g., by an explosion), the portal undergoes **uncontrolled collapse**: releasing stored planar energy as a shockwave. Estimated blast radius for a Class-III: 30 meters, with fire and necrotic damage. The Stios portal was destroyed through a controlled dispelling cascade specifically to avoid this scenario.
- **Dimensional Bleed:** During extended operation, the portal creates a zone of **dimensional thinning** around the anchor point, a 50-meter radius where the barrier between planes is weakened. This thinning facilitates scrying, summoning, and other cross-planar magic within the zone, and creates the ambient "wrongness" reported by sensitive individuals near portal sites ([[Dossier 01-0007.3983.3478]]: "Dimensional Thinning, Environmental Effects and Detection Signatures").

---

**V. DESTRUCTION METHODOLOGY**  
The dispelling cascade used by Operation Valkyrhino's wizard is documented for replication in future operations:

1. **Phase 1: Sigil Disruption:** Targeted *Dispel Magic* applied to each pylon's abjuration-school sigils, disabling the self-regulation system. Duration: 3 minutes.
2. **Phase 2: Conductive Drain:** *Antimagic Field* applied to the base of each pylon, forcing the silver-infused liquid to lose its enchantment and drain into the bedrock. Duration: 5 minutes.
3. **Phase 3: Anchor Break:** Once the pylons are inert, *Shatter* applied to each pylon individually, destroying them sequentially (not simultaneously) to avoid triggering the catastrophic failure mode. Duration: 7 minutes.

Total procedure time: **15 minutes** of sustained, uninterrupted spellcasting. During this time, the caster is vulnerable and must be protected by the strike team ([[Dossier 01-0007.3983.4612]]: "Dispelling Cascade Protocol: Detailed Procedure and Caster Requirements").

---

**VI. COMPARATIVE ANALYSIS**  
The Stios portal's construction is assessed as **standard infernal Class-III**; not unique or experimental. This implies:

- The remaining two portals in eastern Suirx ([[Dossier 01-0007.4737]]) likely use identical anchor systems and are vulnerable to the same dispelling cascade.
- The Greyloch Forest portal ([[Dossier 19-3500.5743]]) is being constructed to a higher standard, likely Class-IV, suggesting it is intended for military-scale throughput rather than trafficking operations.
- The Dûrn Kordrek portal ([[Dossier 19-3999.8638]]) is confirmed Class-IV based on throughput estimates, requiring a more powerful approach.

([[Dossier 01-0007.3983.5738]]: "Portal Standardization: Implications for Countermeasure Uniformity")

---

**VII. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **Replicable Methodology:** The successful dispelling cascade proves that Class-III portals can be destroyed with mortal-level resources: no celestial intervention required. This is a significant operational finding.
2. **Scaling Challenge:** Class-IV portals will require stronger casters and more complex cascade procedures.
3. **Recommendation:** **[REDACTED]**: Develop a formal "Portal Destruction Handbook" codifying the Stios methodology for distribution to all qualified strike teams. Initiate research on Class-IV cascade procedures for the Greyloch and Dûrn Kordrek targets. **[REDACTED]**
