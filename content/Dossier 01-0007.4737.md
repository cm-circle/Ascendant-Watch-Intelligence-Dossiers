**CLASSIFIED: FOR CELESTIAL EYES ONLY**  
**REF. 01-0007.4737**  
**Origin:** Ascendant Watch: Portal Interdiction Division  
**Subject:** Active Portal Hunt: Eastern Suirx Territory

---

**I. OPERATIONAL STATUS**  
Following the successful destruction of the Stios Class-III portal ([[Dossier 01-0007.11]]), the Ascendant Watch has initiated a systematic search for the **two remaining portals** assessed to be operating in the eastern Suirx Trade Company Territories. This dossier documents the search methodology, current findings, and candidate sites.

---

**II. SEARCH METHODOLOGY**  
The portal hunt employs three parallel detection approaches:

### A. Arcane Trace Analysis
Extension of the Stios-to-Deri corridor methodology ([[Dossier 01-0007.3662]]) to additional trade routes. Lantern Scouts conduct systematic sweeps of major and secondary roads, looking for jade amulet signatures, conjuration residue, and dimensional thinning indicators.

**Progress:** 4 of 11 target corridors surveyed. Two corridors show promising trace concentrations (see Section III).

### B. Missing Persons Pattern Matching
Application of the Stios missing persons template ([[Dossier 01-0007.4892]]) to municipal records across the eastern Suirx territories. Ascendant Watch mortal operatives, posing as Suirx Trade Company statistical analysts, are collecting and cross-referencing disappearance data from 23 settlements.

**Progress:** Data collection complete for 15 of 23 settlements. Two settlements show anomalous patterns (see Section III).

### C. Municipal Corruption Screening
Vetting of local government officials for indicators of jade amulet dependency, unexplained wealth, or behavioral patterns consistent with Mayor Kreg's profile. This screening is conducted through discreet observation and financial record analysis; not direct confrontation.

**Progress:** 8 of 23 settlement administrations screened. One high-confidence corruption case identified (see Section III).

([[Dossier 01-0007.4737.1108]]: "Search Methodology: Three-Pillar Approach and Resource Allocation")

**III. CANDIDATE SITES**

### Candidate Alpha: Town of Halvek (NORTHERN)
**Population:** ~4,200  
**Location:** 160 km northeast of Deri, at the junction of the Halvek River and the Northern Trade Road  
**Indicator Strength:** HIGH

Evidence:
- Arcane trace analysis along the Deri-to-Halvek corridor revealed a moderate-intensity conjuration signature gradient increasing toward Halvek, similar to the Stios pattern ([[Dossier 01-0007.4737.2247]]: "Halvek Corridor, Arcane Trace Survey Results").
- Missing persons pattern: 67 disappearances over 7 months (baseline expectation: 8). Demographic profile matches the Stios template: transient adults, refugees, socially isolated individuals.
- Municipal corruption: The town's **Magistrate Rennik Coll** exhibits multiple Kreg-pattern indicators: recent unexplained property acquisitions, reduced responsiveness to citizen petitions, and a newly appointed "private secretary" whose identity cannot be verified through Suirx records.
- The Halvek mining district provides geographic cover for portal construction: abandoned mine shafts are both numerous and deep, offering concealed subterranean space.

**Assessment:** Halvek is the **highest-priority candidate** for Portal 2. Recommendation: advance to detailed pre-operation survey.

### Candidate Beta: Fishing Village of Tessik (COASTAL)
**Population:** ~1,100  
**Location:** 85 km south of Stios along the coast  
**Indicator Strength:** MODERATE

Evidence:
- Maritime trafficking traces from Stios harbor ([[Dossier 01-0007.3662]]) point to a secondary coastal collection point south of Stios. Tessik is the largest settlement in the indicated direction.
- Missing persons pattern: 23 disappearances over 5 months (baseline expectation: 2). The pattern skews toward fishermen and dockworkers: reflecting the settlement's maritime economy.
- No municipal corruption detected: Tessik is too small for a formal government structure. The village is administered by a **fishing guild council**.
- Tessik's coastal geography includes sea caves accessible only at low tide: potential concealment sites for a portal installation.

**Assessment:** Tessik is a viable candidate for Portal 3 but requires further investigation. The small population makes covert observation challenging ([[Dossier 01-0007.4737.3389]]: "Tessik Assessment: Maritime Evidence and Geographic Analysis").

### Candidate Gamma: Suirx City Outskirts (URBAN)
**Population:** Suirx City proper: ~35,000; suburban refugee camps: ~12,000  
**Indicator Strength:** LOW TO MODERATE

Evidence:
- Jade amulet distribution traces converge on the Suirx City refugee camp ([[Dossier 01-0007.3662]]), but this may simply reflect the endpoint of the distribution network rather than a portal location.
- Missing persons pattern: difficult to assess: Suirx City's large transient population makes baseline calculations unreliable. Reported disappearances are elevated, but the noise-to-signal ratio is unfavorable.
- No specific portal-indicative arcane signatures detected within city limits. However, the city's high ambient magical activity (multiple temples, arcane shops, guild halls) may be masking portal emissions ([[Dossier 01-0007.4737.4534]]: "Suirx City Assessment: Urban Environment and Detection Challenges").

**Assessment:** Suirx City cannot be excluded but is the weakest candidate. The urban environment complicates all detection methods.

**IV. TIMELINE AND RESOURCE REQUIREMENTS**  
Completing the portal hunt requires:

- **4 additional Lantern Scouts** to accelerate corridor sweeps (current allocation: 2)
- **6 mortal operatives** for missing persons data collection and municipal screening
- **30-45 days** to complete the three-pillar survey across all remaining settlements

If Candidate Alpha (Halvek) is confirmed, a pre-operation survey (equivalent to the town hall assessment; [[Dossier 01-0007.7561]]) can be initiated in parallel.

**V. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **Urgency:** The destroyed Stios portal's Arcanaloth escaped and will have reported the strike. The remaining portals may be hardened, relocated, or accelerated in their operations. Every day of delay increases the risk of countermeasures.
2. **Sequential vs. Simultaneous:** Ideally, both remaining portals should be destroyed simultaneously to prevent one alerting the other. This requires confirming both locations before executing either strike.
