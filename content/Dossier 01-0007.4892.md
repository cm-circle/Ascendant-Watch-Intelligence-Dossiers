**CLASSIFIED: FOR CELESTIAL EYES ONLY**  
**REF. 01-0007.4892**  
**Origin:** Ascendant Watch: Missing Persons Analysis Unit  
**Subject:** Missing Persons: Stios Municipal Workers, List and Last Known Movements

---

**I. OVERVIEW**  
This dossier documents **143 confirmed missing persons** from the Stios settlement and surrounding villages over a six-month period. The disappearance rate represents an 800% increase above the regional historical average and served as a primary intelligence indicator for Operation Valkyrhino ([[Dossier 01-0007.3515]]).

---

**II. DEMOGRAPHIC ANALYSIS**  
Analysis of the missing persons reveals deliberate targeting patterns:

| Category | Count | Percentage |
|---|---|---|
| Transient laborers (no fixed address) | 52 | 36% |
| Recent refugees (arrived within 6 months) | 38 | 27% |
| Single adults (no immediate family in area) | 29 | 20% |
| Dockworkers and fishermen | 14 | 10% |
| Unknown/other | 10 | 7% |

**Key indicators of deliberate selection:**
- **No children.** The complete absence of child disappearances in a population with significant child poverty suggests selective targeting rather than opportunistic predation.
- **Social isolation.** Over 83% of victims had no spouse, dependent children, or close family members in the Stios area. These are individuals whose absence would generate minimal investigation.
- **Employment pattern.** Many victims were employed in physically demanding occupations, construction, dock labor, mining assistance. This suggests a preference for healthy, able-bodied adults ([[Dossier 01-0007.4892.1182]]: "Victim Selection Criteria, Statistical Analysis and Targeting Logic").

---

**III. DISAPPEARANCE MECHANISM**  
Post-operation interviews with liberated captives and arrested collaborators  reconstructed the trafficking mechanism:

### Stage 1: Identification
Collaborators; primarily tavern owners, lodging house operators, and labor brokers who received gold as payment; identified potential victims and reported them to Kreg's network. Reports included the individual's name, physical description, social connections (if any), and daily routine ([[Dossier 01-0007.4892.2347]]: "Collaborator Network; Identified Informants and Payment Structure").

### Stage 2: Acquisition
Victims were approached with offers of temporary employment: typically "warehouse work" or "construction projects" at the Brysbil Citadel. Those who accepted were escorted to the Citadel and not seen again. Those who declined were targeted for forcible abduction during nighttime hours by Mezzoloth snatch teams operating under magical concealment.

Approximately 60% of victims were lured voluntarily; 40% were abducted.

### Stage 3: Processing
Within the Citadel sub-level, victims were fitted with jade amulets ([[Dossier 19-3999.9275]]) and held in the portal chamber's holding cells.

### Stage 4: Transit
After 7-14 days of processing, victims were transported through the Class-III portal ([[Dossier 01-0007.3983]]) to an unknown destination. The transit process was described by one surviving witness as:

> *"They made us stand in a line. The green light came from the ground. Then the person at the front just... stepped forward and wasn't there anymore. One by one. Nobody came back."*

([[Dossier 01-0007.4892.3514]]: "Victim Processing: Captive Testimony and Stage Timeline")

---

**IV. THE 47 SURVIVORS**  
Operation Valkyrhino liberated 47 individuals from the Citadel sub-level at various stages of processing:

- 12 were in Stage 2 (recently acquired, pre-amulet)
- 23 were in Stage 3 (3-10 days captive)
- 12 were in Stage 4 (awaiting transit)

All 47 were transferred to the treatment vessel in Stios harbor for immediate medical and spiritual care ([[Dossier 01-0007.7631]]). Of the 47, 35 have been individually identified. The remaining 12; all refugees with no prior documentation; remain unidentified and are being processed through the Suirx Refugee Bureau ([[Dossier 01-0007.4892.4682]]: "Survivor Identification; Status and Outstanding Cases").

---

**V. THE 96 UNRECOVERED**  
Of the 143 confirmed missing, 47 were rescued. The remaining **96 individuals** were transited through the portal before Operation Valkyrhino could be executed.

Their fate is unknown. Based on jade amulet analysis ([[Dossier 19-3999.9275]]) and Operative Thornshade's observations of soul processing in Dûrn Kordrek ([[Dossier 19-3999.8638]]), the most probable scenario is:

- Victims were transported to an infernal-controlled processing facility
- Their jade amulets were brought to full-charge through forced death
- Captured souls were extracted and processed for an unknown final purpose

This assessment carries **moderate confidence**. Alternative scenarios; forced labor, conscription into infernal service, or sale to third parties; cannot be excluded ([[Dossier 01-0007.4892.5817]]: "Unrecovered Victims; Probable Fate Analysis and Alternative Scenarios").

---

**VI. MUNICIPAL COMPLICITY**  
Mayor Halverd Kreg has been arrested and is currently in Suirx Trade Company custody. Preliminary interrogation reveals:

- Kreg was approached by "trade representatives" approximately eight months before the portal's discovery. He was offered gold in exchange for administrative cooperation.
- Kreg personally signed off on falsified municipal missing persons reports that underreported disappearances.
- Kreg claims he did not know the victims were being killed or soul-harvested. He states he believed they were being "relocated to labor contracts in a foreign territory."

His claims of ignorance are assessed as **partially credible**, he likely did not understand the full scope of the operation, but he was wilfully blind to the victims' fate ([[Dossier 01-0007.4892.6203]]: "Kreg Interrogation, Complicity Assessment and Legal Status").

---

**VII. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **Pattern Replication:** The victim selection criteria and acquisition mechanism are sufficiently systematic to be deployed at any settlement in the Suirx territories. The remaining two portals ([[Dossier 01-0007.4737]]) likely operate identical networks.
2. **Warning Indicators:** Future operations can use the Stios pattern as a detection template: sudden increases in missing transient adults, jade amulet distribution, and municipal corruption are the three-pronged warning.
3. **Recommendation:** **[REDACTED]**: Issue a classified bulletin to Suirx Trade Company law enforcement identifying the missing persons pattern signatures. Deploy mortal intelligence operatives to settlements matching the high-risk profile. **[REDACTED]**
