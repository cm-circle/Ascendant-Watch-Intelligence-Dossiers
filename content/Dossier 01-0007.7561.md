**CLASSIFIED: FOR CELESTIAL EYES ONLY**  
**REF. 01-0007.7561**  
**Origin:** Ascendant Watch: Structural Intelligence Unit  
**Subject:** Brysbil Citadel: Structural Survey and Infiltration Routes

---

**I. OVERVIEW**  
The Brysbil Citadel served as the cover facility for the Stios Class-III portal installation. This structural survey was conducted pre-operationally by Lantern Scout LN-038 and mortal agents posing as building inspectors. It provided the tactical foundation for Operation Valkyrhino's infiltration plan ([[Dossier 01-0007.3515]]).

---

**II. STRUCTURAL PROFILE**

**Construction Date:** Approximately 400 years ago  
**Original Purpose:** Coastal naval defense installation  
**Current Use:** Municipal government offices (above ground); portal site (hidden sub-level)  
**Construction Material:** Local limestone blocks, iron reinforcement bands, slate roofing  
**Footprint:** 45m × 30m, irregular quadrilateral conforming to the promontory shape  

### Levels:
- **Level 4 (Top):** Mayor's residence and private study. Single access stairway.
- **Level 3:** Administrative offices: tax records, trade licensing, civic courts.
- **Level 2:** Municipal hall: public functions, council chamber, reception areas.
- **Level 1 (Ground):** Gatehouse, guard quarters, armory, kitchens.
- **Basement 1:** Historical dungeons: converted to document storage. Original cell doors still in place.
- **Basement 2:** Deep storage: rarely accessed, used for seasonal surplus.
- **Sub-Level (Hidden):** Portal chamber. Not on any architectural plan. Accessed via a magically concealed stairway from Basement 2 ([[Dossier 01-0007.7561.1147]]: "Hidden Stairway: Discovery Method, Concealment Enchantment, and Access Mechanism").

---

**III. ENTRY POINTS AND INFILTRATION ROUTES**

### Route Alpha: Harbor Service Tunnel (SELECTED)
A 200-meter tunnel running from the harbor seawall to Basement 1. Originally a supply passage for the naval garrison. Currently used for Citadel provisioning.

- **Access:** Stone archway at the harbor seawall, partially submerged at high tide. Accessible during low-tide windows (approximately 4 hours per tidal cycle).
- **Hazards:** No traps or wards detected. The tunnel is regularly used by kitchen staff, ensuring it remains unobstructed.
- **Final Entry:** The tunnel terminates at a reinforced wooden door in Basement 1. Lock is mechanical: standard Suirx-pattern padlock, pickable by a competent rogue in under 30 seconds.
- **Rating: OPTIMAL**: Low detection risk, direct access to the basement levels, and no magical concealment to bypass.

### Route Beta: Eastern Sally Port (BACKUP)
A collapsed military sally port on the promontory's eastern face. The collapse is partial; a skilled climber could negotiate the rubble pile and access a narrow passage leading to Basement 2.

- **Access:** Exterior approach via a 15-meter cliff climb (difficulty moderate, piton-assisted).
- **Hazards:** The rubble pile is unstable. Noise risk: HIGH. Any collapse during transit would alert the garrison.
- **Rating: MARGINAL**: Backup route only. Noise risk is unacceptable for a stealth insertion.

### Route Gamma: Main Gatehouse (NOT RECOMMENDED)
The Citadel's primary entrance; a fortified archway with iron portcullis, manned by 4 guards (2 corrupted, 2 standard municipal) at all times.

- **Access:** Direct approach from Stios town square.
- **Hazards:** Full guard contingent, portcullis mechanism, alarm bell.
- **Rating: NON-VIABLE** for covert insertion. Suitable only for overt assault ([[Dossier 01-0007.7561.2301]]: "Entry Point Assessment: Comparative Risk Matrix").

**Notable:** The sub-level is secured entirely by infernal forces, no human guards. This creates a security compartmentalization: the town guards above are aware that the basement is "off-limits" but do not know what lies below. Only Mayor Kreg and the single Basement 2 guard have knowledge of the hidden stairway ([[Dossier 01-0007.7561.3478]]: "Security Compartmentalization, Knowledge Distribution by Guard Tier").

---

**V. SUB-LEVEL; PORTAL CHAMBER LAYOUT**  
The hidden sub-level is a roughly circular chamber approximately 25 meters in diameter, carved from the limestone bedrock beneath the Citadel. It was not part of the original construction; arcane residue dating (transmutation-school) indicates the chamber was excavated approximately 9 months ago, predating the Citadel's use as a portal site by approximately one month.

**Chamber contents (pre-operation):**
- **Portal anchor:** Central to the chamber: a ring of 6 obsidian pylons, each 2 meters tall, arranged in a hexagonal pattern. The portal activates between the pylons.
- **Holding cells:** 12 cells along the eastern wall, iron-barred, each approximately 2m × 3m. Capacity: approximately 50 individuals under crowded conditions.
- **Command station:** A stone desk and enchanted communication array on the western side, used by the Cambion administrators.
- **Guard positions:** 4 alcoves at cardinal points around the chamber, occupied by Mezzoloth sentries.
- **Service area:** Northern section: provisions, water barrels, and a latrine system.

([[Dossier 01-0007.7561.4612]]: "Sub-Level Chamber: Detailed Floor Plan and Equipment Inventory")

---

**VI. POST-OPERATION STATUS**  
Following Operation Valkyrhino:
- The portal anchor pylons were destroyed during the dispelling cascade. The chamber is now inert.
- The holding cells are empty: all captives evacuated.
- Structural damage to the Citadel above is minimal: the portal's destruction did not compromise the building's integrity.
- The Citadel has been placed under Suirx Trade Company military control pending the investigation into Mayor Kreg ([[Dossier 01-0007.7561.5738]]: "Post-Operation Citadel Status: Structural Assessment and Custody").

---

**VII. STRATEGIC VALUE**  
The Brysbil survey methodology; combining Lantern arcane scanning, mortal infiltrator reconnaissance, and structural analysis; proved effective and should serve as the standard template for future portal-site assessments. The key lesson: hidden sub-levels are a preferred infernal construction technique, and any suspected portal site should be surveyed to a depth below any known architectural plans ([[Dossier 01-0007.7561.6893]]: "Methodology Review; Survey Template for Future Portal-Site Assessments").
