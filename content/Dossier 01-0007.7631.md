**CLASSIFIED: FOR CELESTIAL EYES ONLY**  
**REF. 01-0007.7631**  
**Origin:** Ascendant Watch: Medical Division  
**Subject:** Post-Operation Civilian Welfare: Stios Amulet Dependency Cases

---

**I. OVERVIEW**  
Following Operation Valkyrhino and the destruction of the Stios portal, the Ascendant Watch deployed a **Clerical Treatment Team (CTT)** aboard the chartered vessel *Dawn's Promise* in Stios harbor. The CTT was tasked with treating the dual civilian populations affected by the portal's destruction:

1. **The 47 liberated captives**: removed from the Citadel sub-level at various stages of jade amulet processing
2. **The broader Stios civilian population**: an estimated 200+ residents who had become jade amulet-dependent through the distribution network

---

**II. LIBERATED CAPTIVE TREATMENT; 47 INDIVIDUALS**

### Triage Categories:

| Category | Count | Amulet Exposure | Treatment Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| Green (minimal) | 12 | Pre-amulet contact | Observation only |
| Yellow (moderate) | 23 | 3-10 days forced exposure | *Greater Restoration* + 2 weeks care |
| Red (severe) | 12 | 10+ days forced exposure | *Greater Restoration* + intensive 30-day program |

### Treatment Outcomes:
- **Green cases:** All 12 released within 48 hours with no lasting effects. Psychological counseling offered; 7 accepted.
- **Yellow cases:** 21 of 23 responded well to *Greater Restoration* and showed significant improvement within one week. Two cases required extended care due to pre-existing health conditions that complicated recovery. Soul fragment recovery estimated at 95%+ for this group.
- **Red cases:** 8 of 12 responded to intensive treatment within 30 days. 4 cases remain in ongoing care, their soul fragmentation was advanced, and the attending clerics assess permanent diminishment at 10-15% for these individuals. One patient, a male human, approximately 55, unidentified, remains in a **persistent dissociative state**, responding to external stimuli but unable to form new memories or speak coherently. This case may require miracle-tier divine intervention ([[Dossier 01-0007.7631.1108]]: "CTT Treatment Records, Individual Case Files and Outcomes").

---

**III. CIVILIAN POPULATION TREATMENT; 200+ CASES**

### Discovery and Assessment
The post-operation civilian survey identified **214 individuals** in Stios and surrounding villages exhibiting jade amulet dependency symptoms. The survey was conducted by CTT members working alongside Suirx Trade Company health officials under the pretense of a "public health investigation."

Dependency distribution:

| Stage | Cases | Assessment |
|---|---|---|
| Early (1-8 weeks use) | 89 | Treatable with standard *Greater Restoration* |
| Moderate (2-4 months use) | 78 | Treatable with *Greater Restoration* + follow-up |
| Advanced (4+ months use) | 38 | Intensive treatment required |
| End-stage | 9 | Miracle-tier intervention may be needed |

### Treatment Capacity Constraints
The CTT comprises **4 clerics** of sufficient level to cast *Greater Restoration*. Each cleric can perform approximately 3 restorations per day (limited by spell slot availability and mental fatigue). At this rate, treating 214 patients requires approximately **18 days** of sustained operation, during which the clerics must remain in Stios harbor, creating a visible and potentially compromising celestial-linked presence ([[Dossier 01-0007.7631.2247]]: "Treatment Capacity, Throughput Calculations and Operational Security Constraints").

Current status: treatment is approximately 60% complete. Priority has been given to advanced and end-stage cases.

---

**IV. WITHDRAWAL EFFECTS; UNTREATED CASES**  
With the portal destroyed and the amulet supply chain severed, amulet-dependent civilians who have not yet received treatment are experiencing withdrawal. Symptoms include:

- **Physical:** Cold sweats, tremors, joint pain, nausea, insomnia
- **Psychological:** Depression, anxiety, paranoia, despair, suicidal ideation
- **Spiritual:** A sensation described as "emptiness" or "a hole where something should be": the subjective experience of soul fragmentation

Three untreated individuals **attempted suicide** in the first week following the portal's destruction. All three were intercepted. The CTT has established a **crisis intervention team** working 24-hour shifts to identify and assist at-risk individuals ([[Dossier 01-0007.7631.3389]]: "Withdrawal Syndrome: Clinical Presentation and Crisis Intervention Protocols").

---

**V. SOCIAL IMPACT**  
The jade amulet epidemic has damaged Stios's social fabric:

- **Stigma:** Amulet-dependent individuals are increasingly stigmatized by non-dependent neighbors. The term "jaders" has entered local slang as a pejorative.
- **Economic Impact:** Many dependent individuals ceased productive work during advanced dependency stages. Community businesses have lost workers, and family structures have been disrupted.
- **Trust Deficit:** The revelation of Mayor Kreg's complicity has created a crisis of institutional trust. Several Stios residents have expressed hostility toward *all* authority figures, including the CTT.
- **Conspiracy Theories:** A minority of Stios residents believe the jade amulet problem was created by the Suirx Trade Company to justify increased control over the town. This narrative is false but gaining traction ([[Dossier 01-0007.7631.4534]]: "Social Impact Assessment: Stigma, Trust, and Misinformation in Stios").

---

**VI. LESSONS FOR FUTURE OPERATIONS**  
Post-operation civilian welfare was inadequately planned for Operation Valkyrhino. Key lessons:

1. **Pre-position medical assets:** CTT deployment prior to operation execution would have reduced the withdrawal impact window.
2. **Scale treatment capacity:** 4 clerics for 214 patients is insufficient. Future operations should plan for 1 cleric per 30 expected dependency cases.
3. **Communication strategy:** A pre-prepared public health communication plan would have reduced misinformation and stigma.
4. **Amulet supply tapering:** Rather than abrupt supply cessation (portal destruction), future operations should consider a **managed withdrawal** approach: gradually reducing amulet availability rather than eliminating it overnight.

([[Dossier 01-0007.7631.5671]]: "Lessons Learned: Civilian Welfare Integration into Portal Strike Planning")

---

**VII. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **Humanitarian Cost:** The jade amulet dependency epidemic is a **civilian casualty** of the infernal portal network, equivalent in moral weight to a military attack on a population center.
2. **Operational Tension:** The tension between operational urgency (destroy the portal) and humanitarian considerations (treat the dependent population) will recur at every future portal strike site. Planning must account for both.
3. **Recommendation:** **[REDACTED]**: Complete the Stios treatment program. Document the CTT protocols and outcomes for use as a training case for future operations. Pre-identify clerical assets for deployment to the remaining two portal sites. **[REDACTED]**
