**I. FORCE DESIGNATION AND OVERVIEW**  
The **Velvet Iron Army** is the combined invasion force operating under the supreme command of **Graz'zt, the Dark Prince** (CR 24), Demon Lord of the Triple Realm ([[Dossier 03-0378.6541]]: "Unconfirmed Links to Graz'zt Command Structure"). The army's name derives from its operational doctrine: the "velvet" of subversion and seduction precedes the "iron" of military destruction. This dual-pronged approach distinguishes it from previous abyssal incursions, which have historically relied on brute overwhelming force.

Total estimated strength: **175,000-200,000 combatants**, supplemented by an unknown number of non-combatant support entities (portal engineers, logisticians, overseers).

This dossier represents the most comprehensive order of battle assessment available. Accuracy is estimated at **70-80%**; significant blind spots remain regarding deep reserve formations and Graz'zt's personal guard ([[Dossier 03-0378.9439]]: "Intelligence Gaps: Unobserved Force Elements and Confidence Assessment").

**II. FORCE COMPOSITION BY CATEGORY**

### A. Cannon Fodder: The Dretch Swarm
**Estimated Strength:** 150,000  
**CR Rating:** 1/4 (individually negligible)  
**Role:** Expendable mass assault infantry. Dretches are deployed in overwhelming numbers to absorb defensive firepower, exhaust enemy spell resources, and create chaos in fortified positions.

Tactical notes:
- Deployed in waves of 5,000-10,000 against fixed positions.
- Controlled by embedded **Chasme** (fly-demons) acting as herding overseers; destruction of the Chasme typically causes the swarm to lose cohesion within minutes.
- Dretches emit a **fetid cloud** upon death, creating zones of toxic gas that further disrupt defenders.
- Replacement rate is effectively unlimited, new Dretches are conjured through battlefield portal-spawners at a rate of approximately 500 per day per active spawner ([[Dossier 03-0378.7233]]: "Dretch Spawning Infrastructure, Observed Portal-Spawner Locations and Output").

### B. Mid-Tier Combat Forces: Vrock and Hezrou Formations
**Estimated Strength:** 20,000 combined (approximately 12,000 Vrocks, 8,000 Hezrou)  
**CR Rating:** Vrock CR 6; Hezrou CR 8  
**Role:** Combined arms assault. Vrocks provide aerial superiority and rapid strike capability; Hezrou serve as heavy assault infantry for breakthrough operations.

Tactical notes:
- **Vrocks** operate in hunting packs of 6-12, targeting enemy spellcasters, siege engines, and command positions. Their **stunning screech** ability is employed in massed formation to incapacitate defensive lines before ground assault.
- **Hezrou** are deployed as linebreakers, exploiting gaps created by Dretch waves. Their **stench aura** compounds the area-denial effect of Dretch gas clouds.
- Both formations are coordinated through a system of infernal war-horns operated by embedded Glabrezu field commanders ([[Dossier 03-0378.6143]]: "Infernal Battlefield Communications: Signal Analysis and Disruption Protocols").

### C. Elite Forces: Glabrezu, Nalfeshnee, and Marilith Officers
**Estimated Strength:** 800-1,200 combined  
**CR Rating:** Glabrezu CR 9; Nalfeshnee CR 13; Marilith CR 16  
**Role:** Field command, heavy combat, and strategic operations.

- **Glabrezu** (est. 600) serve as company-level commanders, each controlling a mixed formation of Vrocks and Hezrou. They also function as battlefield interrogators and morale enforcers.
- **Nalfeshnee** (est. 150) occupy regimental command positions and serve as aerial heavy combatants. Their **horror nimbus** ability is used as a psychological weapon against massed mortal troops ([[Dossier 03-0378.8353]]: "Nalfeshnee Psychological Warfare: Observed Effects on Mortal Combat Effectiveness").
- **Marilith** (est. 50-80) are the operational-level commanders. Each Marilith commands a theater-sized formation and reports directly to the Balor high command. Their multi-armed combat capability makes them devastating in personal engagements; confirmed kills include multiple mortal commanders and at least one celestial observer ([[Dossier 03-0378.5831]]: "Confirmed Marilith Kill List: Material Plane Operations").

### D. Strategic Assets: Balors (Dread Destruction Engines)
**Estimated Strength:** 50  
**CR Rating:** CR 19  
**Role:** Strategic-level destruction, siege warfare, and command at the highest level beneath Graz'zt and Thraxxia.

Tactical notes:
- Each Balor is designated a **Dread Destruction Engine**, a title reflecting their role as living siege weapons. A single Balor can reduce a fortified city to rubble within hours.
- The Balor corps operates under a loose hierarchy; the senior Balor, **"Ruin-Tongue"** ([[Dossier 19-3999.11]]: see Ulbgar breach), is believed to hold the equivalent of a supreme field marshal position.
- Balors are deployed sparingly and with deliberation. Their death-explosion upon destruction makes them weapons of last resort in populated areas, or deliberate weapons of mass destruction ([[Dossier 03-0378.4553]]: "Balor Death-Detonation Analysis, Blast Radius, Energy Yield, and Defensive Countermeasures").
- Internal friction exists between the Balor war council and Thraxxia's intelligence apparatus ([[Dossier 03-0380.11]]). The Balors favor annihilation; Thraxxia favors conquest and preservation. This tension has occasionally resulted in uncoordinated operations.

### E. Specialist Assets: Arcanaloths (Portal Engineers)
**Estimated Strength:** Unknown (estimated 30-50)  
**CR Rating:** CR 12  
**Role:** Portal construction, maintenance, and arcane infrastructure.

- Arcanaloths are responsible for the Velvet Iron Army's most critical strategic capability: the **portal network** that enables force projection, supply logistics, and soul-amulet trafficking across the material plane and between planes.
- Each Arcanaloth typically operates with a guard detail of 4-8 Mezzoloths and maintains 2-3 active portals simultaneously.
- The destruction of their portals (as in Operation Valkyrhino: [[Dossier 01-0007.11]]) causes significant logistical disruption but has not yet achieved strategic-level interdiction due to portal redundancy.
- Arcanaloth personnel are assessed as irreplaceable in the short term; each one destroyed represents a permanent reduction in enemy portal capacity ([[Dossier 03-0378.4338]]: "Arcanaloth Attrition Tracking: Confirmed and Probable Kills").

**III. COMMAND HIERARCHY**

```
GRAZ'ZT (Supreme Commander)
├── THRAXXIA (Intelligence / Political Warfare / Ziragz)
│   ├── Cambion agents (infiltration)
│   ├── Succubi/Incubi cells (subversion)
│   └── Arcanaloth portal network (logistics)
├── BALOR WAR COUNCIL (Military Command)
│   ├── Ruin-Tongue (Senior Balor / Field Marshal)
│   ├── 49 additional Balors (theater commands)
│   ├── Marilith operational commanders
│   └── Glabrezu / Nalfeshnee field officers
└── YUGOLOTH AUXILIARIES (Contracted support)
    ├── Arcanaloth engineers
    ├── Mezzoloth security details
    └── Ultroloth overseers (Jade Amulet operations)
```

Note: The Yugoloth contingent operates under mercenary contract rather than loyalty. Their continued service is contingent on payment, primarily in jade amulets and harvested soul-energy. Disruption of the amulet supply chain could fracture this alliance ([[Dossier 03-0378.6752]]: "Yugoloth Contractual Terms, Estimated Defection Thresholds").

**IV. OPERATIONAL DOCTRINE**  
The Velvet Iron Army's doctrine is structured in three phases:

1. **Phase One: "The Velvet":** Subversion, corruption, and destabilization of target territories through espionage, bribery, and political manipulation. Goal: weaken or neutralize mortal resistance before military engagement.
2. **Phase Two: "The Iron":** Conventional military assault using Dretch swarms followed by mid-tier combined arms. Objective: territorial conquest and population capture for soul harvesting.
3. **Phase Three: "The Gilt":** Post-conquest governance under Thraxxia's Ziragz model. Mortal populations are offered the Compact of Gilt; those who refuse are processed through jade amulet harvesting.

Current assessment: The Velvet Iron Army is operating in **Phase One against the Preanese Theocracy** ([[Dossier 19-3611.11]]), **Phase Two against Stuik and the eastern territories** ([[Dossier 19-3522.0001]]), and **Phase Three in conquered Ulbgar and Ziragz** ([[Dossier 19-3999.11]], [[Dossier 03-0380.11]]).

**V. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **Overall Threat:** The Velvet Iron Army is the most organized, disciplined, and strategically sophisticated abyssal invasion force in recorded history. It combines unlimited expendable troops, elite command, political warfare, and economic infrastructure.
2. **Key Vulnerability:** Force cohesion depends on three pillars: portal logistics (Arcanaloths), Yugoloth mercenary alignment (jade amulet payments), and Graz'zt's personal authority. Disruption of any one pillar would degrade the invasion.
3. **Critical Gap:** The location and disposition of Graz'zt himself. He has not been observed on the material plane; his command is exercised remotely. Determining whether he is personally present or directing operations from the Abyss is a Tier-0 intelligence priority.
4. **Recommendation:** **[REDACTED]**: Focus interdiction efforts on the Arcanaloth portal engineers and jade amulet supply chain as force multiplier degradation. Avoid direct engagement with Balors except where absolutely necessary. Develop contingency protocols for a potential Phase Two escalation against the Preanese Theocracy. **[REDACTED]**
