**REF. 03-0378.4553**  
**Origin:** Ascendant Watch: Weapons Effects Analysis  
**Subject:** Balor Death-Detonation: Blast Analysis and Countermeasure Protocols

---

**I. OVERVIEW**  
When a Balor is killed, it does not simply die: it **detonates** in a catastrophic explosion of fire and destructive energy. This "death throes" effect is a fundamental biological property of all Balors and cannot be prevented, suppressed, or redirected. The detonation transforms every Balor kill into a potential **mass-casualty event** for the killing force and any nearby populations.

This dossier documents the observed detonation parameters, models the blast effects, and proposes operational protocols for managing the death-detonation risk.

---

**II. OBSERVED DETONATIONS**  
Only **two** Balor detonations have been observed on the material plane during this campaign:

### Detonation 1: "Ashterrex" at the Battle of Korras Bluffs
**Combat Rating:** Standard Balor (CR 19)  
**Killing Force:** Combined Stuik heavy infantry and celestial strike element (3 Lanterns)  
**Kill Mechanism:** Sustained melee assault, final blow delivered by celestial strike element  
**Blast Parameters:**
- Radius: **80 meters** from the Balor's corpse
- Temperature at center: estimated 1,500°C (melted stone observed in a 10-meter radius)
- Temperature at edge: approximately 200°C (third-degree burns, ignition of organic material)
- Shockwave: equivalent to approximately 2 tonnes of conventional explosive
- Duration: approximately 4 seconds from kill to full blast dissipation

**Casualties:** 47 Stuik soldiers killed by the detonation (friendly fire). 3 additional soldiers critically wounded. All three Lantern operators survived due to fire resistance but were incapacitated for 2-3 days by the shockwave.

([[Dossier 03-0378.4553.1147]]: "Detonation 1: Ashterrex at Korras Bluffs, Full Analysis")

### Detonation 2: "Vuurkaas" at the Siege of Blackwall
**Combat Rating:** Standard Balor  
**Kill Mechanism:** *Holy Word* and sustained bombardment  
**Blast Parameters:** Consistent with Detonation 1; 80-meter radius, comparable temperature and shockwave  
**Casualties:** 12 killed (all Stuik), 23 wounded. Defensive position partially destroyed.  
**Notable:** Vuurkaas detonated within a Stuik defensive fortification; the blast destroyed approximately 30 meters of defensive wall, creating a breach that required emergency fortification.

([[Dossier 03-0378.4553.2301]]: "Detonation 2: Vuurkaas at Blackwall, Full Analysis")

**III. THEORETICAL MODELING; ENHANCED BALORS**  
Standard Balor detonation: 80m radius. However, **enhanced Balors**, individuals of exceptional age or power like Ruin-Tongue ([[Dossier 19-3999.3178]]), are expected to produce larger detonations:

| Balor Category | Estimated Blast Radius | Temperature (Center) |
|---|---|---|
| Standard (CR 19) | 80m | 1,500°C |
| Enhanced (CR 20-21) | 120-150m | 2,000°C+ |
| Ancient (CR 22+, if extant) | 200m+ | Unknown; potentially stone-melting throughout |

Ruin-Tongue is assessed as Enhanced category. His death-detonation is modeled at **150-meter radius** with temperatures sufficient to melt standard fortification materials ([[Dossier 03-0378.4553.3478]]: "Enhanced Balor Detonation: Theoretical Models and Ruin-Tongue Assessment").

---

**IV. OPERATIONAL IMPLICATIONS**

1. **The Balor Kill Paradox:** Killing a Balor removes a dangerous enemy but creates a mass-casualty event that can inflict more damage on friendly forces than the Balor itself did while alive. Every Balor engagement plan must include a **detonation management strategy**.
2. **Defensive Value to the Enemy:** The enemy is aware of the paradox. Balors are sometimes deployed in **close proximity to mortal defensive positions** specifically because their death-detonation would destroy the position: creating a situation where the defenders cannot kill the Balor without destroying their own fortification.
3. **Recovery Time:** Even a survived detonation incapacitates nearby personnel (heat injuries, concussion, psychological shock) for hours to days. The post-detonation recovery window is exploited by infernal follow-up forces.

---

**V. COUNTERMEASURE PROTOCOLS**

### Protocol 1: Standoff Kill
Kill the Balor from beyond the detonation radius using ranged weapons, artillery, or long-range spells. This is the preferred approach but requires sustained ranged capability against a flying, fire-resistant target. *Guiding Bolt*, *Arrow of Slaying*, and concentrated ballista fire are viable options.

Minimum engagement range for standard Balors: **100 meters** (80m blast + 20m safety margin)

### Protocol 2: Containment Kill
Engage the Balor within a pre-prepared containment area; a cleared zone with no friendly forces or valuable infrastructure within the blast radius. Lure or drive the Balor into the containment zone before delivering the killing blow.

This requires terrain preparation and tactical patience but is the safest method for minimizing friendly casualties.

### Protocol 3: Shield-and-Scatter
The current field expedient: when a Balor is near death, the commanding officer orders a **scatter**; all friendly forces within 100 meters retreat at maximum speed. Clerics prepare *Shield of Faith* and *Protection from Energy (Fire)* on retreating personnel. This protocol is effective when the Balor's kill moment can be accurately predicted, which it often cannot.

### Protocol 4: Banishment Before Kill
*Banishment* the Balor back to the Abyss and kill it there; causing the detonation on its home plane rather than the material plane. This is the theoretically ideal solution but requires maintaining concentration on *Banishment* while delivering lethal damage through the planar boundary; currently beyond available capability ([[Dossier 03-0378.4553.4612]]: "Countermeasure Protocols; Comparative Analysis and Feasibility").

---

**VI. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **Force Planning:** Any operation targeting Ruin-Tongue must plan for a 150-meter detonation radius. This means the kill must occur in open terrain or a prepared containment zone: not within a city, fortress, or population center.
2. **The Ulbgar Lesson:** When the Dûrn Kordrek forge raid is planned, the possibility of Balor presence in the capital must be accounted for. A Balor detonation inside the collapsed capital could destroy the remaining survivable infrastructure.
3. **Recommendation:** **[REDACTED]**: Mandate Protocol 1 (Standoff Kill) as the default engagement doctrine. Develop dedicated Balor-killing strike teams trained specifically in detonation management. Conduct terrain surveys to identify containment zones along the Stuik front. **[REDACTED]**
