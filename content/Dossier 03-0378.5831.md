**I. OVERVIEW**  
**Mariliths** (CR 16) are six-armed serpentine demons serving as elite battlefield commanders within the Velvet Iron Army. They combine tactical intelligence with devastating personal combat capability. The Velvet Iron Army deploys an estimated **8-12 Mariliths** on the material plane, each commanding combined-arms formations of 500-1,000 fiends.

This dossier records the named mortal casualties, military commanders, heroes, and notable individuals, killed in direct combat with Mariliths. It serves both as a memorial record and as a tactical analysis resource for understanding Marilith combat methodology.

---

**II. THE KILL LIST**

### Kill 1: General Aldric Thorn (Kingdom of Stuik)
**Rank:** Commander, Stuik 3rd Field Army  
**Engagement:** Battle of Coldbreak Ford, Stuik Eastern Front  
**Marilith Designation:** "Shivarii" (the Silk Fang)  
**Circumstances:** General Thorn led a counter-attack against an infernal column that had crossed the Coldbreak River. Shivarii, commanding the column's rearguard, engaged Thorn and his bodyguard directly. Thorn was killed by simultaneous strikes from four of Shivarii's six swords; his magical shield blocked the first two strikes but could not sustain coverage against the remaining four.  
**Tactical Lesson:** Mariliths exploit action economy; six attacks per round overwhelm any single-target defensive strategy.

([[Dossier 03-0378.5831.1147]]: "Kill 1: General Thorn, Full Engagement Reconstruction")

### Kill 2: Paladin-Commander Orest Veyn (Order of the Silver Flame)
**Rank:** Commander, Silver Flame chapter house  
**Engagement:** Siege of Kethren, Stuik-Ulbgar border  
**Marilith Designation:** "Xalthiis" (the Bloom of Steel)  
**Circumstances:** Veyn led a sortie against besieging infernal forces. Xalthiis challenged Veyn to single combat; a challenge Veyn accepted based on paladin honor codes. Xalthiis employed a **reactive tail-sweep** technique that knocked Veyn prone after he committed to an overhead strike; she then delivered the killing blow while he was grounded.  
**Tactical Lesson:** Mariliths deliberately exploit honor-based combat traditions. Never accept a Marilith's single-combat challenge; it is a trap.

### Kill 3: Archmage Senna Korr (Stuik War College)
**Rank:** Chief Battle-Mage, Stuik 1st Arcane Support Battalion  
**Engagement:** Defense of Halvegård Tower, Stuik Central Front  
**Marilith Designation:** Shivarii (the Silk Fang)  
**Circumstances:** Korr engaged Shivarii at range with *Disintegrate* and *Chain Lightning*. Shivarii's innate magic resistance neutralized both spells and she closed the distance in under 6 seconds; faster than Korr could prepare a second volley. Korr was killed in melee. Her staff was claimed as a trophy by Shivarii.  
**Tactical Lesson:** Mariliths possess innate magic resistance (advantage on saves against spells and magical effects). Spellcaster engagement must account for expected spell failure rates of 30-50%.

([[Dossier 03-0378.5831.2301]]: "Kills 2-3: Veyn and Korr, Engagement Analysis")

### Kill 4: Captain Branna Steelsong (Stuik Rangers)
**Rank:** Captain, 7th Ranger Reconnaissance Company  
**Engagement:** Ambush at the Thornway Pass  
**Marilith Designation:** "Netharii" (the Coil)  
**Circumstances:** Steelsong's company was conducting reconnaissance behind infernal lines when they were detected by Netharii's forward scouts. Netharii personally led the pursuit, navigating the mountainous terrain with her serpentine lower body faster than the human rangers could retreat. Steelsong sacrificed herself to buy time for her company's escape, she engaged Netharii alone in a narrow pass, delaying the Marilith for approximately 7 minutes before being killed.  
**Tactical Lesson:** Mariliths are not restricted to flat terrain. Their serpentine body allows them to navigate rocky, uneven terrain at speeds equal to or exceeding mounted cavalry. Pursuit retreat assumptions must be revised.

### Kill 5: War-Priest Dugan Hammerfalt (Church of Moradin)
**Rank:** Senior War-Priest attached to the Stuik 2nd Field Army  
**Engagement:** Relief of Fort Ashenmoor  
**Marilith Designation:** Xalthiis (the Bloom of Steel)  
**Circumstances:** Hammerfalt successfully *Banished* Xalthiis during the initial engagement; the first confirmed Marilith banishment of the campaign. However, the banishment's 1-minute duration expired (the battle prevented sustained concentration), and Xalthiis returned. Hammerfalt attempted a second banishment but Xalthiis, now targeting him specifically, closed the distance before the casting was complete.  
**Tactical Lesson:** *Banishment* works but must be followed by immediate concentration protection. A banished Marilith that returns is forewarned and will prioritize the caster ([[Dossier 03-0378.5831.3478]]: "Kill 5: Hammerfalt and the Banishment Precedent").

**III. AGGREGATE ANALYSIS**  
Total confirmed kills by Mariliths across all named engagements: **23 named individuals** (5 detailed above, 18 documented in the full appendix). Of these:

| Category | Count |
|---|---|
| Military commanders (Colonel+) | 7 |
| Paladin/War-Priest leaders | 5 |
| Arcane spellcasters | 4 |
| Elite soldiers / special operators | 7 |

The disproportionate targeting of **leadership elements** is deliberate, Mariliths are deployed as command-elimination platforms. Their tactical intelligence allows them to identify and prioritize high-value targets on the battlefield ([[Dossier 03-0378.5831.4612]]: "Leadership Targeting Pattern, Statistical Analysis").

**IV. ANTI-MARILITH DOCTRINE**  
Based on the kill list analysis, the following tactical doctrine is recommended:

1. **Never engage alone.** Minimum 3 coordinated combatants against a single Marilith, attacking from multiple angles to overwhelm the six-sword defensive coverage.
2. **Suppress magic resistance.** Lead engagements with non-magical attacks (siege weapons, alchemical fire) that bypass magic resistance, then follow with magical attacks while the Marilith is occupied.
3. **Prevent pursuit.** Use *Wall of Force*, *Web*, or terrain modification to prevent Marilith from closing to melee range.
4. **Protect the caster.** If *Banishment* is attempted, the caster must be surrounded by a dedicated bodyguard element for the full 1-minute concentration duration.
5. **Decline honor challenges.** Standing order: no mortal combatant is to accept single combat with a Marilith under any circumstances.

([[Dossier 03-0378.5831.5738]]: "Anti-Marilith Doctrine: Recommended Engagement Protocols")
