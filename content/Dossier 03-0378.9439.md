**CLASSIFIED: FOR CELESTIAL EYES ONLY**  
**REF. 03-0378.9439**  
**Origin:** Ascendant Watch: Strategic Assessment Division  
**Subject:** Intelligence Gaps Assessment: Velvet Iron Army

---

**I. PURPOSE**  
This dossier catalogs the **critical intelligence gaps** in the Ascendant Watch's understanding of the Velvet Iron Army's capabilities, intentions, and order of battle. It is maintained as a living document, updated as gaps are confirmed or closed through ongoing collection operations.

---

**II. TIER 1 GAPS; CRITICAL (UNKNOWN AND OPERATIONALLY URGENT)**

### Gap 1.1: Total Force Strength
**What we know:** Estimates of 150,000 Dretches, 20,000-30,000 mid-tier fiends, 50 Balors. These figures are derived from battlefield counting, portal transit analysis ([[Dossier 03-0380.6152]]), and defector estimates.  
**What we don't know:** Actual total force strength. The Velvet Iron Army draws from Graz'zt's Abyssal layers, which have effectively infinite spawning capacity. Our estimates may undercount by a factor of 2-5.  
**Why it matters:** Force planning against an enemy of unknown size is inherently unreliable.

([[Dossier 03-0378.9439.1147]]: "Force Strength Gap: Estimation Methodology and Uncertainty Bounds")

### Gap 1.2: Graz'zt's Personal Involvement
**What we know:** Graz'zt authorized the material plane campaign and assigned Thraxxia and the Balor War Council as operational commanders.  
**What we don't know:** Whether Graz'zt intends to personally intervene. A Demon Prince's direct involvement would escalate the conflict beyond anything mortal or standard celestial forces could address. The detection threshold problem ([[Dossier 01-0001.11]]) cuts both ways; if Graz'zt enters the material plane, he would trigger a celestial response cascade, but the initial damage could be catastrophic.  
**Why it matters:** Campaign-ending variable.

### Gap 1.3: Soul Product Destination
**What we know:** Harvested souls are processed in Dûrn Kordrek ([[Dossier 19-3999.8638]]) then exported through portals.  
**What we don't know:** What happens to souls after export. They may be consumed by Graz'zt for personal power, used as currency in inter-planar trade, or stockpiled for a specific ritual purpose. Understanding the end-use is critical for assessing the entire campaign's strategic objective.  
**Why it matters:** If souls are being stockpiled for a ritual, there may be a **deadline**; and disrupting the stockpile before the deadline could negate the entire campaign's purpose.

([[Dossier 03-0378.9439.2301]]: "Soul Destination Gap: Collection Theories and Evidence Assessment")

**III. TIER 2 GAPS; SIGNIFICANT (PARTIALLY UNDERSTOOD, OPERATIONALLY IMPORTANT)**

### Gap 2.1: Reserve Force Locations
**What we know:** Front-line force dispositions on the Stuik front and occupied Ulbgar.  
**What we don't know:** Location and composition of strategic reserves. The Velvet Iron Army must maintain uncommitted reserves, but their staging areas have not been identified. They may be held in Abyssal layers and deployed via portal on demand.

### Gap 2.2: Yugoloth Contractual Specifics
**What we know:** Yugoloth auxiliaries serve under contract ([[Dossier 03-0378.6752]]). They provide Arcanaloth engineers and specialized technical support.  
**What we don't know:** The specific terms, duration, and termination conditions of the Yugoloth contract. If the contract includes a "force majeure" exit clause, sufficient military pressure might cause Yugoloth defection: removing the portal engineering capability entirely.

### Gap 2.3: Naval Capability
**What we know:** No infernal naval forces have been observed on the material plane.  
**What we don't know:** Whether the Velvet Iron Army possesses or is developing naval/maritime assets. The Suirx Trade Company Territories are coastal; a maritime threat vector is unmonitored.

([[Dossier 03-0378.9439.3478]]: "Tier 2 Gaps: Reserve Locations, Yugoloth Terms, Naval Capability")

**V. GAP CLOSURE PRIORITIES**  
The following collection operations are recommended to address the highest-priority gaps:

1. **Gap 1.3 (Soul Destination):** Requires an intelligence source within Graz'zt's court or the Triple Palace: currently no viable collection path. The Dûrn Kordrek forge raid may yield physical evidence (soul transit documentation).
2. **Gap 1.1 (Force Strength):** Portal transit analysis expansion ([[Dossier 03-0380.6152]]) and battlefield debriefs after major Stuik front engagements.
3. **Gap 2.2 (Yugoloth Terms):** Targeted Arcanaloth capture and interrogation: recommended over attrition killing when operationally feasible.

([[Dossier 03-0378.9439.5738]]: "Collection Plan: Gap Closure Operations and Resource Requirements")

---

**VI. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **Honest Assessment:** The Ascendant Watch's understanding of the Velvet Iron Army is **incomplete**. We have detailed tactical intelligence on specific operations and targets but lack strategic-level certainty about the enemy's total capabilities, ultimate objectives, and reserves.
2. **Risk:** Gap 1.2 (Graz'zt's involvement) is the most dangerous unknown. All planning assumptions presume Graz'zt will not personally intervene: an assumption that cannot be validated.
3. **Recommendation:** **[REDACTED]**: Accept that these gaps may not be fully closed before decisive action is required. Plan for worst-case scenarios on force strength and Graz'zt involvement. Prioritize Gap 1.3 (soul destination) as the most likely variable to reveal the campaign's strategic logic. **[REDACTED]**
