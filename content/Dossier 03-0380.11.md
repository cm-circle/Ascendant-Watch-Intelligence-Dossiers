**INFERNAL COMMAND REF. 03-0380.11**  
**Origin:** Ascendant Watch: Infernal Analysis Bureau  
**Subject:** Thraxxia (Daughter of Graz'zt), the Floating City of Ziragz, and the "Lawful Evil" Governance Model

---

**I. SUBJECT PROFILE; THRAXXIA**  
**Designation:** Thraxxia, the Gilt-Tongued  
**Classification:** Half-Fiend (Demon Lord Progeny); Estimated CR 19  
**Parentage:** Daughter of **Graz'zt**, the Dark Prince, Supreme Commander of the Velvet Iron Army ([[Dossier 03-0378.6541]]: "Unconfirmed Links to Graz'zt Command Structure")  
**Physical Description:** Thraxxia manifests as a tall, statuesque figure with obsidian skin, molten-gold eyes, and six fingers on each hand; a hereditary trait of Graz'zt's bloodline. She favors elaborate mortal-style courtly dress embroidered with living golden thread that shifts patterns in response to her emotional state.  
**Demeanor Assessment:** Unlike the majority of abyssal commanders, Thraxxia operates with calculated restraint, preferring manipulation, seduction, and contractual ensnarement over brute force. Analysts classify her as **the single most dangerous non-divine intelligence operative** on the material plane.

Psychological profiling ([[Dossier 03-0380.2479]]: "Thraxxia; Behavioral Pattern Analysis and Predictive Modeling") suggests Thraxxia views the material plane conquest not as a military campaign but as a **proof of concept**; demonstrating to her father that mortal populations can be governed as permanent, productive assets rather than consumed as disposable resources. This philosophical divergence from standard abyssal doctrine has created tension with other Velvet Iron Army commanders ([[Dossier 03-0380.4458]]: "Internal Friction; Thraxxia vs. Balor War Council").

---

**II. THE FLOATING CITY OF ZIRAGZ: "THE CITY OF ETERNAL GILT"**  
Ziragz is a **levitating urban complex** anchored approximately 1,500 meters above the ruins of the former city of **Seirvane**, which was destroyed during the early stages of the Velvet Iron Army's eastern campaign. The city is suspended by a network of arcane gravity-anchors maintained by a dedicated cadre of Arcanaloth engineers.

Physical characteristics:

- **Diameter:** Approximately 2 kilometers, built upon a disc of fused stone, metal, and crystallized abyssal energy.
- **Architecture:** A deliberate fusion of mortal and abyssal aesthetics: soaring spires of black marble veined with gold, open-air plazas lit by perpetual amber glow-lights, and cascading fountains that run with scented water. The city is designed to be **beautiful**, a calculated psychological tool.
- **Population:** Approximately **18,000 inhabitants**, of whom roughly 16,000 are mortal (predominantly human, with smaller communities of elves, halflings, and tieflings). The remaining 2,000 consist of fiendish administrators, guards, and service creatures.
- **Access:** The city is reachable only by flight or by four **arcane lift-columns** descending to the surface, each guarded by Vrock sentries and warded against unauthorized teleportation.

Infrastructure analysis ([[Dossier 03-0380.3127]]: "Ziragz, Urban Infrastructure and Logistical Assessment") reveals that the city is almost entirely self-sustaining: food is produced in enchanted hydroponic gardens, water is conjured and recycled through purification rituals, and waste is teleported to a designated dump-plane. Power is derived from a central **soul-furnace**, a contained apparatus that burns jade amulets ([[Dossier 19-3999.11]]) to produce arcane energy.

---

**III. THE "LAWFUL EVIL" GOVERNANCE MODEL**  
Ziragz represents an unprecedented experiment in **fiendish governance of mortal populations**. Thraxxia has implemented a social order that analysts have designated the **"Gilt Compact"**: a system that provides genuine material prosperity to its mortal citizens in exchange for absolute compliance with an authoritarian legal framework.

Key features of the Gilt Compact:

- **Zero Crime Rate:** Ziragz has achieved a literal zero reported crime rate. This is enforced through a combination of omnipresent surveillance (via bound Imps acting as watchers, [[Dossier 03-0380.6279]]: "Ziragz Surveillance Network, Imp Integration and Coverage Analysis"), immediate and disproportionate punishment for infractions, and the removal of material want as a criminal motivator.
- **Material Abundance:** Mortal citizens receive food, housing, and entertainment at no cost. Work is optional but incentivized through a tiered privilege system. The upper tiers enjoy private chambers, access to exclusive pleasure gardens, and personal attendants.
- **Contractual Citizenship:** Every mortal resident has signed a **Compact of Gilt**, a magically binding contract drafted in Infernal legalese. Surface-level terms include an agreement to obey city law, participate in community functions, and refrain from worship of deities hostile to "the Sovereign" (Thraxxia). However, embedded sub-clauses ([[Dossier 03-0380.9849]]: "Compact of Gilt, Full Legal Analysis and Hidden Provisions") include:
  - Irrevocable surrender of the signee's soul upon death.
  - Consent to memory modification for "civic harmony."
  - Waiver of the right to leave Ziragz without express permission.
- **Hedonism as Control:** The city encourages and provides for extreme sensory indulgence: feasting, intoxicants, entertainment, and carnal pleasures are readily available and culturally normalized. Analysts assess this as a deliberate pacification strategy; residents who are perpetually sated lack the motivation or clarity to question their circumstances.

The most alarming aspect of the Ziragz model is the **apparent contentment of its mortal population**. Debriefings of the small number of individuals who have escaped or been extracted ([[Dossier 03-0380.9735]]: "Ziragz Defector Debriefs: Psychological Assessment and Testimony") reveal that the majority of residents genuinely believe they are better off under Thraxxia's rule than in the war-ravaged mortal world. Several defectors expressed **reluctance to leave** and required extensive counseling.

---

**IV. STRATEGIC ROLE IN THE VELVET IRON ARMY**  
Thraxxia serves as Graz'zt's primary **intelligence coordinator and political warfare officer** on the material plane. Her responsibilities include:

1. **Corruption Operations:** Thraxxia's agents are responsible for the institutional subversion campaigns in the Preanese Theocracy ([[Dossier 19-3611.11]]), the destabilization of Silesbia ([[Dossier 19-3500.11]]), and the bribery networks sustained by jade amulet distribution.
2. **Portal Network Management:** Ziragz serves as a central hub for the Velvet Iron Army's portal logistics, receiving and redistributing jade amulets, personnel, and intelligence ([[Dossier 03-0380.6152]]: "Ziragz Portal Activity: Traffic Analysis and Node Mapping").
3. **Propaganda and Recruitment:** Thraxxia actively recruits mortal defectors by offering safe passage to Ziragz. Captured intelligence ([[Dossier 03-0380.6922]]: "Recruitment Pamphlets: Intercepted Materials and Distribution Analysis") includes beautifully illustrated pamphlets circulated in refugee camps, promising "peace, plenty, and protection" under Thraxxia's patronage.
4. **Father's Proxy:** Thraxxia maintains regular communication with Graz'zt through means that have not been successfully intercepted. She is believed to hold significant autonomy in operational decisions but defers to Graz'zt on strategic-level matters.

---

**V. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **Threat Level:** **Critical.** Thraxxia is not merely a military threat: she is an ideological one. Ziragz proves that fiendish rule can produce superficially desirable outcomes for mortal populations, undermining the moral foundation of resistance.
2. **Military Vulnerability:** Ziragz's levitation is dependent on its gravity-anchor network. Disruption of even two of the four primary anchors would cause catastrophic destabilization. However, the anchors are located within the city itself, heavily guarded, and warded against tampering ([[Dossier 03-0380.6152]]).
3. **Political Vulnerability:** Thraxxia's relationship with the Balor war council is fractious. Her preference for "soft conquest" is viewed with contempt by the military wing, which favors annihilation. Exploitation of this rift may be possible.
4. **Recommendation:** **[REDACTED]**: Under no circumstances should Ziragz be treated as a legitimate governance model or its population as willing collaborators. Priority: develop countermeasures to the Compact of Gilt contract structure. Secondary: identify and exploit the Thraxxia-Balor factional divide. Tertiary: prevent the Ziragz model from being replicated in other conquered territories. **[REDACTED]**
