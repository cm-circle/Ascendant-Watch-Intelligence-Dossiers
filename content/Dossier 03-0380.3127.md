**REF. 03-0380.3127**  
**Origin:** Ascendant Watch: Urban Intelligence Division  
**Subject:** Ziragz: Urban Infrastructure and Logistical Assessment

---

**I. OVERVIEW**  
Ziragz is a **floating city** suspended approximately 300 meters above the surface of a corrupted landscape in Graz'zt's Abyssal layer. It houses a population of approximately **16,000 mortals** alongside an estimated 2,000 fiendish administrators, guards, and support staff. This dossier documents the city's physical infrastructure, logistical systems, and structural vulnerabilities.

---

**II. PHYSICAL STRUCTURE**

### A. Platform
Ziragz occupies a disc-shaped stone platform approximately 2 kilometers in diameter, suspended through a combination of Abyssal gravity manipulation and permanent *Levitate* field enchantments anchored to buried pylons within the platform's bedrock core. The platform's surface is relatively flat, with artificial elevation variations created through constructed terracing.

The platform's stone matches no known geological source; it is assessed as having been **grown** or shaped through sustained transmutation magic rather than quarried. Edge walls rise approximately 3 meters above the platform surface, preventing accidental falls but not impeding aerial approach.

([[Dossier 03-0380.3127.1108]]: "Platform Analysis: Material Composition and Levitation System")

### B. Urban Layout
Ziragz is organized into four radial districts:

1. **The Gilt Quarter (Center):** Thraxxia's administrative complex: a tower of black glass approximately 40 meters tall, flanked by government offices, court buildings, and the Compact registry. This is the only area where fiendish officials and mortal citizens interact in formal administrative capacity.
2. **The Mortal Quarter (North-Northwest):** Residential and commercial area for the mortal population. Approximately 60% of the platform's surface area. Contains multi-story residential blocks, markets, a temple to Waukeen (goddess of trade: Thraxxia permits worship of deities associated with commerce), workshops, and public gardens.
3. **The Garrison Quarter (South):** Barracks for the Mezzoloth and Cambion security forces. Training grounds, armories, and portal staging areas. Mortals are prohibited from this district.
4. **The Processing Quarter (East):** The jade amulet distribution center, soul-processing facilities, and trade warehouses. Mortal access is restricted to authorized workers.

([[Dossier 03-0380.3127.2247]]: "Urban Layout: District Map and Access Restrictions")

### C. Infrastructure Systems
- **Water:** Magically generated through permanent *Create Water* installations. Distribution via a gravity-fed pipe system served by elevated reservoirs on the platform's highest terrace.
- **Food:** Imported through portals, agricultural produce from conquered territories and trade goods purchased through intermediaries on the material plane. Ziragz produces no food internally. This represents a **critical logistical vulnerability**, portal disruption would create a food crisis within 2 weeks.
- **Waste:** Discharged over the platform edge. The corrupted landscape below serves as an unlimited disposal zone.
- **Lighting:** Permanent *Daylight* enchantments mounted on 30-meter poles throughout the Mortal Quarter, creating an artificial day-night cycle. The cycle is set to 14 hours of light and 10 hours of dimmed "night": described by defectors as "close enough to feel normal, wrong enough to never forget where you are."

([[Dossier 03-0380.3127.3389]]: "Infrastructure Assessment: Water, Food, Waste, and Lighting Systems")

---

**III. PORTAL INFRASTRUCTURE**  
Ziragz contains **four stabilized portals**; the highest concentration of portal infrastructure observed in any single location:

| Portal | Class | Primary Function | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Central Gate | IV | Direct link to Graz'zt's court | Restricted; Thraxxia and senior staff only |
| Commerce Gate | III | Trade and supply importation | Active; daily use |
| Military Gate | III | Garrison rotation and force deployment | Active; controlled by Garrison Quarter |
| Processing Gate | III | Soul-harvest product export and trafficking intake | Active; controlled by Processing Quarter |

The Commerce Gate is the city's lifeline; all food, raw materials, and trade goods transit through it. Its destruction would cripple Ziragz within days.

([[Dossier 03-0380.3127.4534]]: "Portal Infrastructure: Hub Assessment and Vulnerability Mapping")

---

**IV. DEFENSIVE CAPABILITIES**

- **Aerial Defense:** 200+ Vrock garrison capable of intercepted aerial approach. The 300-meter altitude gap between the platform and the surface creates a natural kill zone for ground-based attackers.
- **Anti-Scrying Wards:** The entire platform is covered by a sophisticated anti-divination field that prevents remote observation. Ascendant Watch intelligence on Ziragz's interior comes exclusively from defectors and pre-defection planted observers.
- **Imp Surveillance Network:** Approximately 500 imps serve as a distributed surveillance system, monitoring all mortal activity and reporting anomalies to Cambion security officers ([[Dossier 03-0380.6279]]).
- **Thraxxia Personal Guard:** A cadre of 20 elite Cambions with enhanced sorcerous abilities, stationed in the Gilt Quarter. These individuals are fanatics: selected for loyalty above combat capability.

([[Dossier 03-0380.3127.5671]]: "Defensive Capabilities: Layered Defense Assessment")

---

**V. VULNERABILITIES**

1. **Food dependency:** Ziragz cannot feed itself. Commerce Gate disruption = famine in 2 weeks.
2. **Levitation anchors:** The levitation system depends on buried pylons. If even two of the four pylons were destroyed, the platform would tilt catastrophically.
3. **Anti-scrying limitations:** The wards prevent divination but cannot prevent physical infiltration: particularly by entities small enough to evade the imp network.
4. **Mortal population morale:** Despite Thraxxia's governance, the mortal population is not genuinely content. They are compliant because the alternative, the Abyss outside, is worse. A credible rescue offer could trigger mass defection.

([[Dossier 03-0380.3127.6824]]: "Vulnerability Assessment: Priority Targets and Exploitation Scenarios")

---

**VI. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **Intelligence Priority:** Ziragz is the nerve center of Thraxxia's operations. Understanding its internal dynamics is essential for counter-strategy.
2. **Attack Feasibility:** A direct assault on Ziragz is currently impossible with available resources. However, the food dependency and levitation pylon vulnerabilities offer asymmetric approaches.
3. **Recommendation:** **[REDACTED]**: Maintain intelligence collection through defectors and covert observation. Develop contingency plans for Commerce Gate disruption and levitation pylon sabotage. Do not reveal intelligence on Ziragz's internal layout to mortal allies without operational necessity. **[REDACTED]**
