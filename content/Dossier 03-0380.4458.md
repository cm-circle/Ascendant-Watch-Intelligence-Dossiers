**CLASSIFIED: EMPYREAN CLEARANCE ONLY**  
**REF. 03-0380.4458**  
**Origin:** Ascendant Watch: Infernal Command Analysis  
**Subject:** Internal Friction: Thraxxia vs. the Balor War Council

---

**I. OVERVIEW**  
The Velvet Iron Army's command structure is divided between two irreconcilable strategic philosophies, embodied by the conflict between **Thraxxia** (Governor-General of Ziragz, strategic subversion) and the **Balor War Council** (50 Balors commanding the military wing, led de facto by Ruin-Tongue; [[Dossier 19-3999.3178]]). This internal friction represents the single most significant vulnerability in the enemy's command structure.

---

**II. THE PHILOSOPHICAL DIVIDE**

### Thraxxia's Position: "The Velvet Doctrine"
Thraxxia advocates for the gradual absorption of mortal territories through subversion, economic manipulation, and governance replacement. Her model, tested at Ziragz ([[Dossier 03-0380.11]]), holds that mortal populations are more productive as willing subjects than as conquered slaves. The jade amulet system ([[Dossier 19-3999.9275]]) is her weapon of choice: soft, pervasive, and self-sustaining.

Key tenets:
- Armed conflict is expensive and wasteful
- Mortal institutions can be captured intact and repurposed
- Sustainable soul harvesting requires living, productive populations
- The Compact of Gilt is a superior governance model to military occupation

### The Balor Position: "The Iron Doctrine"
The Balor War Council views Thraxxia's approach with open contempt. Invoking the traditional Abyssal paradigm, the Balors advocate for overwhelming military force followed by total subjugation. Their model has been validated by Ulbgar's rapid conquest (11 days; [[Dossier 19-3999.11]]).

Key tenets:
- Mortals are resources to be consumed, not cultivated
- Diplomacy and subversion are signs of weakness
- The Abyssal way is conquest through terror
- Thraxxia's "experiment" is a dangerous distraction from the campaign's objectives

---

**III. DOCUMENTED INCIDENTS OF FRICTION**

### Incident 1: The Ulbgar Debate (Pre-Invasion)
Before the Ulbgar invasion, Thraxxia advocated for a 2-year subversion campaign targeting the dwarven kingdom's trade networks and troll vassal relationships. The Balor council overruled her and insisted on immediate military assault. The successful 11-day campaign was cited by the Balors as vindication of their doctrine; and by Thraxxia as a waste of potential long-term resources (the Ulbgar forges could have been captured intact with their entire workforce, rather than requiring forced labor of survivors).

Intercepted argument fragment (attributed to Thraxxia addressing Ruin-Tongue):

> *"You conquered a kingdom in eleven days. Impressive. And now you have a ruin. I could have delivered a functioning kingdom in two years: forges running, mines producing, population compliant. Your way gives us rubble and resentment. My way gives us an empire."*

([[Dossier 03-0380.4458.1147]]: "Ulbgar Debate: Reconstructed Arguments and Strategic Assessment")

### Incident 2: The Ziragz Population Crisis
Six months ago, Ruin-Tongue dispatched a raiding force to Ziragz's mortal quarter to "requisition" 300 civilians for forced labor in the Ulbgar forges. Thraxxia's Cambion guard **turned the Balor's troops away at the gates**: a direct military confrontation between two wings of the same army. Ruin-Tongue was reportedly incandescent with rage but did not escalate to force, suggesting either a directive from Graz'zt or a calculation that open civil war would be premature.

The 300 laborers were eventually sourced from new trafficking operations ([[Dossier 01-0007.4892]]), but the incident established that **Thraxxia will use force to defend Ziragz**; even against nominal allies.

([[Dossier 03-0380.4458.2301]]: "Ziragz Gate Incident: Witness Accounts and Force Disposition")

### Incident 3: Portal Allocation Dispute
The Balor War Council requested allocation of three portals for military troop rotation on the Stuik front. Thraxxia controls the Arcanaloth engineers who build and maintain portals ([[Dossier 03-0378.11]]) and refused, citing "logistical constraints." In reality, the portals were allocated to her trafficking and subversion operations (Stios, Halvek, and one unidentified site: [[Dossier 01-0007.4737]]).

The Balors have subsequently attempted to recruit or coerce Arcanaloths directly, with limited success; the Yugoloths' mercenary nature means they follow the contract, and Thraxxia holds the contracts.

([[Dossier 03-0380.4458.3478]]: "Portal Allocation Dispute: Resource Control and Leverage Analysis")

---

**IV. COMMAND AUTHORITY AMBIGUITY**  
The friction persists because **no clear chain of command** exists between Thraxxia and the Balor War Council. Both report to Graz'zt, but Graz'zt's directives are infrequent and often ambiguous; deliberately so, as Graz'zt appears to encourage competition between his subordinates.

In practice:
- Thraxxia controls: Intelligence operations, portal infrastructure, Arcanaloth engineers, Cambion administrative cadre, Ziragz, and all subversion/trafficking operations.
- The Balor War Council controls: Military forces (Dretches through Balors), frontline combat operations, conquered territory occupation (Ulbgar).
- **Contested:** Resource allocation (souls, materials, portals), strategic priority setting, and the ultimate question of whether the campaign's objective is conquest or subversion.

([[Dossier 03-0380.4458.4612]]: "Command Authority Mapping: Contested Domains and Decision Rights")

---

**V. ESCALATION TRAJECTORY**  
Analysis of the friction trajectory suggests:

- **Short-term (0-3 months):** Continued passive-aggressive resource competition. No open conflict.
- **Medium-term (3-6 months):** If the Stuik front stalls or another military setback occurs, the Balors will escalate demands for portal access and resource reallocation. Thraxxia will resist.
- **Long-term (6-12 months):** If Thraxxia's Phase Gilt succeeds in Preas, her power within the command structure will significantly exceed the Balors'. This could trigger a violent attempt by the Balors to reassert dominance: potentially including an assault on Ziragz itself.

The single greatest variable is **Graz'zt's intervention**. If the Dark Prince issues a clear directive favoring one side, the friction resolves immediately. His continued silence suggests he views the competition as productive; or is occupied elsewhere.

([[Dossier 03-0380.4458.5738]]: "Escalation Modeling: Scenarios and Intervention Variables")

---

**VI. EXPLOITATION OPPORTUNITIES**

1. **Feed the fire:** Ascendant Watch disinformation could amplify the friction: false intelligence suggesting Thraxxia is negotiating independently with celestial forces, or that the Balors are planning a coup against her authority. Both sides are predisposed to believe the worst of each other.
2. **Force a choice:** Military pressure on the Stuik front that forces the Balor War Council to demand portal access creates a concrete flash point. If Thraxxia refuses under pressure, the resulting confrontation could produce open schism.
3. **Target the bridge:** The few entities that maintain dialogue between both factions, primarily Arcanaloth intermediaries and Graz'zt's personal emissaries, could be targeted. Removing these connective nodes accelerates the fragmentation.

([[Dossier 03-0380.4458.6893]]: "Exploitation Playbook: Disinformation, Provocation, and Bridge Targeting")

---

**VII. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **Vulnerability:** The Thraxxia-Balor friction is the most exploitable vulnerability in the Velvet Iron Army's command structure. A fractured enemy fights at reduced effectiveness.
2. **Caution:** A successful schism could also produce two independently operating enemy factions: which may be harder to manage than a single conflicted one.
3. **Recommendation:** **[REDACTED]**: Pursue controlled escalation of the friction through disinformation and military pressure. Do not attempt to trigger a full schism until the Ascendant Watch and its mortal allies are positioned to exploit the resulting chaos. **[REDACTED]**
