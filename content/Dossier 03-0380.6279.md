**CLASSIFIED: EMPYREAN CLEARANCE ONLY**  
**REF. 03-0380.6279**  
**Origin:** Ascendant Watch: Surveillance Countermeasures Unit  
**Subject:** Ziragz Imp Surveillance Network: Integration, Coverage Analysis, and Evasion Protocols

---

**I. NETWORK OVERVIEW**  
Thraxxia maintains a distributed surveillance system within Ziragz consisting of approximately **500 imps**; small, winged fiends (CR 1) with innate invisibility and shape-changing capabilities. The imps serve as a living security camera network, observing mortal activity and reporting anomalies to Cambion security officers in the Garrison Quarter.

This network is Thraxxia's primary internal security mechanism and the chief obstacle to intelligence operations within Ziragz.

---

**II. OPERATIONAL STRUCTURE**

### A. Deployment
The 500 imps are organized into **10 flights** of 50, each assigned to a geographic sector of the Mortal Quarter. Each flight is commanded by a senior imp designated a **Flight-Eye** who reports directly to a Cambion security officer. Flight-Eyes coordinate patrol routes, rest rotations, and priority surveillance assignments.

### B. Patrol Patterns
Imp patrols operate on a continuous **rolling coverage** model; at any given time, approximately 300 imps (60%) are actively deployed, with 200 in rest rotation. Active imps maintain invisibility and circulate through their assigned sectors at irregular intervals, observing through windows, eavesdropping on conversations, and monitoring public gatherings.

The pattern is deliberately randomized, defectors report that patrol routes change daily and individual imps are reassigned between sectors weekly to prevent predictability ([[Dossier 03-0380.6279.1108]]: "Patrol Pattern Analysis, Randomization Model and Residual Predictability").

### C. Reporting
Imps report via two channels:
- **Routine:** Daily oral debriefs to Flight-Eyes at dawn and dusk. Imps describe observed activities, conversations, and behavioral anomalies.
- **Emergency:** Imps can transmit immediate telepathic alerts to their assigned Cambion handler when observing suspicious activity. The telepathic link has a range of approximately 1 kilometer: sufficient to cover the entire platform.

([[Dossier 03-0380.6279.2247]]: "Reporting Channels: Routine and Emergency Protocols")

---

**III. COVERAGE ANALYSIS**

### Strengths:
- **Ubiquity:** With 300 active imps covering a Mortal Quarter of approximately 1.2 sq km, the density is approximately 250 imps per square kilometer.
- **Invisibility:** Imps' natural invisibility makes counter-surveillance by mortals nearly impossible without magical detection.
- **Shape-changing:** Imps can assume animal forms (rat, raven, spider). In animal form they can observe from positions where an invisible humanoid form would be impractical: inside walls, under furniture, on ceilings.

### Weaknesses:
- **Cognitive limits:** Imps are low-intelligence creatures (INT 11). They can observe and report but cannot analyze. They frequently report irrelevant information (marital disputes, petty theft) while missing subtle indicators of organized resistance or intelligence activity.
- **Boredom attrition:** Defectors report that over time, imp patrol discipline degrades, particularly during night shifts. After months of observing routine mortal behavior, imps become inattentive, lingering in entertainment-rich areas (taverns, markets) while neglecting residential blocks ([[Dossier 03-0380.6279.3389]]: "Discipline Degradation, Attention Deficit Patterns and Temporal Analysis").
- **Detection vulnerability:** *See Invisibility* and *Detect Evil and Good* spells can identify imp observers. However, casting such spells within Ziragz would itself attract attention from the imp network: a circular detection problem.
- **Cold sensitivity:** Imps have fire resistance but no cold resistance. Defectors report that imps avoid cold environments: specifically, rooms where *Create Water* runoff has created cold stone surfaces. At least one defector ([[Dossier 03-0380.9735]]) used this knowledge to create a "cold room" for secure conversations.

---

**IV. DOCUMENTED INCIDENTS**  
The imp network has successfully identified and prevented at least three known resistance actions:

| Incident | Detection Method | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Escape tunnel construction (6 months ago) | Imp in rat form observed excavation debris | 12 mortals arrested, tunnel collapsed |
| Unauthorized religious ceremony to Tyr (4 months ago) | Imp eavesdropped on planning conversation | Ceremony disrupted, cleric imprisoned |
| Attempted theft of portal key (2 months ago) | Imp observed suspicious behavior near Garrison Quarter | Thief executed publicly |

The network has also produced **17 false positives** in the same period, innocent activities misidentified as suspicious by cognitively limited imps, resulting in unwarranted interrogations and civilian harassment. This has increased mortal resentment and reduced trust in the governance system ([[Dossier 03-0380.6279.4534]]: "False Positive Incidents, Catalog and Governance Impact").

---

**V. EVASION PROTOCOLS**  
Based on defector intelligence and analysis of the network's weaknesses, the following evasion protocols have been developed for Ascendant Watch operatives operating within Ziragz:

1. **Cold room technique:** Conduct sensitive conversations in rooms with sustained cold temperatures (below 10°C). Imps will actively avoid these spaces.
2. **Crowd masking:** Conduct activities in densely populated areas where imp observation density is diluted by the volume of targets.
3. **Night-shift exploitation:** Sensitive activities should be timed to the early-morning hours (0200-0500 Ziragz time) when imp attention is lowest.
4. **Noise discipline:** Imps rely heavily on eavesdropping. Written communication, hand signals, and pre-arranged codes are more secure than spoken conversation.
5. **Animal awareness:** Treat all animals within Ziragz as potential imp observers: rats, ravens, and spiders should be assumed hostile.

([[Dossier 03-0380.6279.5671]]: "Evasion Protocols: Standard Operating Procedures for Ziragz Operations")

---

**VI. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **Effectiveness:** The imp network is a crude but effective surveillance tool. It compensates for low individual intelligence with overwhelming numbers.
2. **Exploitability:** The network's weaknesses, boredom, cognitive limits, cold avoidance, are real and exploitable, but any exploitation must be subtle enough to avoid triggering the emergency telepathic alert system.
3. **Recommendation:** **[REDACTED]**: Develop a specialized counter-surveillance training program for any operatives slated for Ziragz deployment. Include cold room construction, crowd masking timing, and animal threat awareness. **[REDACTED]**
