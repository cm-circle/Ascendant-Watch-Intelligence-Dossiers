**CLASSIFIED: EMPYREAN CLEARANCE ONLY**  
**REF. 03-0380.6922**  
**Origin:** Ascendant Watch: Information Warfare Analysis  
**Subject:** Recruitment Pamphlets: Intercepted Materials and Distribution Analysis

---

**I. COLLECTION SUMMARY**  
Over the past six months, the Ascendant Watch has intercepted **27 distinct recruitment pamphlets** attributed to Thraxxia's Ziragz recruitment operation. These pamphlets target displaced populations, specifically refugees from Ulbgar, the Stuik frontline zones, and the Suirx territorial camps, with promises of safety, employment, and governance under the Compact of Gilt ([[Dossier 03-0380.9849]]).

Pamphlets have been recovered from:
- Silesbian refugee screening points (6 specimens)
- Stuik garrison towns (8 specimens)
- Suirx territorial refugee camps (9 specimens)
- Traveling merchant inventories (4 specimens)

---

**II. PAMPHLET CATEGORIES AND ANALYSIS**

### Category A: "The Haven" (12 specimens)
**Target Audience:** Desperate refugees, particularly families  
**Format:** Single-sheet, illustrated, printed on high-quality paper with copper-ink highlights  
**Key Messaging:**

- *"You have fled war. You deserve peace."*
- *"A city where no demon walks your streets."*
- *"Housing, food, and honest work: guaranteed by contract."*
- Illustrations depict clean streets, smiling families, and well-stocked market stalls. The imagery is **professionally produced**: comparable in quality to the Ironist wall posters ([[Dossier 19-3500.3417]]), suggesting a shared design source.

**Omissions:** No mention of jade amulets, soul harvesting, imp surveillance, or the fact that the city is located in the Abyss.

**Analysis:** These pamphlets are psychological precision instruments. They address the three core anxieties of displaced populations; physical safety, food security, and purposeful employment; with specific, contractual promises. The use of the word "contract" is deliberate: it implies legal protection and voluntariness ([[Dossier 03-0380.6922.1147]]: "Haven Pamphlets; Psychological Targeting Analysis and Messaging Effectiveness").

### Category B: "The Opportunity" (8 specimens)
**Target Audience:** Skilled laborers and artisans  
**Format:** Tri-fold brochure, text-heavy, no illustrations  
**Key Messaging:**

- *"Your skills are valued. Your expertise is needed."*
- *"Smiths, builders, healers, teachers: all are welcome."*
- *"Competitive wages. Advancement based on merit."*
- Specific job listings with stated compensation rates: rates that exceed current market wages in refugee areas by approximately 50%.

**Analysis:** These pamphlets target the economically motivated rather than the emotionally desperate. The absence of illustrations creates a more "professional" aesthetic intended to appeal to skilled workers who might be skeptical of overtly emotional appeals ([[Dossier 03-0380.6922.2301]]: "Opportunity Pamphlets: Economic Targeting and Compensation Comparison").

### Category C: "The Warning" (7 specimens)
**Target Audience:** Communities where other recruitment has already occurred; creating secondary recruitment pressure  
**Format:** Handbill, stark text, no decorative elements  
**Key Messaging:**

- *"The war is coming. Your walls will not hold."*
- *"Your neighbors have already chosen safety. Will you wait until it's too late?"*
- *"[City/Settlement Name] has X days of food remaining."*: The food supply figures inserted into these pamphlets are **accurate**, demonstrating access to current intelligence about specific settlement logistics.

**Analysis:** These pamphlets employ fear rather than hope, a deliberate escalation for communities resistant to positive messaging. The inclusion of accurate food supply data is alarming because it demonstrates real-time intelligence access within targeted settlements ([[Dossier 03-0380.6922.3478]]: "Warning Pamphlets, Fear-Based Messaging and Intelligence Sourcing").

**III. DISTRIBUTION NETWORK**  
Pamphlet distribution operates through three identified channels:

1. **Merchant Intermediaries:** Traveling merchants, possibly knowing, possibly coerced, carry pamphlets as part of their trade goods and distribute them in refugee gathering points. Payment is in gold.
2. **Planted Refugees:** Individuals who present themselves as "Ziragz returnees": claiming to have visited the city and returned voluntarily to share their positive experience. These individuals are either fiendish agents in disguise or genuinely returned individuals who have been instructed and incentivized to recruit.
3. **Drop Points:** Pamphlets are left in public locations, temple noticeboards, tavern countertops, latrine walls, for organic discovery and word-of-mouth spread.

([[Dossier 03-0380.6922.4612]]: "Distribution Channels: Merchant, Planted Refugee, and Drop Point Networks")

---

**IV. RECRUITMENT PIPELINE**  
The end-to-end recruitment pipeline from pamphlet to Ziragz residency:

1. **Awareness:** Pamphlet discovery (organic or planted)
2. **Interest:** Targeted individual contacts a recruitment agent: typically found at a designated tavern or market stall in the settlement
3. **Interview:** Recruitment agent assesses the individual's skills, family size, and social connections. Individuals with minimal social ties are preferred.
4. **Transit:** Accepted recruits are guided to a portal transit point. Previous transit through Stios; current transit points unknown ([[Dossier 01-0007.4737]]).
5. **Arrival:** Recruits arrive in Ziragz, are assigned housing, are presented with the Compact of Gilt for signature, and are issued their identification medallion.

Estimated pipeline throughput: **80-120 new residents per month** ([[Dossier 03-0380.6922.5738]]: "Recruitment Pipeline: Stage Model and Throughput Estimates").

---

**V. COUNTER-RECRUITMENT STRATEGY**

### Current Actions:
- Pamphlets are confiscated when discovered at allied screening points.
- Lantern scouts monitor known distribution points.
- Defector testimony ([[Dossier 03-0380.9735]]) has been compiled into a counter-narrative briefing for distribution to refugee camp administrators.

### Proposed Actions:
1. **Counter-pamphlets:** Produce and distribute materials that expose the Compact's hidden provisions, and the true nature of Ziragz. Must be equally well-produced to compete with Thraxxia's professional design quality.
2. **Planted returnees: counter-version:** Debrief actual Ziragz defectors and, with their consent, deploy them as "anti-recruitment" witnesses in targeted refugee camps.
3. **Merchant network disruption:** Identify and interdict the merchant intermediaries distributing pamphlets. This requires cooperation from territorial trade authorities.

([[Dossier 03-0380.6922.6893]]: "Counter-Recruitment Operations: Proposed Actions and Resource Requirements")

---

**VI. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **Professional Operation:** Thraxxia's recruitment campaign is the most sophisticated mortal-targeted propaganda operation ever observed from an Abyssal entity. It demonstrates an understanding of mortal psychology that exceeds any known fiendish precedent.
2. **Ongoing Threat:** At 80-120 recruits per month, the pipeline feeds Ziragz approximately 1,000-1,400 new souls per year. Each recruit represents both a productive worker and an eventual soul-harvesting yield.
3. **Recommendation:** **[REDACTED]**: Fund and deploy counter-recruitment operations in the three highest-intake refugee populations: Ossward (Silesbia), the Stuik frontier camps, and the Suirx territorial camps. Priority: prevent new intake while planning for Ziragz liberation. **[REDACTED]**
