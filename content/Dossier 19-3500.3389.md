**II. KEY INTERROGATION EXCERPTS**

### Detainee C-1: Lieutenant Fenris Kae
Kae is assessed as the **operational leader** of the armed assault element. He is a disciplined individual who provided minimal information during initial sessions. Extended interrogation produced the following:

**MARR:** Who gave you the command key override sequence for the blast doors?

**KAE:** It's my key. I was duty officer that night. I volunteered for the rotation six weeks in advance.

**MARR:** Six weeks. And the weapons? Starite-edged weapons are not standard Palace Guard issue.

**KAE:** [Silence; 14 seconds] They were provided.

**MARR:** By whom?

**KAE:** By Lord Telvaine's intermediary. A man. I don't know his real name. He called himself "Auric." He met us twice, once to deliver the weapons, once to confirm the operational plan ([[Dossier 19-3500.3389.1134]]: "Intermediary 'Auric', Composite Description and Identification Efforts").

**MARR:** Was Lord Telvaine present at either meeting?

**KAE:** No. Telvaine communicated through sealed letters delivered by Auric. The letters were... odd. They were written on a material I hadn't seen before. Not paper, not parchment. Thin, smooth, slightly warm to the touch. The ink glowed faintly in the dark.

*[Analyst's note: The described material is consistent with **abyssal vellum**, a writing medium crafted from chemically treated fiendish skin, commonly used in Thraxxia's intelligence correspondence. [[Dossier 19-3611.5573]], see Cipher Beta documentation materials.]*

---

### Detainee C-2: Sergeant Halvard Strunn
Strunn is the most cooperative detainee; a career soldier who appears to be experiencing genuine remorse. He provided the most detailed account of recruitment.

**STRUNN:** It started in the taverns. Not political talk: just complaining. Everyone complains. The war is burning the world and we sit in our hole and polish helmets. Then Lord Telvaine's people started... listening. Buying rounds. Agreeing. Saying things like "the Reagent can't keep the gate sealed forever" and "someone needs to act."

**MARR:** When did it escalate from talk to action?

**STRUNN:** About four months before the Ball. Telvaine; through Auric; offered us weapons and a plan. He said the Reagent needed to be removed. Not killed; removed. Put aside. He said there would be a council of war to replace him. I believed him.

**MARR:** But the plan included poison.

**STRUNN:** [Agitated] I didn't know about the poison. None of us did. The poison was separate. Auric told us the Reagent would be "indisposed" by the time we entered the Gallery. He said our job was to secure the room and arrest the Shieldwall Caucus members. When we breached and found the Reagent alive and the room in chaos, I knew we'd been lied to. We were the distraction. We were meant to die ([[Dossier 19-3500.3389.2278]]: "Strunn Full Testimony: Recruitment Arc and Realization Narrative").

---

### Detainee C-6: Private Jori Blackthorn
Blackthorn is the youngest and most junior conspirator. His interrogation provides insight into the Ironist radicalization pipeline.

**BLACKTHORN:** I joined the Guard because I wanted to fight demons. That's what Guards are supposed to do. Instead, I guard doors and carry messages. My cousin died in Ulbgar; fled to the gates and they let him in and he died in the refugee camp a month later. From a fever. Because there wasn't enough medicine. I asked my sergeant why we weren't helping. He said it wasn't our war.

**MARR:** And then?

**BLACKTHORN:** Then Sergeant Strunn told me there were people who wanted to change things. People who wanted us to fight. I didn't know about the killing part until the night of. I thought we were just... making a statement. I was the lookout. I didn't even draw my weapon.

*[Assessment: Blackthorn is a radicalized idealist, not a professional conspirator. His recruitment follows the standard disillusionment-to-radicalization pathway documented in Ironist Faction analysis; [[Dossier 19-3500.3417.]]]*

**III. ANALYSIS; THE TWO-TRACK OPERATION**  
Cross-referencing all six testimonies with Brother Aldren's deposition ([[Dossier 19-3500.8964]]) confirms that the Solstice Ball attack was a **two-track operation**:

- **Track One (Poison):** Administered by the unidentified wine steward: an infernal agent operating independently of the six guards. The guards were unaware of this track.
- **Track Two (Armed Assault):** The six guards: recruited through Ironist ideology and supplied through Lord Telvaine's "Auric" intermediary. Their role was either (a) to serve as secondary insurance if the poison failed, or (b) to provide a **plausible domestic terrorism narrative** that would obscure the infernal connection.

The conspirators were, in operational terms, **expendable pawns** in a compartmentalized infernal operation. They believed they were participating in a political coup; they were actually providing cover for an assassination ([[Dossier 19-3500.3389.3412]]: "Dual-Track Analysis: Operational Compartmentalization and Expendable Asset Doctrine").

---

**IV. LORD TELVAINE; INTELLIGENCE DERIVED FROM INTERROGATIONS**

Telvaine's involvement is confirmed through all six testimonies. Key intelligence:

- Telvaine operated through a single intermediary ("Auric") and never met the conspirators directly.
- Telvaine provided **starite-edged weapons**: suggesting access to Silesbian military-grade equipment, potentially through his noble network or Deep Watch contacts.
- Telvaine departed Grauven approximately 2 hours before the Solstice Ball: suggesting advance knowledge that the operation would occur.
- Telvaine's current location is unknown. Last reported sighting: the Greyloch Forest, heading eastward ([[Dossier 19-3500.3389.4567]]: "Telvaine: Last Known Movement and Flight Route Analysis").

The intermediary "Auric" has not been identified. Composite descriptions suggest a human male, 40-50, with military bearing and a faint eastern accent. If the abyssal vellum evidence is accurate, "Auric" may be a mortal agent of Thraxxia's intelligence network or a disguised fiend ([[Dossier 19-3500.3389.5693]]: "Intermediary Auric: Identification Priority Assessment").

**VI. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **Confirmed Infernal Connection:** The abyssal vellum, the separated poison track, and the supply of weapons through Telvaine's externally-supported network confirm that the Solstice Ball attack was not a simple domestic insurrection: it was an infernally-supported operation.
2. **Ironist Exploitation:** The Ironist movement has been weaponized by infernal interests without the knowledge of its rank-and-file members. This makes the movement simultaneously dangerous and potentially salvageable: with the right leadership, their martial energy could be redirected against the actual enemy.
