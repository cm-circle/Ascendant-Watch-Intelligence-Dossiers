**I. OVERVIEW**  
The **Ironist Faction** is a political movement within Silesbia advocating for the termination of Reagent Lunor's pacifist policies and the full military mobilization of Silesbian forces against the Velvet Iron Army. Since the Solstice Ball incident ([[Dossier 19-3500.11]]), the Ironists have transitioned from a legitimate political opposition into a semi-clandestine movement operating through propaganda, recruitment, and increasingly organized cells within the military and civilian population.

This dossier catalogs and analyzes the Ironist Faction's propaganda materials, distribution networks, recruitment pipelines, and the suspected infernal exploitation of the movement.

---

**II. PROPAGANDA MATERIALS; CATALOG AND ANALYSIS**

### A. Broadsheets: "The Iron Truth"
The flagship Ironist publication, printed on cheap paper and distributed weekly through an underground network. Estimated circulation: **2,000-3,000 copies per issue** across the twelve bunker-cities.

Content themes include:
- Graphic descriptions of suffering in frontline territories (Stuik, fallen Ulbgar), intended to shame Silesbian inaction
- Critiques of Reagent Lunor's leadership, ranging from policy disagreement to outright allegations of cowardice
- Calls for a "Council of War" to replace the Reagent with a military leader
- Biographical profiles of refugee families, emphasizing children: a deliberate emotional manipulation technique

Production origin: traced to a printing workshop in Grauven's Tier 4 commercial district, operated by **Elsa Kott**, a civilian bookbinder and vocal Ironist sympathizer ([[Dossier 19-3500.3417.1147]]: "Kott Printing Workshop: Surveillance Report and Production Capacity Assessment").

### B. Wall Posters: "The Gate is a Cage"
Large-format posters appearing on walls throughout Grauven, Tilvek, and Ossward. Professionally produced with arresting visual design; significantly higher production quality than the broadsheets, suggesting external funding or expertise.

The most common poster depicts the Silesbian blast doors as a **prison gate** with skeletal hands reaching through the bars from inside. The tagline reads: *"The gate was built to keep them out. Now it keeps us in."*

These posters are removed by Deep Watch patrols within hours of appearance, but new copies appear within days. Production source: unidentified. The artistic quality and graphic design sophistication exceed local capability, analysis suggests the design template may have been provided by an external source ([[Dossier 19-3500.3417.2301]]: "Wall Poster Analysis, Production Quality, Design Origins, and External Influence Indicators").

### C. Oral Propaganda: Tavern Networks
The most effective Ironist recruitment mechanism operates through **tavern-based discussion circles**; informal gatherings in garrison-district drinking establishments where grievances are aired and Ironist ideology is introduced through social pressure and peer validation.

Ascendant Watch observation of 14 such gatherings across three bunker-cities reveals a consistent pattern:

1. **Venting Phase:** New attendees are encouraged to express frustration with inaction, poor conditions, or refugee-related tensions.
2. **Validation Phase:** Experienced Ironists affirm the frustrations and provide "context": selectively presented facts about the war, food shortages, and government inaction.
3. **Radicalization Phase:** After 3-5 meetings, attendees are introduced to more extreme positions: the illegitimacy of Lunor's rule, the necessity of forcible regime change.
4. **Recruitment Phase:** Committed individuals are offered participation in "direct action": ranging from poster distribution to, in extreme cases, military cell organizing.

The process typically takes 4-8 weeks from initial contact to active recruitment ([[Dossier 19-3500.3417.3478]]: "Radicalization Pipeline: Stage Model and Intervention Opportunities").

**III. DISTRIBUTION NETWORKS**  
The Ironist distribution network operates through three channels:

1. **Garrison Channels:** Off-duty soldiers carry materials between bunker-cities using inter-garrison transit passes. The Deep Watch has not yet implemented screening of military transit: a significant oversight.
2. **Refugee Network:** Ulbgari refugees, many of whom harbor deep resentment toward Silesbian inaction, serve as willing couriers within civilian areas. Their mobility and large numbers make individual tracking impractical.
3. **Tunnel Post:** An unauthorized mail system operating through Silesbia's maintenance tunnel network. Materials are deposited at designated points and collected by local distributors. The tunnel post reaches all twelve bunker-cities ([[Dossier 19-3500.3417.4612]]: "Tunnel Post Network: Mapped Routes and Drop Point Locations").

---

**IV. LEADERSHIP STRUCTURE**  
The Ironist Faction lacks a single unified leader since **Lord Telvaine's** flight ([[Dossier 19-3500.3389]]). Current leadership is distributed among:

- **Elsa Kott** (Grauven): Controls broadsheet production and civilian recruitment
- **Sergeant-Major Dorn Ashveil** (Tilvek): Military cell organizer, recently discharged from the Tilvek garrison. Known associate of Colonel Stonecrest ([[Dossier 19-3500.7212]]), though Stonecrest has publicly distanced herself from the Ironists
- **Councilwoman Hera Blackwall** (Iron Chamber), The only remaining openly Ironist member of the legislative body. Provides political cover and legal advocacy for arrested Ironist supporters ([[Dossier 19-3500.3417.5738]]: "Blackwall Political Activity, Legislative Record and Ironist Affiliations")
- **Unknown leader**, Ossward: The Ossward Ironist cell ([[Dossier 19-3500.7212]]) operates under a highly compartmentalized structure with an unidentified directing authority

---

**V. INFERNAL EXPLOITATION ASSESSMENT**  
The Ironist Faction is **not** an infernal creation; its grievances are genuine and its core membership is motivated by authentic patriotic concern. However, the movement is being actively exploited by infernal interests:

- Lord Telvaine received material support from Thraxxia's network ([[Dossier 19-3500.6195]]). His "Auric" intermediary supplied the Solstice Ball conspirators with weapons and operational planning.
- The high-quality wall posters suggest external design input potentially originating from Thraxxia's propaganda operations ([[Dossier 03-0380.11]]: see recruitment pamphlets).
- Infernal exploitation follows the standard destabilization pattern: identify genuine grievances, amplify them through material and logistical support, and guide the resulting instability toward outcomes that serve infernal strategic objectives ([[Dossier 19-3500.3417.6893]]: "Exploitation Pattern Analysis: Ironist Movement and Infernal Destabilization Doctrine").

The critical distinction: the Ironists want Silesbia to fight the war. The infernal exploiters want Silesbia to tear itself apart so it *cannot* fight the war. These objectives are fundamentally incompatible; but the Ironist rank and file do not recognize the exploitation.

---

**VI. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **Dual Nature:** The Ironist movement is simultaneously a genuine pro-mobilization force and an infernal destabilization tool. Any intervention must account for both dimensions.
2. **Salvage Potential:** If the infernal exploitation can be exposed and severed, the Ironists' martial energy and organizational infrastructure could be redirected toward the actual war effort: a significant strategic asset.
3. **Destabilization Risk:** If left unchecked, the Ironists will eventually attempt another direct action against the Reagent, potentially succeeding. An uncontrolled coup would produce chaos that the Velvet Iron Army is positioned to exploit.
4. **Recommendation:** **[REDACTED]**: Approach Councilwoman Blackwall through a trusted intermediary and present evidence of infernal exploitation of the Ironist movement. If she can be convinced, she has the political standing to redirect the movement's energy. Simultaneously, sever Telvaine's network by identifying and neutralizing the "Auric" intermediary. **[REDACTED]**
