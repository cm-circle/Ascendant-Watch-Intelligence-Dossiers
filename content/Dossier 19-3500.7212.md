**CLASSIFIED: FOR CELESTIAL EYES ONLY**  
**REF. 19-3500.7212**  
**Origin:** Ascendant Watch: Military Assessment Division  
**Subject:** Garrison Readiness Audit: Grauven, Tilvek, and Ossward Bunker-Cities

---

**I. AUDIT PURPOSE AND METHODOLOGY**  
This assessment evaluates the combat readiness of Silesbia's three largest garrison commands, Grauven (primary capital garrison), Tilvek (northern bunker-city), and Ossward (eastern frontier), which collectively account for 60% of Silesbia's standing military force.

Data was collected through:
- Covert observation by Ascendant Watch mortal operatives embedded as supply merchants and contract workers
- Lantern scout surveillance of training exercises and guard rotations
- Analysis of intercepted internal military communications (non-encrypted standard dispatch)

---

**II. GRAUVEN GARRISON; 8,500 PERSONNEL**

**Overall Readiness Assessment: DEGRADED; 45% Combat Effective**

The Grauven garrison is the largest and theoretically best-equipped military force in Silesbia. In practice, it is a garrison in name only.

- **Training:** Formal combat drills have not been conducted in seven months. The last scheduled exercise was cancelled by the garrison commander, **General Valk Thursten**, who cited "resource conservation." Observation indicates Thursten; a Lunor loyalist; has interpreted the Doctrine of the Sealed Gate as extending to military preparedness itself ([[Dossier 19-3500.7212.1102]]: "General Thursten; Command Philosophy Assessment").
- **Equipment:** Personal weapons and armor are maintained at approximately 70% readiness. However, siege equipment, Grauven's primary defensive advantage, has deteriorated significantly. Three of eight tunnel-bore engines are non-functional. Blast-door mechanisms on Tier 3 and Tier 5 have not been tested in over a year.
- **Morale:** Morale is assessed as **critically low** among junior and mid-grade officers. Unit cohesion surveys intercepted from the garrison quartermaster's office show a 40% dissatisfaction rate, with recurring themes of purposelessness, boredom, and resentment of civilian refugees occupying military-adjacent housing ([[Dossier 19-3500.7212.2247]]: "Grauven Morale Indicators: Intercepted Survey Data and Analysis").
- **Ironist Sympathies:** An estimated 30% of Grauven's officer corps holds active sympathies with the Ironist Faction. Following the Solstice Ball incident ([[Dossier 19-3500.3389]]), overt Ironist expression has been suppressed, but underground recruitment continues through informal social networks in the garrison's off-duty establishments.

---

**III. TILVEK GARRISON; 5,200 PERSONNEL**

**Overall Readiness Assessment: MODERATE; 60% Combat Effective**

Tilvek occupies a northern position with direct exposure to potential invasion routes from the Khanate border region. Its garrison commander, **Colonel Myra Stonecrest**, maintains a significantly higher standard of preparedness than her Grauven counterpart; a stance that has generated friction with the Reagent's office.

- **Training:** Monthly combat drills are conducted, though at reduced intensity compared to pre-pacifist-era standards. Stonecrest has framed these exercises as "tunnel maintenance readiness procedures" to avoid political scrutiny ([[Dossier 19-3500.7212.3389]]: "Stonecrest: Administrative Circumvention Techniques").
- **Equipment:** Weapons and defensive systems maintained at 85% readiness. Tilvek benefits from proximity to an active starite mine, providing direct access to raw materials for equipment repair.
- **Morale:** Higher than Grauven's. Stonecrest's active leadership and the garrison's sense of purpose (protecting the northern approach) provide meaningful cohesion. However, frustration with inaction is growing.
- **Ironist Sympathies:** Estimated at 15% among officers. Stonecrest herself is not aligned with the Ironists but privately shares their military mobilization objectives. She has been in discreet contact with external parties, the nature of these communications is under investigation ([[Dossier 19-3500.7212.4512]]: "Stonecrest External Communications, Partial Intercepts and Assessment").

---

**IV. OSSWARD GARRISON; 3,800 PERSONNEL**

**Overall Readiness Assessment: CRITICAL; 30% Combat Effective**

Ossward is the most concerning of the three garrisons. Located on Silesbia's eastern frontier, the direction from which any Velvet Iron Army advance would come, it should be the most combat-ready. Instead, it is the least.

- **Training:** No formal training exercises in over a year. The garrison commander, **Major Edren Calloway**, is a political appointee with no combat experience, selected by the Iron Chamber's Shieldwall Caucus specifically because of his non-martial background ([[Dossier 19-3500.7212.5684]]: "Major Calloway, Appointment Circumstances and Qualification Assessment").
- **Equipment:** Weapons maintenance at 50%. Defensive tunnel infrastructure showing signs of neglect: two collapse alerts in the past six months due to unmaintained support structures. Ironic, given the Korvael Collapse ([[Dossier 19-3500.4368]]).
- **Morale:** Effectively non-existent. Desertions averaging 3-4 per month: soldiers simply walking off post and relocating to interior bunker-cities. No pursuit or prosecution has been undertaken.
- **Refugee Burden:** Ossward hosts the highest concentration of Ulbgari refugees (approximately 25,000) relative to its size. The garrison has been functionally converted into a refugee administration facility, with soldiers serving as food distribution coordinators rather than military personnel.
- **Ironist Sympathies:** Over 50%. Ossward's proximity to the front and its firsthand experience of refugee suffering has radicalized a significant portion of the garrison. Two non-commissioned officers have been identified as active Ironist cell organizers ([[Dossier 19-3500.7212.6831]]: "Ossward Ironist Cell: Identified Organizers and Activity Patterns").

| Ironist Penetration | 30% | 15% | 50%+ |
| Commander Type | Loyalist (passive) | Professional (discreet) | Political appointee |

**Combined effective combat strength:** Approximately 8,600 soldiers out of a nominal 17,500; a 51% reduction from paper strength.

---

**VI. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **Overall Posture:** Silesbia's military, while numerically significant on paper, is a hollow force incapable of sustained combat operations in its current state. Restoring combat effectiveness would require a minimum of 6 months of intensive retraining and re-equipment under competent leadership.
2. **Key Asset:** Colonel Stonecrest at Tilvek represents the most capable and willing military leader in the Silesbian garrison structure. She is a natural candidate for partnership in any mobilization effort.
3. **Key Risk:** Ossward's collapse in readiness means the eastern frontier is effectively undefended. Any Velvet Iron Army advance through the Greyloch Forest would encounter negligible resistance.
4. **Recommendation:** **[REDACTED]**, Establish covert contact with Colonel Stonecrest and assess her willingness to participate in coordinated mobilization planning. Simultaneously, monitor the Ossward Ironist cells, their radicalization makes them potential assets or liabilities depending on direction. **[REDACTED]**
