**I. DEPONENT IDENTIFICATION**  
**Name:** Brother Aldren Morvaine  
**Affiliation:** The Weeping Order; a monastic order devoted to Ilmater, the Crying God (deity of endurance, suffering, and martyrdom)  
**Position:** Travelling mendicant healer; present at the Solstice Ball as a guest of the Grauven Healers' Guild  
**Species:** Human male, age 62  
**Current Status:** In protective custody at an undisclosed location ([[Dossier 19-3500.8964.1108]]: "Brother Aldren: Protective Custody Arrangements and Security Protocols")

---

**II. DEPOSITION; TRANSCRIPTION**  
*The following is a direct transcription of Brother Aldren's formal deposition, taken 48 hours after the Solstice Ball incident. Interviewer: Operative Greenveil (Ascendant Watch). Deposition was recorded via enchanted scribing quill for accuracy.*

**GREENVEIL:** Brother Aldren, please describe in your own words what you observed on the night of the Solstice Ball.

**ALDREN:** I was seated at the fourth table from the dais; the Healers' Guild table. We were positioned approximately twelve meters from the Reagent's table. I had a clear line of sight to the Reagent throughout the evening.

**GREENVEIL:** When did you first notice something wrong?

**ALDREN:** The Reagent took his first sip of wine during the second toast: to "enduring peace," I believe. I noticed nothing at that moment. It was perhaps... six minutes later. The Reagent's left hand began to tremble. Very slight. Nobody else appeared to notice. But I have spent forty years treating the sick and the poisoned. That tremor was not nerves. It was *nightsbloom*.

**GREENVEIL:** You recognized the poison immediately?

**ALDREN:** Deathcap nightsbloom produces a distinctive **cascade tremor**; it begins in the extremities and moves inward. Left hand first, then the right, then the arms, then the core muscles. If untreated, full respiratory paralysis occurs within twenty minutes. I have treated three nightsbloom cases in my career. I was certain.

**GREENVEIL:** What did you do?

**ALDREN:** I stood immediately. I know I violated protocol, guests do not approach the Reagent's table uninvited. But I am a healer. I shouted, I believe I shouted "The Reagent has been poisoned." There was immediate confusion. Several guards moved to intercept me. I reached the dais and, forgive me, I physically pushed past a guard. I placed my hands on the Reagent's chest and invoked Ilmater's mercy. A *Lesser Restoration*. It arrested the tremor within seconds ([[Dossier 19-3500.8964.2246]]: "Medical Assessment, Nightsbloom Poisoning Progression and Restoration Intervention Efficacy").

**GREENVEIL:** You saved his life.

**ALDREN:** Ilmater saved his life. I simply acted. I am told the armed attack began approximately three minutes after my intervention. I was still at the dais when the blast doors sealed. I did not see the guards breach the Gallery; I was attending to the Reagent and did not look up until I heard the fighting.

**GREENVEIL:** Did you observe anything unusual about the wine service?

**ALDREN:** [Pause] The wine steward. I noticed him earlier in the evening because he was... nervous. Not the ordinary nervousness of serving at a state function. He fumbled twice, once when pouring for the Reagent, once when passing a decanter to a server. After the Reagent's first sip, the steward left the Hall through the service entrance. I did not see him return ([[Dossier 19-3500.8964.3378]]: "Wine Steward Identification, Witness Composite and Personnel Search").

**GREENVEIL:** Can you describe the steward?

**ALDREN:** Male, human, perhaps thirty years old. Brown hair, closely trimmed. Pale complexion; unusual for someone who works in the kitchens. A thin scar along his left jaw. He wore the standard Palace staff livery but it appeared... new. Unworn. As if recently issued.

**GREENVEIL:** Do you have anything else to add?

**ALDREN:** Only this. I have been a wandering healer for four decades. I have seen plague, war, and famine. But what I saw in that Hall was not random violence. The doors sealed *before* the armed attack. The poison was administered *before* the doors sealed. These were two separate actions, coordinated in sequence. Someone wanted the Reagent dead and the witnesses trapped. The guards were not the primary weapon; the poison was. The guards were insurance.

**III. ANALYSIS OF KEY CLAIMS**

### Wine Steward
The individual described by Brother Aldren does not match any registered Palace staff member. Deep Watch personnel records show no steward matching the physical description was assigned to Solstice Ball duty. The "steward" is assessed as an **inserted agent**: likely the individual who administered the nightsbloom to the Reagent's wine.

Composite sketches based on Aldren's description have been circulated to Ascendant Watch operatives. One potential sighting was reported in the Ossward refugee camp three weeks after the incident; the individual was not apprehended ([[Dossier 19-3500.8964.4512]]: "Wine Steward: Search Status and Potential Sighting Reports").

### Coordination Hypothesis
Aldren's observation that the blast doors sealed *before* the armed attack aligns with the mechanical evidence. Blast door activation requires a **command key** held by the garrison duty officer. The duty officer on the night of the Solstice Ball, **Lieutenant Fenris Kae**, was among the six rogue guards who subsequently attacked. He used his command key to seal the Gallery before joining the assault.

This confirms the attack was a **two-layer operation**: primary (poisoning) and secondary (armed assault if poison failed). The secondary layer also served as a distraction; investigations have focused heavily on the armed attackers while the poisoner escaped.

### Nightsbloom Sourcing
Deathcap nightsbloom is an extremely rare toxin derived from a fungus native to the deep caves of **Ulbgar** ([[Dossier 19-3999.11]]). Pre-war, it was available only through specialist alchemists. Post-war, the only known source is the infernal-controlled remnants of the Ulbgari tunnel network. This strongly implies the poison was **supplied by infernal contacts** ([[Dossier 19-3500.8964.5678]]: "Nightsbloom Sourcing: Deep Fungal Origins and Infernal Supply Chain").

**IV. ALDREN'S CURRENT STATUS**  
Brother Aldren was placed in Ascendant Watch protective custody voluntarily after receiving death threats. He has provided full cooperation and remains willing to testify publicly if circumstances allow. He is the **single most credible witness** to the poisoning component of the Solstice Ball attack.

His safety is a high-priority concern, if the infernal network eliminates Aldren, the poisoning angle of the investigation loses its strongest evidentiary foundation ([[Dossier 19-3500.8964.6802]]: "Witness Protection Assessment, Threat Level and Security Requirements").
