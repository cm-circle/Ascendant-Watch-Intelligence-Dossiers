**CLASSIFIED: FOR CELESTIAL EYES ONLY**  
**REF. 19-3522.5758**  
**Origin:** Ascendant Watch: Logistics Monitoring Section  
**Subject:** Stuik Supply Chain Disruptions: Evidence of Suspected Sabotage

---

**I. OVERVIEW**  
The **Kingdom of Stuik** ([[Dossier 19-3522.0001]]) is currently sustaining the heaviest frontline combat against the Velvet Iron Army alongside the Fygesian territories. Stuik's military viability depends critically on external supply chains; particularly **grain imports** from the Preanese Theocracy and **arms/materiel shipments** from Silesbia ([[Dossier 19-3500.11]]).

Over the past 18 months, both supply chains have experienced progressive and accelerating disruption. This dossier documents the pattern, identifies likely causes, and assesses strategic impact on Stuik's war-fighting capacity.

---

**II. GRAIN SUPPLY DISRUPTION; THE PREANESE PIPELINE**  
Stuik imports approximately **60% of its grain** from Preanese parishes via a network of overland trade routes crossing the Pale Marches. Pre-war monthly deliveries averaged 14,000 tonnes. Current deliveries have declined to approximately **9,200 tonnes per month**; a 34% reduction.

The reduction has been achieved through a combination of mechanisms:

- **Bureaucratic delay:** Export permits, which previously required 3 days for processing, now take an average of 21 days due to "administrative backlogs" imposed by compromised Vice-Clerics ([[Dossier 19-3611.2319]]). Each delay cascades through the logistics chain, causing spoilage, missed convoy windows, and warehousing costs.
- **Reallocation:** Grain shipments designated for Stuik have been redirected to "emergency parish reserves" in eastern Preanese parishes that have no documented emergency. Analysis of reallocation orders traces 73% to Vice-Cleric Thomen Asward's office ([[Dossier 19-3611.5423]]).
- **Route denial:** Three of five primary overland routes through the Pale Marches have been intermittently closed for "road maintenance" or "security concerns." No maintenance has been observed; the security concerns are fabricated ([[Dossier 19-3522.5758.1156]]: "Pale Marches Route Closure Analysis: Correlation with Preanese Administrative Orders").

**Impact on Stuik:**  
- Military ration quality has been reduced twice in six months. Current rations provide approximately 1,800 calories per day per soldier: below the 2,500-calorie minimum recommended for sustained combat operations.
- Civilian populations in garrison towns are experiencing localized famine. Bread riots have occurred in **Trevnor** and **Hammerseat**, two of Stuik's largest frontline settlements ([[Dossier 19-3522.5758.2301]]: "Stuik Civilian Unrest, Incident Reports and Trend Analysis").
- King Trusk Hammerward has issued formal diplomatic complaints to the Theocracy, all of which have been received by the compromised Devan Council and dismissed as "logistical misunderstandings."

---

**III. ARMS AND MATERIEL DISRUPTION; THE SILESBIAN PIPELINE**  
Stuik relies on Silesbia for approximately **40% of its manufactured armaments**, including starite-alloy weapons, shield plates, and siege bolt mechanisms. Pre-war deliveries arrived monthly via armored caravan through the Greyloch Forest corridor.

Disruptions include:

- **Silesbian export restrictions:** Reagent Lunor's pacifist *Doctrine of the Sealed Gate* ([[Dossier 19-3500.11]]) has been interpreted by Silesbian trade officials as prohibiting arms exports to active combatant states. Formal exports ceased eight months ago. Informal smuggling through sympathetic merchants continues at approximately 15% of pre-war volume.
- **Greyloch Forest interdiction:** The Arcanaloth agent operating in the Greyloch Forest ([[Dossier 19-3500.5743]]) has established at least two ambush points along the caravan route. Three shipments have been attacked in the past six months, resulting in total loss of cargo and 47 civilian teamster casualties ([[Dossier 19-3522.5758.3478]]: "Greyloch Caravan Attacks: Incident Reports and Arcanaloth Activity Correlation").
- **Jade amulet diversion:** Intelligence suggests that a portion of Silesbian arms production is being redirected through Lord Telvaine's network to unknown buyers, potentially the Velvet Iron Army itself, laundering mortal-forged weapons through third-party intermediaries ([[Dossier 19-3522.5758.4612]]: "Arms Diversion Hypothesis, Telvaine Network and End-User Analysis").

**Impact on Stuik:**  
- Frontline units report weapon and shield replacement rates 60% below required levels. Soldiers are fighting with damaged or improvised equipment.
- Stuik's indigenous arms production, primarily dwarven-run surface forges, has been scaled up but cannot compensate for the Silesbian shortfall. Current indigenous production meets approximately 55% of demand.
- Siege engine maintenance parts are critically short. Two of Stuik's twelve heavy trebuchets are non-operational due to missing components.

---

**IV. SECONDARY SUPPLY CHAIN VULNERABILITIES**

### A. Medical Supplies
Healing potions, medicinal herbs, and clerical components are sourced from multiple territories. However:

- Preanese temple tithes that historically included healing supplies have been suspended for "internal allocation" by compromised Vice-Clerics.
- Khanate herbalists ([[Dossier 19-3189.0001]]) continue to supply traditional medicines, but overland delivery from the western steppes is slow and unreliable.
- Stuik's field hospitals report potion stocks at **30% of recommended levels** ([[Dossier 19-3522.5758.5789]]: "Medical Supply Audit: Stuik Frontline Field Hospitals").

### B. Manpower
Refugee volunteers from fallen territories have partially offset Stuik's casualty rates, but these recruits are typically:

- Untrained civilians requiring 6-8 weeks of basic military instruction
- Psychologically traumatized by displacement experiences
- Dependent on the same strained supply chains for equipment and sustenance

Current Stuik military strength: approximately **22,000 active combatants**, down from a pre-war strength of 28,000. Monthly casualty rate: 300-400, offset by 200-250 new recruits.

---

**V. THE COORDINATION HYPOTHESIS**  
Analysis of the disruption patterns across all supply chains reveals a level of temporal coordination that cannot be attributed to coincidence:

- Grain delays, arms export cessation, and Greyloch caravan attacks all intensified within the same three-month window.
- The escalation correlates with the deployment of infernal agents through the Theocracy ([[Dossier 19-3611.5573]]) and the increasing radicalization of Lord Telvaine's network in Silesbia ([[Dossier 19-3500.6195]]).
- The overall pattern is consistent with a deliberate **logistical strangulation strategy**, degrading Stuik's war-fighting capacity below sustainable levels without direct military action ([[Dossier 19-3522.5758.6934]]: "Coordinated Strangulation Hypothesis, Timeline Analysis and Confidence Assessment").

This strategy aligns with Thraxxia's "velvet" doctrine of indirect subversion preceding "iron" military action ([[Dossier 03-0378.11]]).

---

**VI. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **Critical Timeline:** At current depletion rates, Stuik's military will reach **unsustainable operational deficit** within 4-6 months. Beyond this threshold, frontline forces will be unable to maintain their defensive positions.
2. **Cascading Failure:** If Stuik's frontline collapses, the Velvet Iron Army gains direct access to the Preanese Theocracy's southern border: triggering the Phase Two military invasion that the bloodless subversion was designed to precede.
3. **Immediate Need:** Restoration of any single supply chain, grain, arms, or medical, would extend Stuik's operational viability by an estimated 2-3 months. Restoration of all three would stabilize the front indefinitely.
4. **Recommendation:** **[REDACTED]**: Priority action on three fronts: (1) Expose and disrupt the Preanese grain embargo through the Commander Vassara asset and Dawn Circle network. (2) Neutralize the Greyloch Forest Arcanaloth to reopen the Silesbian arms corridor. (3) Establish an emergency supply airlift via Lantern-guided Aarakocra couriers from Tsastashan Range allies ([[Dossier 19-3781.1267]]). **[REDACTED]**
