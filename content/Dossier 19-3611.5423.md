**CLASSIFIED: FOR CELESTIAL EYES ONLY**  
**REF. 19-3611.5423**  
**Origin:** Ascendant Watch: Counter-Intelligence Division  
**Subject:** Compromised Officials: Tier One Suspects Within the Preanese Theocracy

---

**I. PURPOSE AND CLASSIFICATION CRITERIA**  
This registry identifies individuals within the Preanese Theocracy's governance structure who are assessed with **high confidence** as compromised by infernal influence. "Tier One" classification requires at least two independent corroborating intelligence streams confirming active collaboration, dependency, or impersonation. Individuals with lower-confidence assessments are tracked in the Tier Two registry ([[Dossier 19-3611.5423.1042]]: "Compromised Officials: Tier Two Suspects and Watch List").

This document is updated quarterly. Current revision reflects intelligence as of the most recent assessment cycle.

---

**II. SUSPECT PROFILES**

### SUSPECT 01: Deva Councilor Yrathiel
**Position:** Devan Council; Overseer of Eastern Parishes and Trade Policy  
**Species:** Deva (manifested)  
**Compromise Type:** Voluntary collaboration  
**Confidence:** HIGH

Yrathiel is assessed as the **primary architect** of the grain embargo affecting allied territories ([[Dossier 19-3522.5758]]: "Stuik Supply Chain Disruptions"). His motivations remain unclear: Devas are theoretically incapable of moral corruption without extraordinary external influence. Two hypotheses are under investigation:

1. Yrathiel is operating under a coerced geas or similar compulsion placed by a high-tier fiend. Arcane analysis of his public appearances has detected faint abjuration residue consistent with mind-affecting enchantments ([[Dossier 19-3611.5423.2178]]: "Yrathiel: Arcane Signature Analysis and Compulsion Hypothesis").
2. Yrathiel has been replaced by a disguised fiend. However, Deva auras are exceptionally difficult to replicate, and no known shapechanger has successfully maintained a Deva impersonation for the duration Yrathiel has been in office. This hypothesis is considered less likely but not excluded ([[Dossier 19-3611.3723]]).

Yrathiel has refused three formal summons to the Grand Reliquary for review. He has not left the eastern administrative complex in Aurevast for seven months. His personal guard has been replaced with individuals not on the original Aurelian Guard roster.

### SUSPECT 02: Deva Councilor Myreth
**Position:** Devan Council; Overseer of Judicial Affairs  
**Species:** Deva (manifested)  
**Compromise Type:** Suspected voluntary collaboration  
**Confidence:** HIGH

Myreth oversees the ecclesiastical court system and is directly responsible for the surge in heresy prosecutions targeting anti-corruption whistleblowers ([[Dossier 19-3611.2319]]). She personally authorized the Vassara Tribunal ([[Dossier 19-3611.9529]]) over objections from two fellow Council members.

Intercepted correspondence ([[Dossier 19-3611.5573]]) includes messages from Myreth's private secretary to a known Cambion intermediary, using a cipher consistent with Thraxxia's intelligence protocols ([[Dossier 19-3611.5423.3314]]: "Myreth, Communication Intercepts and Cipher Analysis"). Myreth herself may not be writing these messages, her secretary, **Aldris Venn**, is a separate Tier One target (see below).

### SUSPECT 03: Aldris Venn (Secretary to Councilor Myreth)
**Position:** Private Secretary, Office of Judicial Affairs  
**Species:** Human (unconfirmed; see notes)  
**Compromise Type:** Suspected shapechanged infiltrator  
**Confidence:** HIGH

Venn arrived in Myreth's office approximately 18 months ago, replacing a long-serving secretary who died of "natural causes", a heart failure now assessed as potentially induced by infernal toxin ([[Dossier 19-3611.5423.4489]]: "Predecessor Analysis, Death of Secretary Halmuth"). Since Venn's appointment, the volume of heresy prosecutions has increased 400% and all anti-corruption investigations within the judicial branch have been suspended.

Venn's personal background cannot be verified beyond 3 years prior to his appointment. Lantern scouts report anomalous thermal signatures when observing Venn at distance; consistent with fiendish physiology under illusion ([[Dossier 19-3611.3723]]).

### SUSPECT 04: Vice-Cleric Thomen Asward
**Position:** Parish Governor, Ilsevine Reach  
**Species:** Human  
**Compromise Type:** Jade amulet dependency and active collaboration  
**Confidence:** HIGH

Asward governs the Theocracy's most productive agricultural region. He has systematically diverted grain reserves away from export channels while maintaining surface-level compliance with Devan Council directives. Dead-drop communications via enchanted stones have been documented ([[Dossier 19-3611.11]]).

Physical observation confirms advanced jade amulet addiction, Asward presents with dilated pupils, involuntary tremors when separated from amulets for extended periods, and increasingly erratic decision-making ([[Dossier 19-3611.5423.5672]]: "Asward, Medical and Behavioral Assessment via Remote Observation").

### SUSPECT 05: Seal-Clerk Voranthi
**Position:** Office of the Celestial Seal (information gatekeeper to Light-Vicar)  
**Species:** Half-Celestial (human variant)  
**Compromise Type:** Jade amulet dependency  
**Confidence:** HIGH

Voranthi's corruption is detailed in [[Dossier 19-3611.2319.]] She has been selectively delaying, altering, and destroying intelligence reports before they reach the Light-Vicar for approximately nine months. Her amulet supply is believed to be delivered through Vice-Cleric Asward's network.

### SUSPECT 06: Seal-Clerk Orenthas
**Position:** Office of the Celestial Seal  
**Species:** Aasimar  
**Compromise Type:** Suspected Succubus replacement  
**Confidence:** HIGH

The original Orenthas has not been positively identified in **eleven months**. The individual currently occupying his position exhibits minor but consistent behavioral deviations documented by long-term observation ([[Dossier 19-3611.5423.6841]]: "Orenthas: Behavioral Deviation Timeline and Impersonation Indicators"). Cross-reference with confirmed shapechanger detection protocols ([[Dossier 19-3611.3723]]).

---

**III. NETWORK ANALYSIS**  
The six Tier One suspects do not operate independently. Network mapping reveals a **hub-and-spoke structure** with Councilor Yrathiel at the center:

- Yrathiel controls trade policy → directs Asward's grain diversion
- Yrathiel's eastern complex → suspected physical meeting point for coordination
- Myreth/Venn → suppress institutional investigation into Yrathiel's activities
- Voranthi/Orenthas → prevent intelligence about all of the above from reaching the Light-Vicar

This structure ensures **self-reinforcing protection**: each compromised node shields the others from exposure through their respective institutional roles ([[Dossier 19-3611.5423.7956]]: "Network Topology: Interconnections and Single Points of Failure").

---

**IV. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **Network Resilience:** Removing any single suspect would likely trigger exposure protocols that alert the remaining network. Effective intervention requires **simultaneous neutralization** of multiple targets.
2. **Priority Target:** Aldris Venn. If confirmed as a shapechanger, his exposure would provide undeniable evidence of infernal infiltration that even the Divine Investiture doctrine cannot dismiss.
3. **Escalation Risk:** Direct confrontation with Councilor Yrathiel, a manifested Deva, carries significant theological and political risk. If Yrathiel is indeed a genuine Deva operating under compulsion, his liberation must be handled with extreme delicacy.
4. **Recommendation:** **[REDACTED]**: Deploy a specialized team to confirm Venn's nature through close-proximity detection methods. Simultaneously, establish an independent intelligence channel to the Light-Vicar bypassing the Celestial Seal entirely. Timeline: immediate. **[REDACTED]**
