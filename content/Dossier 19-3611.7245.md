**CLASSIFIED: FOR CELESTIAL EYES ONLY**  
**REF. 19-3611.7245**  
**Origin:** Ascendant Watch: Personnel Recovery Division  
**Subject:** Missing Persons: Operative Eclipse and Operative Dawnmark

---

**I. CASE SUMMARY**  
Two Ascendant Watch mortal informants embedded within the Preanese Theocracy have been missing and presumed compromised. Both operatives ceased communication within a 72-hour window, suggesting coordinated counter-intelligence action by the infernal network operating within Preas ([[Dossier 19-3611.5423]]).

This dossier documents the circumstances of each disappearance, intelligence recovered from their last known activities, and the ongoing search effort.

---

**II. OPERATIVE ECLIPSE; CASE FILE**

**Real Name:** Soraya Velt  
**Cover Identity:** Archival clerk, Office of Parish Records, Preas Central Administration  
**Species:** Human female, age 34  
**Handler:** FOB Dawnspire ([[Dossier 01-0001.6424]])  
**Active Duration:** 22 months  
**Last Contact:** 47 days ago  

**Assignment:** Eclipse was tasked with documenting irregularities in parish-level administrative reports; specifically, discrepancies between reported and actual refugee intake numbers, militia readiness statistics, and tithe remittances. Her work directly contributed to the Parish Report Integrity Audit ([[Dossier 19-3611.2319]]).

**Circumstances of Disappearance:**  
Eclipse's last communication, transmitted via enchanted ink on a routine archival document smuggled through the courier network, contained the following fragment:

> *"Have identified a seventh Vice-Cleric showing clear signs of jade dependency. Name: [ILLEGIBLE; ink degraded]. He controls a parish on the Aurentine coast; not Eastmere. A second trafficking port. Requesting extraction window; I believe my access to the restricted stacks has been noticed. Someone searched my quarters while I was at work. Nothing taken, but-"*

The message terminates mid-sentence. Analysis of the ink degradation pattern suggests the document was exposed to a dispelling field during courier transit; possibly by the compromised Courier-Captain Trent replacement ([[Dossier 19-3611.3723]]; Case File Gamma). The illegible section may have contained the vice-cleric's name ([[Dossier 19-3611.7245.1142]]: "Eclipse Final Transmission; Ink Residue Recovery and Spectral Analysis").

**Search Efforts:**  
- Lantern Scout LN-031 conducted a thermal sweep of Eclipse's known quarters and workplace. The quarters showed signs of hasty departure; personal effects removed, furniture undisturbed.
- No body recovered. No detention record found in Preas judicial archives.
- A woman matching Eclipse's description was observed under guard at a Vice-Cleric's estate in the Thornwall Parish six days after disappearance, but the sighting has not been confirmed ([[Dossier 19-3611.7245.2278]]: "Eclipse: Unconfirmed Sighting Report, Thornwall Parish").

**Assessment:** Eclipse is assessed as **captured alive** with moderate confidence. Her intelligence value to the infernal network, specifically, her knowledge of the Ascendant Watch's collection methods and informant procedures, makes immediate execution unlikely. Interrogation is the probable current scenario.

---

**III. OPERATIVE DAWNMARK; CASE FILE**

**Real Name:** Kellan Ash  
**Cover Identity:** Junior quartermaster, Aurelian Guard South Garrison, Preas  
**Species:** Human male, age 28  
**Handler:** FOB Dawnspire ([[Dossier 01-0001.6424]])  
**Active Duration:** 9 months  
**Last Contact:** 45 days ago  

**Assignment:** Dawnmark was tasked with monitoring Aurelian Guard troop readiness, morale indicators, and any signs of infernal influence within the military garrison structure. His reporting contributed to the Conscription Readiness Assessment ([[Dossier 19-3611.2319]]).

**Circumstances of Disappearance:**  
Dawnmark failed to deliver his scheduled weekly report via dead-drop. His last known position was the South Garrison barracks. Fellow garrison members report that Dawnmark was summoned to an "unscheduled audit review" by a Vice-Cleric's adjutant and did not return.

Investigation by FOB Dawnspire agents revealed:

- No Vice-Cleric had ordered an audit review for that date.
- The individual who issued the summons; described as a tall man in clerical robes with a silver seal; does not match any known Preanese official ([[Dossier 19-3611.7245.3416]]: "Dawnmark; False Summons: Witness Descriptions and Composite Analysis").
- Dawnmark's personal effects were collected from the barracks the following day by individuals presenting garrison transfer orders signed by a captain who denies issuing them ([[Dossier 19-3611.7245.4503]]: "Dawnmark: Forged Transfer Orders: Document Analysis").

**Assessment:** Dawnmark is assessed as **captured or killed** with high confidence. His shorter operational tenure and lower-level access make sustained interrogation less likely than Eclipse's case. The sophistication of the false summons and forged documents suggests the infernal network's counter-intelligence capability is more developed than previously assessed.

---

**IV. COUNTER-INTELLIGENCE IMPLICATIONS**  
The coordinated elimination of both operatives within 72 hours indicates that the infernal network:

1. **Has identified the Ascendant Watch's mortal informant methodology**: specifically, the use of civilian cover identities in administrative positions.
2. **Possesses counter-intelligence capabilities** including surveillance detection, document forgery, and rapid-response capture teams.
3. **May have compromised FOB Dawnspire's communication protocols**, both operatives reported through Dawnspire. If the FOB's handling procedures are known to the enemy, all operatives serviced by Dawnspire must be considered at risk ([[Dossier 19-3611.7245.5689]]: "FOB Dawnspire, Communication Security Review Following Personnel Loss").

---

**V. RESCUE ASSESSMENT**

Extraction operations have been evaluated for both operatives:

- **Eclipse:** If the Thornwall sighting is confirmed, extraction is feasible but requires a strike team capable of operating within a Vice-Cleric's fortified estate. Risk: moderate. Political fallout: severe if conducted without cover.
- **Dawnmark:** Location unknown. Without a confirmed position, extraction cannot be planned. Priority: intelligence-gathering to determine status and location.

Rescue operations would require authorization from Commander Galadren ([[Dossier 01-0001.11]]) and carry significant risk of exposing the Ascendant Watch's interest in the Theocracy ([[Dossier 19-3611.7245.6817]]: "Operative Recovery: Risk Assessment and Escalation Analysis").

---

**VI. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **Personnel Impact:** The loss of two operatives has created significant intelligence gaps in both administrative monitoring and military assessment within Preas.
2. **Security Breach:** The coordinated nature of the disappearances suggests a systemic compromise. All Ascendant Watch operations within the Theocracy must be reviewed for security.
3. **Recommendation:** **[REDACTED]**, Suspend new operative insertions into Preas until the security breach is identified and contained. Redirect intelligence collection to remote methods (Lantern surveillance, courier interception) pending review. If Eclipse is confirmed alive, prioritize extraction, she possesses knowledge that, under interrogation, could compromise FOB Dawnspire and the broader Lantern network. **[REDACTED]**
