**INTELLIGENCE DOSSIER 19-3781.1267**  
**Origin:** Ascendant Watch
**Subject:** The Tsastashan Range (Aerd), and Recent Infernal Activity

---

**I. GEOGRAPHICAL OVERVIEW**  
The Tsastashan Range lies between the **Suirx Trade Company Territories** ([[Dossier 19-3428.0001]]) to the east and the **Khanate of Tsayandakh** ([[Dossier 19-3189.0001]]) to the west, with the **Kingdom of Stuik** ([[Dossier 19-3522.0001]]) forming its southern border. The range is part of the broader **Khamarynn Highlands**, extending southwest into the Inner Steppe.

Topographical features include:

- Abruptly rising peaks between 1,000-2,000 meters above the immediate lowlands.
- Plateaued Absheron Plains at 5,000-6,000 meters average elevation.
- Summits reaching as high as 9,806 meters, several of which are capped by extensive glacial formations.
- Mixed deciduous-coniferous forests along the lower slopes, thinning to alpine scrub and bare rock above 7,000 meters.
- Severe weather patterns including seasonal blizzards and gale-force winds, contributing to extreme inaccessibility.

**II. INHABITANTS; AARAKOCRA ENCLAVE AND [REDACTED]**  
Population: ~**350-400 Aarakocra**.  

Their nests cling to sheer cliffs across multiple peaks, defended by disciplined aerial patrols. Though highly mobile, their vulnerability lies in the fragility of nesting sites and dependence on narrow hunting ranges. Surrounding states have historically avoided attempts to claim Aerd due to its natural fortifications and lack of strategic value.

Records indicate that the Aarakocra of Aerd retain fragmentary echoes of **[REDACTED]** _the First Nest_, an agreement between mortals and celestials predating recorded history. Prophecy fragments suggest their dispersal would unbind, granting Hell an avenue of ascension through the high air currents. **[REDACTED]**

At least three **[REDACTED]** Sky-Talons show signs of celestial heritage. **[REDACTED]** Such information must remain undisclosed to mortals without sanction of the relevant Throne.

**III. RECENT INCIDENTS**  
Civilian reports from Bibuzanar ([[Dossier 19-3428.5621]]), Deri ([[Dossier 19-3428.1897]]), and Altayliko ([[Dossier 19-3189.7684]]) describe the following irregularities over the last year:

- Slain children and livestock, bodies exhibiting signs of extreme cold exposure and chitinous lacerations.
- Night terrors and mass hallucinations, often preceding physical attacks.
- Entire herds disappearing, leaving no tracks or blood evidence.

Since the fall of **Bibuzanar** to demonic forces, there has been no confirmed movement of infernal legions up the Tsastashan slopes. However, localized strikes against the Aarakocra and mountain villages continue at irregular intervals. Unlike incidents involving Aarakocra patrols, victims from these villages have not been returned.

---

**IV. ENEMY PRESENCE AND TACTICS**  
Our observers confirm patterns consistent with the operations of a **Gelugon (Ice Devil)**:

- Victims found frozen or impaled with icicle-like shards.
- Glacial scarring on cliffsides consistent with unnatural ice formation.
- Uncharacteristic _return of bodies_ to staging grounds: highly irregular behavior, suggesting higher command involvement.

Following Bibuzanar’s fall, the Gelugon has also turned against infernal detachments. Scattered remnants of Hell’s armies were discovered frozen solid, their insignia obliterated. Whether this indicates factional disobedience, personal ambition, or independent command remains undetermined.

It is possible the Gelugon operates under the directive of a higher infernal power. While Graz’zt’s influence in the region cannot be excluded, confirmation remains completely unavailable. Standard Gelugon behavior emphasizes efficiency in disposal of prey; the preservation and collection of corpses implies ritual or experimental objectives, possibly related to demoralization campaigns. Analysts speculate the Gelugon may be attempting to claim the Tsastashan peaks as its own stronghold. This would explain the campaign of terror against Aerd, either to drive them out or eradicate them entirely.

**V. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **Objective:** To destabilize the borderlands and render Aerd uninhabitable for the Aarakocra. Potential independent establishment of a Gelugon lair in the Tsastashan range.
2. **Capability:** A Gelugon alone is assessed as an existential threat to the Aarakocra. However, complete control of the high range remains unlikely in the immediate term (6 months).
3. **Vulnerability:** The Aarakocra are highly mobile but lack the numbers for sustained defense. Aarakocra are numerous but fragile in sustained combat; loss of Aerd would cede the high air currents to infernal forces.
4. **Recommendation:** Monitor Aerd discreetly, **[REDACTED]** deny the Gelugon permanent claim to the mountains, and monitor for possible escalation. Mortal agents are advised for intervention, as overt celestial action risks revealing **[REDACTED]**.
