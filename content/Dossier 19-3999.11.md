**CLASSIFIED: FOR CELESTIAL EYES ONLY**  
**INTELLIGENCE DOSSIER 19-3999.11**  
**Origin:** Ascendant Watch: Post-Mortem Analysis Division  
**Subject:** The Fall of the Kingdom of Ulbgar, the Jade Amulet Harvesting Operations, and Strategic Aftermath

---

**I. GEOGRAPHICAL & SUBTERRANEAN OVERVIEW**  
The Kingdom of Ulbgar occupied a vast subterranean network beneath the **Mordrek Plateau**, an elevated tableland of basalt and granite situated east of the **Preanese Theocracy** ([[Dossier 19-3611.11]]) and northeast of the **Kingdom of Stuik** ([[Dossier 19-3522.0001]]). The surface territory was sparsely inhabited; the kingdom's true extent lay beneath.

Key features included:

- A primary cave system spanning approximately **1,200 kilometers of mapped tunnels** across seven vertical strata, with unmapped lower reaches extending to unknown depths.
- The capital city of **Dûrn Kordrek**, carved from a single colossal cavern measuring 3 kilometers in diameter, illuminated by thousands of bioluminescent fungal colonies cultivated over centuries.
- **Geothermal forges** powered by natural magma channels, producing weapons, armor, and construction materials of exceptional quality.
- **Troll warrens** occupying the upper strata: semi-autonomous communities of stone trolls who served as heavy shock troops and manual laborers under a vassal arrangement dating back over 400 years.
- Surface access controlled through **fourteen fortified gatehouse complexes**, each designed to withstand prolonged siege through collapsible tunnel architecture and flooding mechanisms.

Pre-invasion population: approximately **310,000 dwarves** and **85,000 trolls**.

---

**II. GOVERNMENT & THE STONEFORGED DYNASTY**  
Ulbgar operated as a hereditary monarchy under the **Stoneforged dynasty**, a lineage claiming descent from the mythic founder-king Barlok the First Hammer. The last ruling monarch, **King Bardrum the Stoneforged**, was a dwarf of considerable martial reputation who had spent the majority of his 180-year reign expanding the lower strata and fortifying Ulbgar's defenses against deep-earth threats.

Administrative structure:

1. **The Stone Throne**: Absolute authority over military, economic, and judicial matters.
2. **The Anvil Council**: An advisory body of twelve master smiths, miners, and war-chiefs, each representing a major clan or industrial guild.
3. **Troll Thanes**: Vassal lords governing the troll warrens, permitted limited self-governance in exchange for military conscription quotas and labor tithes.
4. **The Hammerseal**: Ulbgar's internal security apparatus, numbering approximately 2,000 agents. Responsible for counter-espionage, tunnel integrity, and the suppression of deep-earth incursions.

Intelligence records ([[Dossier 19-3999.4896]]: "Pre-invasion Diplomatic Cables: Ulbgar to Stuik and Preas") reveal that King Bardrum received no fewer than **seven formal warnings** from the Ascendant Watch and allied intelligence services regarding anomalous arcane signatures detected beneath the Mordrek Plateau. Bardrum dismissed these warnings, citing the impregnability of dwarven engineering and the historical absence of any successful breach of the gatehouse complexes.

---

**III. THE FALL; TIMELINE AND MECHANISM**  
The Kingdom of Ulbgar fell in **eleven days**. The speed of its collapse remains one of the most devastating intelligence failures in recorded history.

**Day 1-2: The Deep Breach**  
Infernal forces did not assault the fortified surface gatehouses. Instead, a team of **Arcanaloth portal engineers** ([[Dossier 03-0378.11]]: "Velvet Iron Army Order of Battle") opened a series of micro-portals in the deepest unmapped strata, regions Ulbgar's miners had never reached. The initial incursion force consisted of approximately 2,000 Babau assassins, specialized for tunnel warfare ([[Dossier 19-3999.1981]]: "Tactical Analysis, Infernal Subterranean Doctrine").

**Day 3-5: The Severing**  
The Babau systematically destroyed geothermal conduits, flooding and collapsing key transit tunnels between the seven strata. This severed communication between Dûrn Kordrek and the outlying settlements, preventing coordinated defense. The troll warrens, isolated in the upper strata, were overrun first, their regenerative capabilities proving insufficient against infernal weapons designed to inflict necrotic damage ([[Dossier 19-3999.3853]]: "Infernal Anti-Regeneration Weaponry, Field Assessment").

**Day 6-8: The Siege of Dûrn Kordrek**  
With the kingdom fragmented, a larger force of Vrocks, Hezrou, and Dretch poured through widened portals into the mid-strata. King Bardrum organized a desperate defense of the capital, collapsing six of the seven access tunnels and flooding the seventh with diverted geothermal runoff. Approximately 4,000 defenders held the narrowed approach for 72 hours.

**Day 9-11: The Breach and Capitulation**  
An infernal strike force led by a **Balor designated "Ruin-Tongue"** ([[Dossier 19-3999.3178]]: "High-Value Target Profile, Balor Ruin-Tongue") breached the capital's sealed western wall through sustained application of elemental fire. The remaining defenders were slaughtered or captured. King Bardrum's body was recovered by Ascendant Watch scouts three weeks later, impaled on his own throne, his soul extracted.

---

**IV. THE JADE AMULET OPERATIONS**  
The strategic purpose of Ulbgar's conquest was not territorial; the geothermal forges were destroyed, the tunnels destabilized, and no infernal garrison has been permanently established. Instead, Ulbgar serves as a **soul harvesting facility**.

Intelligence from deep-insertion operatives ([[Dossier 19-3999.8638]]: "Operative Thornshade: Field Report: Dûrn Kordrek Interior") confirms the following:

- Captured dwarven and troll populations, estimated at **40,000-60,000 survivors**, have been confined to the remnants of Dûrn Kordrek's lower chambers.
- Infernal overseers, primarily Yugoloth task-masters, employ a ritual process by which mortal souls are **magically compressed into jade amulets**. The process does not immediately kill the subject; rather, it strips away fragments of the soul over repeated sessions, producing multiple amulets from a single individual before final dissolution.
- Each jade amulet contains sufficient soul-energy to power minor infernal enchantments, serve as currency in abyssal economies, or be consumed for sustenance by mid-tier fiends.
- Production output is estimated at **200-300 amulets per week**. Amulets are transported via portal to distribution points, including Thraxxia's stronghold at Ziragz ([[Dossier 03-0380.11]]) and unknown destinations in the lower planes.
- The jade amulets have also been used as **bribes to corrupt mortal officials** in territories like the Preanese Theocracy ([[Dossier 19-3611.11]]), their soul-energy conferring a euphoric, addictive sensation upon physical contact ([[Dossier 19-3999.9275]]: "Jade Amulet Properties: Alchemical and Psychometric Analysis").

---

**V. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **Current Status:** The Kingdom of Ulbgar is effectively destroyed as a political entity. The tunnel network remains partially navigable but is laced with infernal sentries and structural traps.
2. **Ongoing Threat:** The jade amulet harvesting operation represents a significant strategic asset for the Velvet Iron Army, providing both a currency system and a corruption tool for their broader subversion campaigns.
3. **Vulnerability:** The harvesting operation relies on portal infrastructure maintained by Arcanaloth engineers. Disruption of these portals would sever supply lines, strand the Yugoloth overseers, and potentially allow surviving captives to resist or escape.
4. **Refugee Impact:** An estimated **120,000 Ulbgari refugees** fled through the surface gatehouses before infernal forces sealed them. The majority have settled in Silesbia ([[Dossier 19-3500.11]]), placing severe strain on that territory's resources.
5. **Recommendation:** **[REDACTED]**, Priority: identify and target the Arcanaloth portal engineers sustaining the harvesting operation. Secondary: establish contact with any surviving resistance elements within the tunnel network. Intelligence suggests a remnant force under **Thane Grundir Ashcleave** may still be operational in the sixth stratum ([[Dossier 19-3999.4637]]: "Unconfirmed Transmission, Frequency Analysis: Sixth Stratum Beacon"). **[REDACTED]**
