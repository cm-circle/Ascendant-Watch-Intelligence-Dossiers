**CLASSIFIED: FOR CELESTIAL EYES ONLY**  
**REF. 19-3999.1981**  
**Origin:** Ascendant Watch: Infernal Tactical Studies  
**Subject:** Tactical Analysis: Infernal Subterranean Doctrine

---

**I. PURPOSE**  
This analysis documents the tactics, techniques, and procedures employed by the Velvet Iron Army during the subterranean invasion of Ulbgar. The eleven-day campaign represents the first confirmed deployment of **purpose-built infernal tunnel warfare doctrine** and provides critical intelligence for the defense of remaining subterranean territories; particularly Silesbia's bunker-cities ([[Dossier 19-3500.11]]).

---

**II. PHASE ONE; DEEP BREACH (DAYS 1-2)**

### Force Composition
The initial assault force consisted of approximately **2,000 Babau assassins** (CR 4) inserted through micro-portals in unmapped strata beneath Ulbgar's lowest level. Babau were selected for this role based on specific capabilities:

- **Innate Darkness:** Babau can generate magical darkness, negating dwarven darkvision within a 15-foot radius: a critical tactical advantage in tunnel environments.
- **Acid Secretion:** Their mucous membrane coating dissolves organic material on contact, allowing silent elimination of sentries without weapon noise.
- **Dimensional Agility:** Babau possess limited short-range teleportation, enabling them to bypass sealed doors, collapsed barricades, and narrow chokepoints.

### Insertion Technique
The micro-portals used for insertion were **temporary one-way constructs**, designed to accept troop throughput for approximately 4 hours before collapsing. This technique trades portal infrastructure durability for undetectability; the portals leave no persistent arcane signature that pre-positioned detection wards would identify ([[Dossier 19-3999.1981.1147]]: "Micro-Portal Insertion Technique, Technical Specifications and Countermeasure Gaps").

Each micro-portal delivered approximately 200 Babau per hour. Ten portals operating simultaneously achieved full insertion of 2,000 units within a single operational period.

---

**III. PHASE TWO; THE SEVERING (DAYS 3-5)**

### Objective
Destroy the connective infrastructure between Ulbgar's seven vertical strata, preventing coordinated defense and isolating population centers for sequential destruction.

### Technique: "The Root Cut"
Babau teams targeted three categories of infrastructure simultaneously:

1. **Geothermal conduits:** Destruction of these channels caused immediate loss of heating, lighting, and forge power to dependent strata. In several cases, severed conduits released superheated steam into inhabited areas, causing casualties and panic ([[Dossier 19-3999.1981.2289]]: "Geothermal Infrastructure Targeting: Damage Assessment by Stratum").
2. **Transit tunnels:** Load-bearing pillars and ceiling supports were selectively dissolved using Babau acid and supplementary transmutation magic, collapsing key transit routes. The precision of the collapses, targeting specific sections while leaving adjacent areas intact, indicates pre-assault geological survey data that should not have been available to external forces.
3. **Communication relays:** Ulbgar's internal messaging system relied on dwarven-engineered sonic relay stations: tuning forks embedded in the tunnel walls that transmitted encoded vibrations across distances. The Babau destroyed 80% of these relays within 48 hours, leaving Dûrn Kordrek effectively deaf and blind.

### Psychological Warfare Component
During the Severing, Babau units employed terror tactics designed to maximize panic:

- Bodies of slain dwarves and trolls were arranged in ritualistic patterns at tunnel intersections
- The Babau's darkness aura was used to create **wandering zones of absolute blackness** that moved through inhabited areas, disorienting civilians
- Sonic disruption devices (origin: unknown, not standard Babau equipment) generated infrasonic frequencies that induced nausea, vertigo, and irrational fear in exposed populations ([[Dossier 19-3999.1981.3401]]: "Psychological Operations, Infrasonic Warfare Devices and Observed Effects")

---

**IV. PHASE THREE; MAIN ASSAULT (DAYS 6-11)**

### Force Escalation
With the kingdom fragmented, the Velvet Iron Army widened its micro-portals into **stabilized Class-IV portals** and deployed main-force elements:

- 5,000 Dretch (wave assault through flooded corridors)
- 2,000 Vrocks (aerial assault through large cavern spaces: particularly effective in Dûrn Kordrek's 3-kilometer capital cavern)
- 800 Hezrou (heavy assault through barricaded positions)
- 1 Balor, "Ruin-Tongue", deployed for final breach of the capital ([[Dossier 19-3999.3178]])

### Tunnel Warfare Adaptations
The main-force deployment revealed purpose-built adaptations for subterranean operations:

- **Dretch Flooding:** Dretches were deployed in densities exceeding 100 per tunnel section, creating a literal wall of bodies that advanced through corridors regardless of defensive fire. Defenders who killed the front ranks were immediately buried under the next wave. The Dretch fetid cloud death-gas accumulated in enclosed spaces with devastating effect.
- **Vrock Cave Tactics:** In large cavern spaces, Vrocks operated in vertical attack formations: diving from ceiling heights to strike defenders on elevated positions, then retreating above melee range.
- **Hezrou Barrier Breaking:** Hezrou were used specifically to destroy barricades, sealed doors, and improvised defensive works. Their stench aura preceded them, weakening defenders before physical contact ([[Dossier 19-3999.1981.4567]]: "Main Force Adaptation: Subterranean Combat Modifications, Unit-by-Unit").

**V. ANTI-TROLL OPERATIONS**  
The troll warrens (upper strata) were specifically targeted with **anti-regeneration protocols**:

- Weapons were pre-treated with necrotic enchantments that prevented troll regeneration ([[Dossier 19-3999.3853]])
- Fire-element Dretch variants were deployed in troll-inhabited areas: a sub-type not observed in surface operations, suggesting purpose-breeding for the Ulbgar campaign
- Troll Thanes who attempted to negotiate surrender were killed publicly and their bodies burned, a deliberate demonstration that no quarter would be offered ([[Dossier 19-3999.1981.5738]]: "Anti-Troll Operations, Doctrinal Analysis and Precedent Assessment")

---

**VI. IMPLICATIONS FOR SILESBIAN DEFENSE**  
The Ulbgar campaign provides a tactical blueprint that could be replicated against Silesbia's bunker-cities. Key parallels and vulnerabilities:

| Ulbgar Feature | Silesbian Equivalent | Risk Level |
|---|---|---|
| Unmapped lower strata | Unexplored cave systems below bunker-cities | HIGH |
| Geothermal infrastructure | Geothermal venting systems in all 12 cities | CRITICAL |
| Sonic relay communications | Similar vibration-based messaging system | MODERATE |
| Single-species garrison | Mixed garrison; less susceptible to monoculture exploitation | LOW |
| Sealed surface gates | Reinforced blast doors; equally irrelevant if bypassed | HIGH |

The Greyloch Forest portal ([[Dossier 19-3500.5743]]) may represent the preparatory phase of a Silesbia-targeted application of this doctrine ([[Dossier 19-3999.1981.6893]]: "Silesbia Vulnerability Assessment: Ulbgar Doctrine Application Scenarios").

---

**VII. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **Doctrine Maturity:** The infernal subterranean doctrine is purpose-built, thoroughly tested, and devastatingly effective. It assumes defenders will rely on surface-denial and will be unprepared for attacks from below.
2. **Countermeasure Priority:** Detection of micro-portal insertion is the critical defensive gap. Investment in deep-stratum arcane sensor networks is essential for any subterranean territory.
3. **Recommendation:** **[REDACTED]**: Distribute this tactical analysis to Silesbia's Deep Watch and Stuik's military engineering corps. Specifically flag the geothermal infrastructure vulnerability and the sonic relay targeting. Both territories must implement deep-stratum monitoring immediately. **[REDACTED]**
