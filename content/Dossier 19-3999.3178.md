**I. TARGET IDENTIFICATION**  
**Designation:** Ruin-Tongue  
**True Name:** **[REDACTED; EMPYREAN CLEARANCE REQUIRED]**  
**Classification:** Balor (Greater Demon); CR 19  
**Current Assessment:** Senior Field Marshal of the Velvet Iron Army's military wing ([[Dossier 03-0378.11]])  
**Physical Markers:** 4.5 meters tall. Left horn shattered at the midpoint; a battle scar predating the material plane invasion. Carries a whip of living fire that extends to approximately 15 meters. His sword; designated **"Ashverdict"**; is a greatsword of black iron that trails molten sparks and bears inscriptions in a proto-Abyssal dialect that predates known infernal linguistic records.

---

**II. OPERATIONAL HISTORY; MATERIAL PLANE**

### The Ulbgar Breach
Ruin-Tongue personally led the final assault on Dûrn Kordrek ([[Dossier 19-3999.11]]). After the Babau Severing and Dretch flooding had fragmented the kingdom's defenses, Ruin-Tongue was deployed as the **decisive weapon** to breach the capital's sealed western wall.

Eyewitness accounts from surviving defenders (evacuated through upper strata before final collapse) describe the breach:

> *"The wall was three meters of dwarven granite, reinforced with iron bands and blessed by the Stone-Priests. It had stood for six hundred years. The Balor struck it once with his sword and a crack appeared. He struck it twice and the crack became a fissure. He struck it a third time and the wall was gone. Not collapsed: gone. As though it had never existed."*

([[Dossier 19-3999.3178.1182]]: "Dûrn Kordrek Breach: Survivor Testimonies and Structural Analysis")

Following the breach, Ruin-Tongue personally killed **King Bardrum the Stoneforged**. The manner of execution, impaling the king on his own throne, was a deliberate symbolic act: a message that dwarven sovereignty was not merely defeated but desecrated.

### Post-Ulbgar Operations
Following Ulbgar's fall, Ruin-Tongue was not observed in the continued occupation. He reappeared on the eastern Stuik front approximately six weeks later, commanding a major assault on the fortress-town of **Hammerseat**. The assault was repulsed; one of the few confirmed tactical defeats the Velvet Iron Army has suffered; but at a cost of 2,400 Stuik defenders killed ([[Dossier 19-3999.3178.2347]]: "Battle of Hammerseat; Tactical Analysis and Ruin-Tongue's Command Performance").

Ruin-Tongue's command style is assessed as **aggressive but intelligent**; he commits overwhelming force to a single point of attack rather than distributing forces across a broad front. This contrasts with the Velvet Iron Army's typical doctrine and suggests Ruin-Tongue operates with significant tactical autonomy.

---

**III. HISTORICAL INTELLIGENCE**  
Celestial archives identify Ruin-Tongue as one of the **oldest surviving Balors**; his existence predates the current Age by at least two full cycles. Known prior activities include:

- Participation in the **Siege of Celestia's Third Gate** during the Second Abyss War: one of only four Balors to survive the engagement
- Destruction of the **Crystal Citadel of Velunthae** on an unnamed material plane approximately 800 years ago
- Service under multiple Demon Lords before swearing allegiance to Graz'zt: the timing and circumstances of this allegiance are unknown but assessed as recent (within the past century)

Ruin-Tongue's relationship with Thraxxia is **hostile**. Intercepted communications ([[Dossier 03-0380.4458]]: "Internal Friction, Thraxxia vs. Balor War Council") confirm that Ruin-Tongue has openly criticized Thraxxia's "soft conquest" doctrine and has advocated for the destruction of Ziragz's mortal population, which he views as a wasteful indulgence ([[Dossier 19-3999.3178.3514]]: "Ruin-Tongue, Anti-Thraxxia Statements and Factional Position").

---

**IV. CAPABILITIES AND VULNERABILITIES**

### Combat Capabilities
- **Ashverdict:** The weapon appears to function as a standard Balor greatsword with additional properties: sustained contact with dwarven stone causes catastrophic structural failure disproportionate to the kinetic force applied. This suggests an enchantment specifically tuned for fortress destruction.
- **Fire Whip:** Standard Balor fire whip, extended range (15m). Used primarily for area control and target grappling.
- **Death Throes:** As with all Balors, Ruin-Tongue's death would trigger a catastrophic explosion ([[Dossier 03-0378.4553]]). Given his age and power, the detonation is estimated to be larger than the standard Balor baseline: estimated blast radius of **150 meters** with temperatures sufficient to melt stone.
- **Tactical Intelligence:** Unlike many Balors, Ruin-Tongue demonstrates strategic thinking, retreat discipline, and the ability to learn from failed engagements (the Hammerseat withdrawal was orderly, not panicked).

### Assessed Vulnerabilities
- **Ashverdict Dependency:** Ruin-Tongue has been observed reacting protectively when Ashverdict was threatened during the Hammerseat battle, a soldier managed to grapple the weapon for approximately three seconds before being incinerated. This suggests the sword may have unique significance beyond its combat utility ([[Dossier 19-3999.3178.4682]]: "Ashverdict, Significance Analysis and Potential Vulnerability").
- **Factional Isolation:** Ruin-Tongue's conflict with Thraxxia means he operates without the intelligence support her network provides. This isolation may be exploitable: feeding false intelligence to Ruin-Tongue through channels he trusts could produce tactical advantages.
- **Pride:** Ruin-Tongue's personal kill of King Bardrum, rather than delegating to subordinates, suggests a pride-driven need for personal glory. This could be exploited to draw him into unfavorable tactical positions ([[Dossier 19-3999.3178.5817]]: "Psychological Profile: Pride Exploitation Scenarios").

---

**V. ENGAGEMENT PROTOCOLS**  
Direct engagement with Ruin-Tongue is **not recommended** under any circumstances short of total defensive necessity. A Balor of his age and capability requires a minimum of:

- 3-4 CR 15+ combatants operating in coordination
- Pre-prepared battlefield with fire-resistance infrastructure
- Containment wards to prevent dimensional escape
- Death-throes evacuation plan for a 150-meter blast radius

No mortal force currently deployed on the material plane meets these requirements. Celestial intervention would be required for a deliberate engagement; which triggers detection-threshold complications ([[Dossier 01-0001.11]]).

Current doctrine: **avoid, monitor, and exploit factional tensions** rather than attempt direct confrontation ([[Dossier 19-3999.3178.6203]]: "Ruin-Tongue Engagement Doctrine: Avoidance Protocols and Factional Exploitation").

---

**VI. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **Threat Level:** Ruin-Tongue is the most dangerous individual combatant in the Velvet Iron Army's material plane forces. His combination of raw power, tactical intelligence, and historical experience makes him a tier above standard Balors.
2. **Strategic Value of Elimination:** Removing Ruin-Tongue would decapitate the military wing's field command and create a succession struggle among the remaining 49 Balors: a significant operational disruption.
3. **Recommendation:** **[REDACTED]**, Do not attempt engagement at this time. Continue monitoring and intelligence collection. Investigate Ashverdict's nature, if the weapon contains a bound essence or phylactery-equivalent, its destruction could provide a non-combat vector for neutralization. **[REDACTED]**
