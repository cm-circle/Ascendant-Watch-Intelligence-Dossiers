**CLASSIFIED: FOR CELESTIAL EYES ONLY**  
**REF. 19-3999.3853**  
**Origin:** Ascendant Watch: Weapons Analysis Division  
**Subject:** Infernal Anti-Regeneration Weaponry: Field Assessment

---

**I. OVERVIEW**  
During the fall of Ulbgar, the Velvet Iron Army deployed a class of **necrotic-enchanted weaponry** specifically designed to negate the regenerative capabilities of trolls; a key component of Ulbgar's military strength. This dossier documents the recovered specimens, their properties, and their implications for the broader war.

---

**II. RECOVERED SPECIMENS**  
Three weapon specimens were recovered from the Ulbgar collapse zone by Ascendant Watch deep-insertion operatives:

- **Specimen A:** A Babau-scaled short sword, single-edged, with a blade of blackened iron etched with necrotic sigils
- **Specimen B:** An arrowhead (Vrock-launched javelin tip), crystallized obsidian infused with visible dark energy threads
- **Specimen C:** A Hezrou-scaled warhammer head, enchanted with a persistent necrotic field that causes organic material to blacken and decay on contact

All three specimens share common enchantment characteristics:

- **Necrotic Resonance:** Wounds inflicted by these weapons channel necrotic energy directly into the victim's regenerative biology. In trolls, this disrupts the natural healing cascade, forcing the creature's body to fight both the physical wound and the necrotic contamination simultaneously.
- **Regeneration Suppression Duration:** Testing on cadaveric troll tissue indicates that regeneration is fully suppressed for **4-6 hours** after a single wound. Multiple wounds extend suppression proportionally ([[Dossier 19-3999.3853.1183]]: "Specimen Testing: Regeneration Suppression Duration and Dose-Response Curves").
- **Residual Effect:** Even after the necrotic energy dissipates, healed wounds exhibit scarification that does not regenerate: a permanent marker of exposure. Surviving trolls from Ulbgar bear distinctive black-web scarring patterns.

---

**III. ENCHANTMENT ANALYSIS**  
Arcane examination of the recovered specimens reveals:

- The necrotic enchantments are **factory-produced**, standardized, mass-manufactured, and applied through a repeatable ritual process requiring no individual customization. This indicates an **industrial enchantment pipeline** operated somewhere within the infernal logistics chain ([[Dossier 19-3999.3853.2347]]: "Enchantment Manufacturing Assessment, Production Scale and Facility Requirements").
- The enchantments draw energy from the **Negative Energy Plane** through embedded micro-conduits in the weapon's material structure. The conduits are not self-sustaining; they deplete after approximately 100 strikes, rendering the weapon mundane. This disposability is consistent with the Velvet Iron Army's approach to Dretch forces: high volume, low investment per unit.
- The sigil patterns are not standard Abyssal or Infernal script. They most closely resemble **Yugoloth technical notation**, consistent with the Yugoloth auxiliaries' role in specialized equipment provision ([[Dossier 03-0378.11]]). The weapons may be manufactured by Yugoloth armorsmiths under contract ([[Dossier 19-3999.3853.3514]]: "Sigil Analysis, Yugoloth Technical Notation Comparison").

---

**IV. BROADER APPLICATIONS AND CONCERNS**  
The anti-regeneration weaponry was deployed specifically against trolls in Ulbgar, but the underlying necrotic enchantment mechanism is **not troll-specific**. Testing indicates effectiveness against:

- Any creature with natural regeneration (trolls, hydras, certain lycanthropes)
- Clerical healing magic, necrotic-infected wounds resist *Cure Wounds* and similar spells, requiring dispelling of the necrotic contamination before healing can take effect ([[Dossier 19-3999.3853.4682]]: "Interaction with Divine Healing, Observed Interference and Workaround Protocols")
- Troll-blooded individuals and half-trolls: a significant concern for Stuik, whose integrated orc minority includes individuals with regenerative heritage

The potential deployment of these weapons against **mortal armies** would significantly degrade the effectiveness of battlefield healing; the primary force multiplier that currently allows mortal armies to sustain combat against numerically superior infernal forces.

---

**V. COUNTERMEASURE DEVELOPMENT**  
Research into countermeasures is underway at FOB Ashwatch under Commander Galadren's direction:

1. **Pre-treatment Wards:** An abjuration-based protective enchantment applied to armor that neutralizes necrotic enchantments on contact. Currently functional at prototype level; requires approximately 10 minutes of spellcasting per suit of armor and lasts 24 hours. Not yet scalable to army-wide deployment ([[Dossier 19-3999.3853.5817]]: "Anti-Necrotic Ward Prototype: Specifications, Limitations, and Scaling Challenges").
2. **Enhanced Healing Protocols:** Modified *Greater Restoration* spell sequences that include a necrotic-dispelling component. Effective but resource-intensive: each application requires a 4th-level slot minimum.
3. **Weapon Degradation:** The weapons' limited 100-strike lifespan is an exploitable vulnerability. Defensive tactics that force fiends to expend their enchanted weapons on expendable targets (e.g., summoned creatures, animated objects) could neutralize the weapon advantage before the main engagement.

---

**VI. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **Current Threat:** Anti-regeneration weaponry has been deployed exclusively in subterranean operations to date. Extension to surface warfare is assessed as likely if the war of attrition on the Stuik front demands tactical escalation.
2. **Healing Impact:** If deployed against mortal armies, the interference with clerical healing would shift the casualty equation dramatically in the Velvet Iron Army's favor.
3. **Production Vulnerability:** The Yugoloth manufacture hypothesis suggests a single production facility. Identifying and disrupting this facility would remove the weapon from the infernal arsenal.
4. **Recommendation:** **[REDACTED]**: Prioritize counter-ward scaling for deployment to Stuik frontline units. Investigate Yugoloth armament production facilities through Arcanaloth attrition tracking ([[Dossier 03-0378.4338]]) and portal logistics analysis ([[Dossier 03-0380.6152]]). Distribute specimen analysis results to allied military commanders with operational security restrictions. **[REDACTED]**
