**CLASSIFIED: FOR CELESTIAL EYES ONLY**  
**REF. 19-3999.4637**  
**Origin:** Ascendant Watch: Signals Analysis Section  
**Subject:** Unconfirmed Transmission: Frequency Analysis: Sixth Stratum Beacon

---

**I. DETECTION**  
Eleven weeks after the fall of Dûrn Kordrek, Lantern Scout LN-044, operating at the Mordrek Plateau surface monitoring station, detected an anomalous **low-frequency vibration** emanating from the subterranean structure below. The vibration was initially attributed to geological settling of the collapsed tunnel infrastructure. Routine logging continued until a pattern was identified.

The vibration is **rhythmic and structured**; repeating in a precise 47-second cycle:

```
[TAP-TAP-TAP] [PAUSE 3s] [TAP-TAP] [PAUSE 3s] [TAP-TAP-TAP-TAP] [PAUSE 3s] [TAP-TAP-TAP]
... [12-second rest] ... [CYCLE REPEATS]
```

Dwarven linguistic analysis identified this as a **Hammercode** transmission; the dwarven equivalent of military signal code, transmitted through stone and metal by physical percussion. The decoded message reads:

**"ULBGAR ENDURES. ULBGAR ENDURES."**

([[Dossier 19-3999.4637.1183]]: "Hammercode Decryption: Linguistic Analysis and Confidence Assessment")

---

**II. SOURCE LOCALIZATION**  
Analysis of the vibration's frequency, amplitude, and propagation characteristics indicates:

- **Source Depth:** Approximately Stratum 3 (upper-mid levels of the original Ulbgari tunnel system): consistent with the water treatment facility where Operative Thornshade made contact with survivors ([[Dossier 19-3999.8638]])
- **Transmission Medium:** A geothermal conduit pipe: one of the few infrastructure elements that was not destroyed during the Severing ([[Dossier 19-3999.1981]]). The pipe's metal composition allows sound vibrations to travel efficiently for several kilometers.
- **Estimated Distance:** The vibration propagates approximately 4.7 kilometers from source to the nearest surface-accessible detection point.
- **Signal Strength:** Weak but consistent. The transmitter is using physical hand tools; a hammer or mallet; rather than any mechanical or magical amplification device ([[Dossier 19-3999.4637.2347]]: "Vibration Propagation Analysis; Source Depth, Medium, and Distance Calculations").

---

**III. IDENTIFIED TRANSMITTER; THANE GRUNDIR**  
Cross-referencing with Thornshade's Report 5 ([[Dossier 19-3999.8638]]), the transmitter is almost certainly **Thane Grundir**, last known leader of Ulbgari survivors within the fallen kingdom. Thornshade's garbled report described a Thane broadcasting a repeating signal through a geothermal pipe; the pattern, location, and medium match precisely.

### Grundir's Known Background
**Name:** Grundir Ironheart  
**Species:** Shield Dwarf, age approximately 180  
**Previous Position:** Thane of the Sixth Stratum; governor of Ulbgar's deepest inhabited level, primarily responsible for deep-mining operations and troll vassal administration  
**Reputation:** A stern but pragmatic administrator, known for unusually progressive relations with the troll population under his governance. He was one of the few Thanes who publicly supported the troll early-warning reports that King Bardrum dismissed ([[Dossier 19-3999.4896]]).

Grundir's survival is assessed as highly probable. His knowledge of the deep tunnel system and his administrative authority over the lower strata would have given him both the geographic knowledge and the social authority to organize survivors.

---

**IV. MESSAGE ANALYSIS**  
The message "ULBGAR ENDURES" is notable in several respects:

1. **It is a broadcast, not a targeted communication.** Hammercode through geothermal pipes is omnidirectional: the signal propagates to anyone with ears pressed to the right surface. Grundir is not attempting to reach a specific recipient; he is declaring survival to anyone listening.

2. **The repetition interval (47 seconds)** is consistent with a single individual tapping with one hand while resting between cycles. The 12-second rest period suggests physical fatigue: Grundir has been maintaining this broadcast for **at minimum 11 weeks**, tapping every hour.

3. **The message has not changed.** A static broadcast suggests either (a) Grundir has no new information to convey, (b) he lacks the resources to compose more complex messages, or (c) the message itself is the objective: a morale signal to any scattered survivors who might hear it through the pipe network.

4. **Secondary Pattern:** Embedded within the primary broadcast, at irregular intervals, a **different pattern** has been detected, shorter, more complex, and using a different Hammercode dialect. Initial analysis suggests this secondary signal may contain tactical information, survivor counts, supply status, or location data, but the dialect is archaic and has not yet been fully decoded ([[Dossier 19-3999.4637.3514]]: "Secondary Signal, Archaic Hammercode Dialect and Partial Decryption").

---

**V. INFERNAL AWARENESS ASSESSMENT**  
Critical question: does the enemy know about the beacon?

- The Arcanaloths operating in Dûrn Kordrek ([[Dossier 19-3999.8638]]) would not naturally detect a mechanical vibration transmitted through metal pipes: their detection capabilities are arcane in nature.
- The Mezzoloth guards are unlikely to recognize Hammercode as structured communication rather than ambient noise.
- However, if the infernal forces have captured dwarven prisoners with knowledge of Hammercode ([[Dossier 19-3999.8638]] confirms forced labor in the forges), a captured dwarf could potentially identify the signal as intentional communication ([[Dossier 19-3999.4637.4682]]: "Infernal Detection Risk: Probability Assessment and Mitigation Strategies").

Current assessment: **low to moderate probability** that the enemy is aware of the beacon. However, the risk increases with time, and any rescue operation's timing must account for the possibility that the enemy is already monitoring the signal and using it to locate the survivors.

---

**VI. RESPONSE OPTIONS**

### Option A: Passive Monitoring
Continue listening to the beacon without responding. Advantage: zero risk of revealing Ascendant Watch interest. Disadvantage: the survivors may not be able to hold out indefinitely without external support.

### Option B: Signal Response
Transmit a return Hammercode signal through the same geothermal pipe network, confirming that the beacon is heard and that rescue is planned. This would require a surface operative to physically access the pipe terminus and tap a response.

Risk: if the enemy is monitoring the pipe network, a response signal would confirm that external forces are interested in the survivors.

### Option C: Integrated Rescue
Incorporate the beacon intelligence into the planned Dûrn Kordrek forge raid, use Grundir's survivor location as a rally point during the operation, and task the rescue element with reaching the water treatment facility as a secondary objective ([[Dossier 19-3999.4637.5817]]: "Rescue Integration, Forge Raid Operational Concept and Survivor Rally point").

---

**VII. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **Symbolic Value:** Dwarven survivors maintaining a resistance signal inside a conquered kingdom carries enormous symbolic weight. Their rescue, if achievable, would serve as a rallying point for allied morale across the continent.
2. **Intelligence Value:** Thane Grundir, with 180 years of experience and intimate knowledge of the lower strata, is an invaluable intelligence asset for any future operations in or beneath the Mordrek Plateau.
3. **Urgency:** The beacon has been active for 11+ weeks. Survivor supplies will not last indefinitely. If the secondary signal contains supply status data, decryption is urgent.
4. **Recommendation:** **[REDACTED]**, Pursue Option C: integrated rescue within the forge raid. Prioritize decryption of the secondary signal to assess survivor supply status and inform operational timeline. Do not respond to the beacon unless rescue operation is imminent, maintain passive monitoring. **[REDACTED]**
