**CLASSIFIED: FOR CELESTIAL EYES ONLY**  
**REF. 19-3999.4896**  
**Origin:** Ascendant Watch: Diplomatic Archives  
**Subject:** Pre-invasion Diplomatic Cables: Ulbgar to Stuik and Preas (Ignored Warnings)

---

**I. OVERVIEW**  
This dossier compiles seven formal diplomatic warnings transmitted to **King Bardrum the Stoneforged** of Ulbgar from allied intelligence services and the Ascendant Watch over a period of fourteen months prior to the kingdom's fall. Each warning identified anomalous arcane activity beneath the Mordrek Plateau and urged defensive preparation. All seven were dismissed.

The cables are presented in chronological order with the king's documented responses.

---

**II. CABLE ARCHIVE**

### Cable 1: Ascendant Watch Initial Advisory (14 months pre-fall)
**From:** Commander Galadren, FOB Ashwatch ([[Dossier 01-0001.11]])  
**To:** King Bardrum, via Hammerseal courier  
**Content:** *"Lantern scout detections indicate conjuration-school arcane activity at approximately 2,000 meters below the lowest mapped stratum of the Ulbgari tunnel system. Signatures are consistent with portal preparation. Recommend immediate deep-survey and reinforcement of lower stratum access points."*

**Bardrum's Response:** *"The Ascendant Watch may concern itself with the sky. The stone is ours. No portal has ever breached dwarven-sealed bedrock. No survey is required."*

([[Dossier 19-3999.4896.1108]]: "Cable 1: Full Text and Response Analysis")

### Cable 2: Stuik Intelligence Advisory (12 months pre-fall)
**From:** Royal War Cabinet of Stuik ([[Dossier 19-3522.0001]])  
**To:** King Bardrum, via diplomatic pouch  
**Content:** *"Stuik forward observers report increased infernal activity along the eastern Mordrek surface. Patrol elements of the Velvet Iron Army have been observed conducting geological surveys near surface fault lines above Ulbgari territory. This pattern is consistent with pre-assault reconnaissance."*

**Bardrum's Response:** *"Let them survey. They will find nothing but granite and contempt. The fourteen gates are sealed. I will not waste resources chasing shadows on the surface."*

### Cable 3: Ascendant Watch Escalated Warning (10 months pre-fall)
**From:** FOB Underhearth ([[Dossier 01-0001.11]])  
**To:** Hammerseal Intelligence directorate  
**Content:** *"Arcane signatures have tripled in intensity. We are now detecting at least three distinct conjuration sources at irregular intervals. Pattern is consistent with coordinated multi-portal construction. URGENT: We believe a breach is imminent within 6-12 months. Repeat: this assessment is URGENT."*

**Hammerseal Response:** The Hammerseal directorate forwarded this cable to King Bardrum with a recommendation for lower-stratum survey. Bardrum rejected the recommendation and instructed the Hammerseal to **suspend all communication** with the Ascendant Watch's subterranean assets, citing "sovereignty of the deep" ([[Dossier 19-3999.4896.2289]]: "Cable 3: Hammerseal Internal Memorandum and Bardrum's Sovereignty Directive").

### Cable 4: Khanate of Tsayandakh (8 months pre-fall)
**From:** Khan-Adessa, the Sky-Stained ([[Dossier 19-3189.0001]])  
**To:** King Bardrum, via wyvern courier  
**Content:** *"My wind-weavers have felt trembling in the deep earth beneath the Plateau. The horses are uneasy. Something builds beneath your kingdom, Bardrum. We who live above the stone can feel what you who live within it cannot. Heed the sky's counsel."*

**Bardrum's Response:** *"I thank the Khan for her concern. The trembling she feels is our miners at work. Ulbgar thrives."*

### Cables 5-7: Final Warnings (4-2 months pre-fall)
Three additional cables, two from the Ascendant Watch and one from the Preanese Theocracy's intelligence directorate, were transmitted in the final four months. None received formal responses. Hammerseal records (recovered from surface gatehouse archives post-fall) indicate that Cable 6 was **not delivered to the King**, the Hammerseal duty officer filed it as "duplicate advisory, no action required" ([[Dossier 19-3999.4896.3401]]: "Cables 5-7, Delivery Failure Analysis and Hammerseal Negligence").

**III. ANALYSIS; WHY THE WARNINGS FAILED**

### A. Cultural Arrogance
The dwarven doctrine of **"Stone Supremacy"**, the belief that dwarven-worked stone is fundamentally impenetrable to any force, is not merely a military assessment but a civilizational identity. Bardrum's refusal to consider subterranean threats was not a tactical error but a theological one; to acknowledge vulnerability would be to question the very foundation of dwarven cultural identity.

### B. Intelligence Institutional Failure
The Hammerseal; Ulbgar's intelligence apparatus; was structured to detect surface threats, deep-earth creature incursions, and internal dissent. It had no doctrine or capability for detecting **planar intrusions** from below. Its analysts lacked the arcane expertise to interpret the Ascendant Watch's signature data ([[Dossier 19-3999.4896.4567]]: "Hammerseal Capability Assessment; Doctrinal Gaps and Arcane Literacy").

### C. Diplomatic Mistrust
Bardrum viewed external intelligence offerings as potential **manipulation**; a reasonable suspicion given the history of surface powers attempting to claim Ulbgari mineral wealth. This legitimate caution was exploited, effectively, by the Velvet Iron Army's strategy of attacking from a direction no one expected.

### D. The Troll Factor
An underexamined element: the **troll vassal population** occupying the upper strata reported vibrations and cold drafts from below to their Thanes as early as eighteen months before the fall. The Thanes transmitted these reports to the Anvil Council, where they were dismissed as typical geological activity. Troll testimony was historically discounted by dwarven administrators as unreliable, a systemic prejudice that contributed to the intelligence failure ([[Dossier 19-3999.4896.5693]]: "Troll Early Warning, Dismissed Reports and Systemic Bias Analysis").

---

**IV. POST-FALL RELEVANCE**  
These cables are archived not merely as historical record but as a **case study in intelligence failure** applicable to current operations. Specifically:

- The Preanese Theocracy is currently following a parallel pattern: ignoring external warnings about internal subversion with the same institutional confidence that Ulbgar displayed about its physical defenses.
- Silesbia's bunker-city doctrine mirrors Ulbgar's "Stone Supremacy" in its conviction that fortification equals safety.
- The Ascendant Watch must consider whether its warning mechanisms themselves are structurally inadequate, warning the right people does not help if the recipients are culturally unable to hear ([[Dossier 19-3999.4896.6824]]: "Warning Delivery Reform, Proposed Changes to Ascendant Watch Advisory Protocols").

---

**V. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **Lesson:** No fortress is impregnable against an enemy that attacks from an unexpected vector. Every defensive doctrine contains assumptions that can be exploited.
2. **Application:** Current territorial assessments must include stress-testing of assumptions: particularly Stuik's land-war focus and the Theocracy's institutional trust.
