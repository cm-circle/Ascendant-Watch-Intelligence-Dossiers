**REF. 19-4002.2736**  
**Origin:** Ascendant Watch: Civilian Impact Assessment  
**Subject:** The Unblinded: Violence Against Civilians: Testimony and Analysis

---

**I. PURPOSE**  
While the Unblinded's stated mission is anti-infernal resistance, their methods have produced a pattern of **civilian harm**; interrogation abuse, destruction of property, intimidation, and at least two confirmed wrongful killings. This dossier documents these incidents to assess whether the Unblinded's net impact on civilian populations is beneficial or harmful.

---

**II. DOCUMENTED INCIDENTS**

### Incident 1, The Brethwick Interrogations (Cell 1, Anvil)
**Location:** Brethwick, a farming village 20km south of Ossward  
**Date:** 5 months ago  
**Circumstances:** Anvil members identified a merchant, **Petr Vordan**, as a suspected jade amulet distributor. Vordan was seized from his shop, taken to a barn outside the village, and interrogated through **physical coercion**; specifically, beating, sleep deprivation, and immersion in cold water.

Vordan confessed to distributing amulets. The confession was accurate, Vordan was indeed part of the distribution network. However, the interrogation continued beyond the initial confession. Anvil members demanded names of Vordan's contacts. Under sustained duress, Vordan provided 7 names, **4 of which were fabricated** (naming innocent neighbors to end the pain).

The Anvil detained all 7 named individuals. Two of the innocent detainees were beaten during secondary interrogations before the fabricated identifications were recognized. One innocent detainee, an elderly woman named Hilde Karst, suffered a broken arm during her interrogation. She has not regained full use of the arm.

([[Dossier 19-4002.2736.1147]]: "Brethwick Interrogations: Full Incident Report and Victim Statements")

### Incident 2, Wrongful Killing of Torek Arend (Cell 2, Blind Justice)
**Location:** Stuik frontier town of Ashenmoor  
**Date:** 4 months ago  
**Circumstances:** Cell 2 identified **Torek Arend**, a retired soldier, as a suspected infernal collaborator based on his recent acquisition of unexplained wealth. Arend was confronted at his home. When he attempted to flee (reasonably fearing for his life), a Cell 2 Justicar struck him with a mace, killing him.

Post-mortem investigation by Stuik municipal authorities revealed that Arend's wealth came from a **gambling win** at a Stuik card house, verified by multiple witnesses. Arend was not an infernal collaborator.

The killing has not been acknowledged by Cell 2. No member has been identified or held responsible.

([[Dossier 19-4002.2736.2301]]: "Wrongful Killing: Torek Arend, Full Investigation")

### Incident 3, Intimidation Campaign (Cell 3, The Scourge)
**Location:** Preas, eastern parish of Tervane  
**Date:** Ongoing over 3 months  
**Circumstances:** Cell 3 has conducted a sustained intimidation campaign against individuals they suspect of being infernal sympathizers within the Tervane parish. Methods include:
- Anonymous threatening letters left at homes and businesses
- Public denunciation: shouting accusations in marketplaces against named individuals
- Property destruction: arson of a warehouse owned by a merchant accused (without evidence) of trading with Ziragz

Two of the accused individuals have fled the parish. One, a **schoolteacher named Delina Voss**, was subsequently confirmed to have no infernal connections whatsoever. She is now homeless in a Stuik refugee camp, having lost her home, employment, and community standing.

([[Dossier 19-4002.2736.3478]]: "Tervane Intimidation Campaign: Activity Log and Victim Impact")

### Incident 4, Collateral Damage at Amulet Destruction (Cell 4, Children of the Maimed God)
**Location:** Suirx territorial refugee camp  
**Date:** 2 months ago  
**Circumstances:** Cell 4 raided a tent in the refugee camp suspected of containing jade amulets. During the raid, a cooking fire was overturned, setting fire to 3 adjacent tents. **14 refugees** were displaced; 2 suffered burns. The tent did contain jade amulets, the operation achieved its objective, but the fire damage is considered disproportionate to the target's value.

Cell 4 provided no compensation or assistance to the burned refugees.

([[Dossier 19-4002.2736.4612]]: "Refugee Camp Fire: Incident Report and Damage Assessment")

**IV. COMMUNITY REACTION**  
In communities where the Unblinded operate, civilian reaction is divided:

- **Supportive (~30%):** Individuals who are grateful for any anti-infernal action, regardless of methods. This group views the Unblinded as "doing what the authorities won't."
- **Fearful (~45%):** The majority, who are afraid both of the infernal threat *and* of the Unblinded themselves. These individuals avoid drawing attention, comply with Unblinded demands, and do not report incidents to authorities.
- **Hostile (~25%):** Individuals who view the Unblinded as a threat to community stability. This group is concentrated among merchants, local officials, and individuals who have been wrongfully targeted. Some actively cooperate with authorities to suppress Unblinded activity.

The net effect is a **fractured community** in which the Unblinded simultaneously fight the enemy and undermine the social trust necessary for organized resistance.

---

**V. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **The 40% Problem:** A 40% wrongful targeting rate is unacceptable. It turns potential allies into enemies, creates propaganda material for Thraxxia's recruitment operations, and undermines community trust in anti-infernal movements.
2. **Structural Cause:** The Unblinded lack intelligence training, evidence verification standards, and accountability mechanisms. These are structural deficiencies, not moral failures: the Unblinded believe they are acting justly and are psychologically unprepared to accept that their methods harm innocents.
3. **No Easy Solution:** Direct confrontation with the Unblinded over civilian harm would drive them underground without reducing the problem. The institutional hatred that fuels their movement ([[Dossier 19-4002.2927]]) makes them resistant to external correction.
4. **Recommendation:** **[REDACTED]**, Consider providing covert intelligence support to cells with the highest wrongful-targeting rates (Cells 2 and 3) through mortal intermediaries. If the Unblinded can be quietly guided toward accurate targets, their error rate drops and their net impact becomes positive, without requiring acknowledgment of the Ascendant Watch's involvement. This is the "guided vigilante" concept and it carries significant ethical risks. **[REDACTED]**
