**CLASSIFIED: FOR CELESTIAL EYES ONLY**  
**REF. 19-4002.4569**  
**Origin:** Ascendant Watch: Anomalous Entity Analysis  
**Subject:** DJLMFAO: Origin and Allegiance Analysis: Classification of an Ancient Undead Maritime Threat

---

**I. THE QUESTION**

The DJLMFAO entity ([[Dossier 19-4002.4569]]) engaged a diplomatic vessel on the western sea lanes using a crew of living, veiled Clerics of Tyr, frost-based necromantic magic, and life-drain capabilities of extraordinary potency. Upon destruction, the entity detonated in a cloud of ash, leaving a frost-coated crater in which all organic material had been fully desiccated. A coded journal recovered from the entity's vessel has been confirmed as written in Ancient Abyssal; a language extinct for millennia and readable only by entities of comparable age.

---

**II. HYPOTHESIS 1; INFERNAL MERCENARY**

**Theory:** The DJLMFAO entity was an independent ancient undead, predating the current campaign by centuries or more, who was contracted by Hell's invading armies to serve as a maritime strike asset. The entity's considerable age and power made it a valuable hired weapon, and its Ancient Abyssal writings are consistent with an origin in a pre-campaign era.

**Evidence For:**

- Monica (diplomatic contacts, extensive intelligence network) assessed that DJLMFAO was "likely hired by Hell's armies to sow chaos," and that his powers were unlikely to be of infernal origin; consistent with the profile of an independent contractor rather than an infernal native
- More vessels have been lost in the western sea lanes than in any prior season since the invasion began; the entity may have been responsible for multiple engagements before this one
- The entity's crew; veiled Clerics of Tyr, were alive, suggesting the entity had been operating long enough in the material plane to recruit or dominate mortal followers, which requires sustained infernal protection or sponsorship

**Evidence Against:**

- The entity's powers showed no infernal signature; the crater left at destruction was frost-coated and necrotic, not fiendish or sulfurous
- Infernal assets typically fly identifiable flags or carry infernal markings; the entity's ship bore no colors
- An ancient undead of this magnitude would represent extraordinary leverage for Hell's armies; deploying it as an assassin against a single diplomatic vessel seems disproportionate unless the target was of far greater strategic importance than currently assessed

([[Dossier 19-4002.4569.1147]]: "Hypothesis 1: Infernal Mercenary: Supporting Analysis and Strategic Fit")

---

**III. HYPOTHESIS 2; INDEPENDENT ANCIENT UNDEAD**

**Theory:** The DJLMFAO entity is entirely independent of Hell's invading forces. It is an ancient undead mage, likely awakened or resurged by the chaos of the current invasion, operating according to its own centuries-old agenda. The Clerics of Tyr under its command are dominated thralls or corrupted converts, not infernal assets.

**Evidence For:**

- Ancient Abyssal has been extinct as a living language for millennia; the entity's use of it in a personal journal ([[Dossier 19-4002.4568.9846]]) places its origin at a minimum of several thousand years prior to the current campaign. No infernal contractor would require translation assistance for their own operative's records
- The entity demonstrated the ability to raise the dead as thralls within seconds of a kill (raising the first mate, Gylp, immediately upon death), indicating necromantic power far exceeding standard infernal mercenary profiles
- Monica assessed that the invasion may represent "something much larger at work" and that the discovery of the journal was a "bad omen" distinct from the infernal threat, suggesting the entity's connection to something older and more fundamental than the current campaign
- The frost-coated, life-desiccated crater at point of destruction is consistent with an ancient entropic magic system unrelated to known infernal methodologies

**Evidence Against:**

- An entity of this age operating without infernal support during an active campaign would likely have been identified, engaged, or destroyed by Hell's armies unless some form of non-aggression or cooperation existed between them
- The coincidence of the entity's activation and the current infernal campaign may not be coincidental; Hell's armies' disruption of the material plane may have been necessary to awaken or release such an entity

([[Dossier 19-4002.4569.2301]]: "Hypothesis 2: Independent Ancient Undead: Age Indicators and Power Classification")

---

**IV. HYPOTHESIS 3; FALLEN SERVANT OF TYR (LEADING ASSESSMENT)**

**Theory:** The DJLMFAO entity was once a high-ranking mortal servant of Tyr, likely a senior cleric or inquisitor, who committed a catastrophic sin against Tyrran doctrine. Rather than divine penance (see [[Dossier 19-4002.4568]]), this entity was _abandoned_ by Tyr and fell into undeath through its own corruption, taking a faction of the Tyrran clergy with it. The veiled Clerics of Tyr aboard the vessel are not random converts but the remnant of a schismatic sect bound to the entity by shared apostasy.

**Evidence For:**

- The Clerics of Tyr present during the engagement were veiled — an inversion of Tyr's core doctrine of "unblinded" justice, which explicitly forbids the covering of the eyes in sacred practice. This is not a minor deviation; it is a direct theological negation
- The entity's Ancient Abyssal journal, held in the captain's quarters in a position of authority, suggests the entity was the leader and intellectual architect of this sect, not merely its muscle
- The entity demonstrated no hesitation in destroying a ship flying no colors and in killing its first mate immediately upon engagement — a behavioral profile consistent with an entity operating entirely outside moral or divine law
- Monica's assessment that "only ancient demons and devils remember" Ancient Abyssal implies the entity had contact with, or was studying, demonic and devilish knowledge at a point of corruption far predating the current invasion
- The entity's simultaneous use of frost-based magic (an uncommon necromantic specialization associated with pre-Tyrran death cults) alongside Tyrran clerical followers suggests a syncretic theology of Tyr-worship fused with ancient entropic doctrine

**Evidence Against:**

- No specific apostasy event has been identified in surviving Tyrran ecclesiastical records
- The concept of a Tyrran schism of this magnitude occurring and going unrecorded stretches credibility; however, if it predates current archival infrastructure by thousands of years, absence of record is expected rather than exceptional
- The entity's destruction was complete and immediate upon a single high-magnitude psychic strike; a truly ancient and consolidated undead might be expected to show greater resilience

([[Dossier 19-4002.4569.3478]]: "Hypothesis 3: Fallen Servant of Tyr: Theological Evidence and Schismatic Indicators")

---

**V. IMPLICATIONS OF THE FALLEN SERVANT HYPOTHESIS**

If DJLMFAO was a corrupted Tyrran apostate commanding a schismatic Tyrran sect, then:

1. **The veiled Clerics of Tyr are a larger network.** The eight clerics aboard the vessel represent a small deployment. If this sect has persisted for millennia, there are likely more — active somewhere in the material plane and possibly embedded within legitimate Tyrran institutions under the cover of the current invasion's chaos.
2. **The coded journal is the primary intelligence asset.** The journal, is written in a dead language specifically to prevent outside reading. Its contents likely document the sect's doctrine, membership, goals, and potentially its connection to Hell's armies or older infernal structures. Decipherment is the highest current intelligence priority.
3. **Monica's network is insufficient for translation.** She acknowledged that her contacts are unlikely to have results given the language's extinction. Translation requires access to entities old enough to remember Ancient Abyssal — which means ancient demons, ancient devils, or extremely obscure archival resources. This is not a trivial undertaking.

([[Dossier 19-4002.4569.4612]]: "Fallen Servant Implications: Network Scale and Journal Priority")

---

**VI. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT**

1. **The threat is not contained.** The entity's destruction does not neutralize the sect. The veiled Clerics of Tyr represent an active and potentially widespread network, and without journal translation, the sect's goals and scale remain unknown.
2. **The journal is time-sensitive.** If the sect is aware of the entity's destruction, it may move to neutralize parties known to carry the journal. The party's possession of this document is a significant liability and an equally significant asset.
3. **Recommendation:** **[REDACTED]** Prioritize translation of the recovered journal through unconventional channels, ancient extraplanar contacts, pre-invasion archival deposits, or direct interrogation of any surviving Clerics of Tyr captured during future engagements. Simultaneously, investigate Monica's full passenger and crew manifest for potential sect infiltration. **[REDACTED]**
